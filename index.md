## Concepts

- [[wiki/concepts/llm-wiki|LLM Wiki]] - A persistent, LLM-maintained wiki that compiles and updates knowledge instead of re-deriving it on every query.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/artificial-intelligence|Artificial Intelligence]] - The umbrella field that contains the narrower model families and training paradigms in the AI branch.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-and-biotechnology-convergence|AI and Biotechnology Convergence]] - The claim that AI becomes especially disruptive when fused with biological and behavioral insight that can steer human choice.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/machine-learning|Machine Learning]] - The data-driven AI layer where models learn patterns from examples rather than only following hand-written rules.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/deep-learning|Deep Learning]] - The neural-network-heavy subfield inside machine learning that underpins modern generative systems and LLMs.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/generative-ai|Generative AI]] - The output-producing model layer focused on creating new text, images, audio, video, and other artifacts.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/foundation-models|Foundation Models]] - Broadly pre-trained reusable models that serve as adaptable bases for many downstream tasks, with LLMs as one major branch.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/open-weight-models|Open-Weight Models]] - Released model weights that can be run or adapted locally, with major implications for privacy, customization, and licensing freedom.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/large-language-models|Large Language Models]] - Large-scale language systems trained for broad text understanding and generation, then often adapted for narrower tasks.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/tokenization|Tokenization]] - The text-to-token layer that governs how LLMs encode inputs, use context, and incur cost.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/supervised-and-unsupervised-learning|Supervised and Unsupervised Learning]] - The core beginner distinction between labeled-feedback learning and pattern-finding in unlabeled data.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/fine-tuning|Fine-Tuning]] - The adaptation step that turns broadly trained models into more domain-specific systems.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/prompt-engineering|Prompt Engineering]] - The practice of structuring instructions, context, examples, constraints, and desired outputs so AI systems produce more useful results.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/context-engineering|Context Engineering]] - The higher-level practice of briefing the model with enough goals, background, examples, constraints, and evaluation context that better output becomes easier.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/human-machine-collaboration|Human-Machine Collaboration]] - Designing workflows and rollout patterns where human judgment and machine capability compound rather than compete.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-era-thoughtfulness|AI-Era Thoughtfulness]] - Independent thinking, empathy, world simulation, and value creation as the human capacity AI collaboration should protect rather than replace.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-powered-creativity|AI-Powered Creativity]] - Using AI as a collaborative partner for creative exploration, workflow discovery, volume, variation, critique, and selection without surrendering human taste or judgment.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-critical-thinking-partner|AI Critical Thinking Partner]] - An AI interaction pattern that uses critique, assumption checks, alternatives, and bias surfacing to preserve human judgment.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-roleplay-simulation|AI Roleplay Simulation]] - A workflow for rehearsing difficult conversations through profiling, simulated interaction, and transcript-based feedback.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/riceco-prompt-framework|RICECO Prompt Framework]] - A six-part prompt-design framework built around Role, Instruction, Context, Examples, Constraints, and Output Format.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/few-shot-prompting|Few-Shot Prompting]] - Using one or more examples in a prompt to anchor tone, structure, style, or reasoning more precisely.
- [[wiki/concepts/ai/reverse-prompting|Reverse Prompting]] - Having the model ask clarifying questions before answering so missing context is surfaced before bad output is produced.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/advertising|Advertising]] - Humane, culturally aware brand communication that people may actually want to engage with.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/social-entrepreneurship|Social Entrepreneurship]] - Building economically sustainable systems that solve real human problems while treating impact and human delight as central rewards.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/career-capital|Career Capital]] - The accumulated rare value, proof, and trust that can be exchanged for more autonomy, impact, and better work conditions.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/mimetic-desire|Mimetic Desire]] - Wanting goals mainly because status systems, media, or other people taught you to want them rather than because they were chosen deliberately.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/specific-knowledge.md|Specific Knowledge]] - A hard-to-copy stack of natural strengths, background, obsessions, and self-built capability that creates unusual economic value, often discovered through broader exploration before concentrated commitment.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/ownership-ladder|Ownership Ladder]] - A progression from selling hours to owning increasingly leveraged outcomes, where accountability, specific knowledge, and control over the stack rise together.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/craftsman-mindset|Craftsman Mindset]] - A career posture centered on contribution and skill compounding rather than constant emotional evaluation of whether work feels special enough.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/blue-ocean-strategy|Blue Ocean Strategy]] - The attempt to create uncontested market space by redesigning the category instead of competing harder inside a crowded one.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/value-innovation|Value Innovation]] - The rule of raising buyer value while lowering cost by removing over-served complexity and redesigning the offer.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/errc-framework|ERRC Framework]] - A four-part redesign grid for deciding what to eliminate, reduce, raise, and create when reshaping a category.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/okrs|OKRs]] - A goal-and-review system that ties one objective to a small set of measurable key results and recurring status checks.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/stretch-goals|Stretch Goals]] - Targets set high enough to force growth without crossing into empty fantasy or chronic demoralization.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/mere-exposure-effect|Mere-Exposure Effect]] - The tendency for repetition and familiarity to inflate liking, trust, or perceived truth without improving evidence.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/status-quo-bias|Status Quo Bias]] - The tendency to preserve defaults because losses loom larger and owned options feel more valuable than they are.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/wysiati|WYSIATI]] - Kahneman's model of overconfident judgment from incomplete evidence when the visible story feels like the whole story.
- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/acting-craft|Acting Craft]] - Performance truth built from reaction, stakes, textual precision, variation, and arc awareness rather than affectation.
- [[wiki/concepts/communication/accusation-audit|Accusation Audit]] - A preemptive cluster of negative labels used to surface and defuse the bad story before a difficult ask or hard conversation.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/animal-cognition|Animal Cognition]] - The continuity of cognition, emotion, and culture across nonhuman animals.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/arrow-of-time-and-entropy|Arrow of Time and Entropy]] - The thermodynamic asymmetry that makes irreversibility, memory, and temporary complexity intelligible.
- [[wiki/concepts/communication/assertiveness|Assertiveness]] - The ability to state what is true, wanted, or not acceptable without collapsing into passivity, over-explanation, or aggression.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/bullying-as-defense|Bullying as Defense]] - Aggression interpreted as fear-management, shame defense, or injured status repair rather than real inner strength.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/emergence-and-levels-of-explanation|Emergence and Levels of Explanation]] - The claim that higher-level realities can be real and explanatory without being fundamental physics terms.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/expert-intuition|Expert Intuition]] - Fast judgment that becomes trustworthy only when a domain offers stable patterns and real feedback.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/hollywood-romance-myths|Hollywood Romance Myths]] - Recurring movie-shaped relationship scripts that glamorize fantasy, volatility, and boundary violations as love.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/mask-behavior|Mask Behavior]] - Visible personality used as protective performance rather than transparent self-revelation.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/core-shame|Core Shame]] - The organizing fear of being seen in an identity-level way that feels socially or existentially intolerable.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/naturalistic-meaning|Naturalistic Meaning]] - The claim that human purpose and value can be real inside a nonteleological physical universe.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/quiet-eye|Quiet Eye]] - The final stable fixation before and during action that may improve skilled performance by coupling attention more tightly to execution.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/shared-fictions|Shared Fictions]] - Collectively believed stories such as money, nations, and corporations that coordinate humans at scale but can morally mislead when reified.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/inversion-patterns|Inversion Patterns]] - Recurring outward personas that overcompensate for feared inner traits or shame scripts.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/projection-as-self-signal|Projection as Self-Signal]] - The use of emotionally charged judgment as a clue to disowned material in oneself.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/shadow-integration|Shadow Integration]] - Recognizing and channeling disowned traits so they stop leaking through sabotage, projection, or rigid persona.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/two-systems-thinking|Two-Systems Thinking]] - A fast-versus-slow cognition model explaining why judgment can be both adaptive and predictably biased.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/emotional-gravity|Emotional Gravity]] - The pull of unfinished emotional stories that keeps people recreating familiar patterns until they are consciously rewritten.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/childhood-development-triangle|Childhood Development Triangle]] - A heuristic model that traces adult scripts back to early safety, belonging, and reward contracts.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/orion-principle|Orion Principle]] - A named heuristic linking ideal self, feared self, projection, and mask intensity.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/drama-vs-secure-connection|Drama vs Secure Connection]] - The distinction between volatile emotional spectacle and calmer attachment built on steadiness, respect, and safety.
- [[wiki/concepts/others/romantic-completion-myth|Romantic Completion Myth]] - The false belief that romance will solve emptiness, prove worth, or complete an otherwise incomplete self.
- [[wiki/concepts/creativity/art-making|Art Making]] - Building art through material openness, self-acceptance, viewer contact, and Godin's broader frame of generous human work that might not work.
- [[wiki/concepts/communication/authentic-communication|Authentic Communication]] - Speaking truthfully and humanly enough that audiences feel respected rather than managed, including vulnerable stories that reveal real change.
- [[wiki/concepts/communication/attunement|Attunement]] - Perspective-taking as a practical influence skill for understanding interests, constraints, and likely reactions.
- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/arena-vs-story|Arena vs Story]] - The distinction between an interesting backdrop and an actual dramatic engine with goals, conflict, stakes, and change.
- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/auditioning|Auditioning]] - Preparing, adapting, and presenting work under compressed professional conditions without collapsing into panic or ego display.
- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/visual-storytelling-first|Visual Storytelling First]] - The rule that screenplays should push story burden into action, image, and staging before relying on explanatory dialogue.
- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/story-lenses|Story Lenses]] - Distinctive angles or prisms that make familiar topics feel newly legible rather than interchangeable.
- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/voiceover-as-interiority|Voiceover as Interiority]] - Voiceover used as legitimate subjective access when it reveals psychology rather than patching weak dramatization.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/brand-stewardship|Brand Stewardship]] - Protecting and evolving a brand's long-term identity without hollowing it out.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/mass-intimacy|Mass Intimacy]] - Creating communication at mass scale that still feels personal and respectful.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/reverse-benchmarking|Reverse Benchmarking]] - Finding the dimension competitors neglect and turning that neglected space into strategic distinctiveness.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/smallest-viable-audience|Smallest Viable Audience]] - Choosing the smallest group that cares deeply enough to adopt, spread, and organize around the work.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/psychographic-targeting|Psychographic Targeting]] - Defining audiences by beliefs, aspirations, identity, and status logic rather than by demographics alone.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/connection-economy|Connection Economy]] - The networked market logic where coordination, trust, permission, and sharing matter more than mass interruption and average sameness.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/process-over-outcomes|Process Over Outcomes]] - Organizing meaningful work around repeatable practice and contribution rather than constant dependence on applause or short-term results.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/strategic-quitting|Strategic Quitting]] - Distinguishing real dips from bad games so quitting becomes a decision-quality skill instead of automatic failure.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/megatrendy|Мегатренды]] - Большие политические, экономические и социокультурные сдвиги, которые создают и разрушают рынки.
- [[wiki/concepts/communication/mirroring|Mirroring]] - Repeating a small fragment of the other person's words so they elaborate instead of shutting down or defending.
- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/monologue-preparation|Monologue Preparation]] - Selecting, analyzing, grounding, and varying monologue material until it becomes dramatically playable.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/brand-strategy|Brand Strategy]] - The insight layer that gives creative work a coherent reason to exist and helps brands express durable values clearly.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/experience-design|Experience Design]] - The deliberate shaping of the full customer journey so the promise is felt through touchpoints, rituals, pricing feel, and memory.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/positioning-as-a-service|Positioning as a Service]] - Differentiation framed as a service that helps the buyer choose clearly instead of rewarding the loudest seller.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/golden-circle|Golden Circle]] - Simon Sinek's why-how-what model for purpose-led communication, belief alignment, and inside-out messaging.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/perception-driven-value|Perception-Driven Value]] - The idea that framing, context, symbolism, and expectation can materially change what the market experiences as valuable.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/psychological-leverage|Psychological Leverage]] - The use of framing, agency, uncertainty reduction, and symbolic design to improve behavior or satisfaction without brute-force mechanical change.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/psychological-arbitrage|Psychological Arbitrage]] - The search for underpriced value in reference points, context shifts, and weakly measured human motives such as certainty, status, and autonomy.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/social-heuristics|Social Heuristics]] - Simple named rules, scripts, and binary commitments that spread behavior through visibility, imitation, and shared expectations.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/transaction-utility|Transaction Utility]] - The felt value of the purchase itself, shaped by fairness, context, seller identity, occasion, and what buying seems to mean.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/fat-tailed-marketing|Fat-Tailed Marketing]] - The view that a small number of unusual marketing and innovation ideas create most long-term value, which makes exploration economically necessary.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/paradigm-shifts|Paradigm Shifts]] - High-leverage frame changes that alter how choices are understood, often creating large behavioral effects without equivalent mechanical redesign.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/experience-goods|Experience Goods]] - Products, services, and choices whose value is revealed mainly through use rather than through first-glance comparison.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/costly-signaling|Costly Signaling]] - The use of expensive, effortful, scarce, or hard-to-fake cues to make trust, seriousness, or status more believable.
- [[wiki/concepts/communication/batna|BATNA]] - A negotiation anchor that defines the best credible walk-away alternative and sets a real reservation floor before tactics begin.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/hidden-functions|Hidden Functions]] - The tacit roles a product, service, ritual, or human touchpoint plays beyond its obvious mechanical task.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/success-metrics|Success Metrics]] - The scoreboards and measures that determine whether institutions reward durable health or visible but misleading performance.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/business-strategy|Business Strategy]] - A small set of strategic priorities that govern where an organization places time, capital, attention, and which directions it actively stops supporting.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/game-theory|Game Theory]] - Decisions under strategic interdependence, where outcomes depend partly on what other actors choose and stable incentives can still produce bad collective results.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/social-mobility|Social Mobility]] - The degree to which people can move across class positions rather than being trapped near the status, trust conditions, and opportunity set of their birth.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/shapley-value|Shapley Value]] - A cooperative-game-theory fairness rule that allocates gains or costs by average marginal contribution across possible coalition orderings.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/backward-induction|Backward Induction]] - Solving finite sequential decisions by reasoning from the last move backward, especially useful for timing problems.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/tit-for-tat|Tit for Tat]] - A repeated-games strategy of cooperating first, retaliating proportionally, forgiving quickly, and adapting toward greater generosity when noise would otherwise trap both sides in retaliation.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/zero-sum-games|Zero-Sum Games]] - Strategic situations where one side's gain is exactly another's loss, now also linked to everyday status games that people mistakenly treat as the only game worth playing.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/operational-productivity|Operational Productivity]] - Technology leverage applied to the core value-creating work of the business rather than only to administrative coordination, with direct attention to products and production systems.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/first-principles-problem-solving|First-Principles Problem-Solving]] - Challenging requirements, distinguishing understanding from recipe stacks, and deleting or redesigning systems before optimizing, accelerating, or automating them.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/platonic-ideal-product|Platonic Ideal Product]] - Defining the best theoretically possible product first, then building the tools and manufacturing system needed to approach it.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/founder-mode|Founder Mode]] - Scaling philosophy where the founder stays close to standards, wedge-defining details, and key tradeoffs while turning heroics into durable systems.

- [[wiki/concepts/business/business-systems|Business Systems]] - Treating business operations as measurable machines whose design controls feedback speed, clarity, and throughput rather than as documents alone.
