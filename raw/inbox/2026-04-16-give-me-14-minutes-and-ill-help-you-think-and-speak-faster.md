💡 Core Concepts

1. Improve the “hardware” of speech  
    The first lever is your physical ability to articulate sounds quickly and clearly. Tongue twisters strengthen lips and tongue, train efficient mouth movements, and force you to maintain clarity at higher speeds because they are deliberately constructed to trip you up if articulation is weak. This prevents the common problem where speaking faster turns into mumbling, similar to unintelligible “mumble rap,” rather than crisp, fast speech.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
    
2. Measure and train rate of speech  
    A 300-word story becomes a practical diagnostic tool to quantify your current words-per-minute (WPM). You read for one minute, mark where you stopped, count the words, and that gives you a baseline rate. From there you deliberately increase your target by about 10 extra words each repetition (e.g., 100 → 110 → 120 WPM), using reflection when you hit a plateau to identify whether the bottleneck is mouth mechanics (back to tongue twisters) or something else.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
    
3. Breath control and vocal efficiency  
    Many people speak slowly because they unconsciously expel too much air per word, forcing frequent breaths and causing a breathy, weak sound. Efficient breath control means letting air “ease out” and giving each word just enough breath and energy so you can sustain long phrases on a single inhalation. Training includes prolonged “S” sounds on one breath and counting sequences (1, 2, 3, …) while keeping consistent energy for each number and avoiding the vocal fry that appears when you run out of air but still push your voice.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
    
4. Understanding vs memorizing to think faster  
    Slow responses in professional settings are often due to shallow, memorized knowledge in a new domain rather than true understanding. When you deeply understand a topic—through reading multiple books, knowing rules, processes, and practical details in your niche—you no longer need to search for “the memorized answer” and can respond more quickly and flexibly. Depth of understanding becomes the foundation that enables spontaneous, confident explanations.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
    
5. The 3–2–1 framework for structured answers  
    To avoid mental overload when asked broad or “loaded” questions (e.g., “How do I improve my communication skills?”), you use a simple structure: three steps, two types, or one thing. In practice, you pick one of these lenses instantly (often “the one thing” for simplicity), which constrains your thinking and prevents your brain from trying to cover everything at once. For example, instead of listing dozens of communication techniques, you might choose “the one thing is self-awareness,” and give a clear, actionable instruction like “record yourself and watch for non-functional behaviors.”[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
    
6. Anxiety, stress, and cognitive speed  
    High anxiety and stress impair clear thinking and slow down your ability to formulate responses. Anticipating high-pressure situations, you prepare in advance and use breathing techniques—especially diaphragmatic (belly) breathing and box breathing—to calm your nervous system. Box breathing (exhale fully, then inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4, repeated 3–5 times) reduces anxiety, supports clearer cognition, and indirectly helps you think and speak faster.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)

🔨 Actionable Tips

- Daily tongue-twister drill
    
    - Find ~50 tongue twisters and pick one at a time.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
    - Read each tongue twister five times in a row, increasing your speed each time while keeping every word clear.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
- WPM training with a 300-word story
    
    - Choose a ~300-word passage.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
    - Read aloud for exactly one minute, mark your stopping point, and count how many words you covered to get your baseline WPM.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
    - Repeat with the goal of adding roughly 10 extra words each attempt, adjusting pace while preserving clarity; if you stall, return to articulation work.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
- Breath control exercises
    
    - Take a deep breath and sustain an “S” sound until you fully run out of air, building awareness of efficient, steady airflow.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
    - Practice counting aloud on a single breath (1, 2, 3, …) while giving each number equal energy and avoiding a strained, croaky finish.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
- Build domain depth
    
    - Identify your main area (e.g., real estate, development, finance) and pick several substantive books or resources on that specific niche.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
    - Study to understand mechanisms, rules, and practical workflows (e.g., regulations, tax implications, how to work with stakeholders), not just memorize talking points.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
- Use the 3–2–1 framework in conversations
    
    - When asked a broad question, mentally choose one: “3 steps,” “2 types,” or “1 thing” about the topic.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
    - Deliver a concise answer within that structure, and then let the other person ask follow-up questions instead of overwhelming them with everything you know.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
- Pre-event anxiety protocol
    
    - Before a stressful meeting, practice diaphragmatic breathing (belly breaths instead of shallow chest breaths).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
        
    - Perform 3–5 cycles of box breathing (empty lungs, inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4) to lower anxiety and improve mental clarity.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)

- The video emphasizes that many communication problems are “invisible” habits (over-breathiness, vocal fry, uneven energy) that become obvious once you know what to look for, making self-recording a powerful diagnostic.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
    
- Experts often sabotage themselves by trying to deliver everything they know at once; constraining responses via the 3–2–1 framework protects the listener from overload and the speaker from cognitive meltdown.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
    
- Breathing is presented as a serious, high-leverage skill (with entire multi-day workshops devoted to it), countering the common perception that it is trivial or “lame.”[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DN5OnGxSWuY&list=WL&index=13)
