## 📝 Executive Summary

The episode explores how Jesse Cole built the Savannah Bananas and Banana Ball by turning baseball into a **fans-first** live entertainment experience that prioritizes joy, inclusion, and emotional connection over tradition and short-term profit. Through deliberate experience design, relentless experimentation, and inspiration from Walt Disney, PT Barnum, and the Grateful Dead, Cole created a new genre that focuses on making every fan, player, and staff member feel seen, valued, and part of something bigger than themselves.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

---

## 🎯 Key Takeaways

- Fans-first philosophy: Every decision is anchored in creating remarkable, inclusive experiences for fans, especially those in the “upper deck,” rather than maximizing revenue or appeasing investors.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- Experience as core product: The Bananas treat baseball as a canvas for showmanship (trick plays, music, choreography, rituals), engineered from first impression to final moment, inspired heavily by Disney’s experiential design.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- Long-term over short-term: They intentionally reject typical sports monetization (dynamic ticket pricing, heavy fees, sponsorship clutter, exploitative concessions) to build lifelong, generational fandom.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- Systematic innovation: The organization runs like a creative lab, doing 10–20 new things each night, rigorously debriefing, and constantly “plussing” the show based on a structured review process.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- Human meaning and belonging: The deeper purpose is helping people feel seen, included, and worthy—especially those who were rejected, sidelined, or overlooked, from players cut from the pros to fans in emotional crisis.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

## 💡 Core Concepts

## 1. Fans First as an Operating Standard

“Fans first” is not a slogan but a decision filter for everything: programming, pricing, operations, and hiring. Cole insists that “every night is someone’s first show,” so every experience must feel special regardless of seat location or ticket price.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

They explicitly design for people who are not traditional baseball fans: families seeking shared joy, people who find baseball boring, and those who want something they’ve never seen before. The clear choice is to be “not for baseball traditionalists” but for people who want fun, surprise, and emotional connection.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

## 2. Baseball as Canvas, Not the Product

Cole separates “baseball” from “entertainment”: baseball is the structure, but the product is the immersive show that happens around and through the game. Players perform choreographed dances, trick plays, elaborate celebrations, and constant crowd interactions, yet they still compete seriously; outcomes are not scripted, unlike the Harlem Globetrotters.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

This duality—real competition plus theatricality—creates continuous tension and engagement: fans must stay to the end because both the game result and the show content are genuinely unpredictable.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

## 3. Experience Design Inspired by Disney, PT Barnum, and SNL

Cole borrows from:

- PT Barnum: Multiplexing unique entertainment forms into a single show and letting the product market itself through word of mouth; the Bananas spend _zero_ on traditional marketing, focusing resources on making the experience so remarkable that fans and social media do the promotion.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- Walt Disney: Total control of the guest journey—from approach (the “berm” blocking the outside world) to music, sightlines, and microscopic details like trash can spacing. Cole similarly maps the emotional arc of a Bananas event: early gate opening, pregame show outside the stadium, marching through crowds, on-field rituals, and a closing communal song (“Stand by Me”).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- Saturday Night Live: A weekly cadence of new, topical bits; the Bananas run 15–20 brand-new promotions, celebrations, or show elements each night to keep the experience fresh.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    

This yields a “living, breathing” show that is never complete—echoing Disney’s notion of always “plussing” the experience.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

## 4. Obsessive Operational Feedback Loops

After every game, the team conducts structured LCP (Learn–Change–Plus) reviews, producing multi-page reports covering each promotion, “over the top” moment, celebration, and detail. This guarantees that experiments are not random; they are observed, evaluated, and either iterated or discarded.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

Constant innovation is normalized: they expect to fail on many new ideas nightly, but the small number that work become “gold” and compound the overall experience.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

## 5. Democratized Value: Upper Deck, Pricing, and Access

Cole deliberately challenges the “front of plane vs. back of plane” inequality typical in sports and entertainment. The team’s mantra is “win the upper deck”: leaders personally spend time in nosebleed sections, deliver roses to kids, run sing-offs in cheap seats, and debrief on “upper deck stories” after games.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

Pricing reflects the same ethos:

- Home games in Savannah include free hot dogs, burgers, sandwiches, drinks, popcorn, and dessert with the ticket.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- Tickets are kept in the roughly 40–60 dollar range, with no junk fees; the organization built its own secondary market to maintain face-value resale and resist price gouging.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- They keep all games free to watch on YouTube and avoid conventional sponsorship clutter and TV rights deals.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    

Merchandise and fan affection, not extraction, drive the business economics.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

## 6. Rejected Players, New Identities, and Non-Financial Purpose

Many Bananas players were cut from professional systems at Single-A, Double-A, or Triple-A, or never advanced past college, leaving them with identity crises. Cole’s model gives them a new, more expansive identity: entertainer, community figure, and part of a brotherhood that competes hard but is united by a shared mission of joy.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

They have attracted a former MLB All-Star and World Series champion to play full-time, supporting the thesis that having fun improves performance. Internally, the captain frames the team as a family playing “for something bigger” than winning alone.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

## 7. Long-Term Horizon and Generational Fandom

Cole thinks in quarter centuries, not quarters. He is uninterested in going public or chasing venture capital because those stakeholders push for growth and liquidity, not experience quality.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

He aims to build “a billion fans” globally and sees Bananas fandom as potentially generational—like the Grateful Dead or Disney—where families pass the tradition down, fans travel city to city, and original “banana babies” grow up to attend youth camps and shows.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

## 🔨 Actionable Tips

1. **Define a Human-Centered Standard (“Fans First”)**
    
    - Articulate a simple, behaviorally specific standard (e.g., “fans first,” “customers first,” “patients first”) and use it as a decision filter for pricing, product changes, and operations.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
    - Regularly ask: “What would this feel like for someone in the ‘upper deck’—the least prioritized customer segment?”[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
2. **Engineer the Experience End-to-End**
    
    - Map the emotional journey: first contact, arrival, main event, and exit. Decide intentionally what guests see, hear, and feel at each stage, as Disney did with entrance berms, music, and sightlines.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
    - Introduce signature rituals that bookend the experience (e.g., a pre-event welcome and a closing collective moment) to anchor memory and meaning.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
3. **Institutionalize Small-Batch Experimentation and Debriefing**
    
    - Commit to a target number of new experiments per cycle (e.g., “10 new micro-experiments per week”) recognizing that most will fail.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
    - Run a structured post-event review (like LCP) capturing what to learn, change, and “plus,” and codify successful experiments into standard practice.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
4. **Compete on Generosity, Not Extraction**
    
    - Identify the “pain points” in your category (fees, hidden costs, price spikes, nickel-and-diming) and intentionally reverse them where possible.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
    - Consider building mechanisms (e.g., your own resale or service channels) to prevent third parties from violating your fairness standards.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
5. **Turn Rejection and Marginality into Core Talent Strategy**
    
    - Actively recruit from people and groups who have been rejected or sidelined but carry high motivation: cut candidates, overlooked staff, career changers.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
    - Give them visible roles, narrative recognition, and meaningful rituals (like making an intern’s initiative central to a story, or elevating a staff member to “coach”).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
6. **Use Stories as Cultural Operating Systems**
    
    - Collect and retell specific stories (e.g., the grieving family, Reggie’s birthday, Cole’s own childhood moment with Lee Smith) that embody your standard and make it emotionally non-negotiable.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
    - Use these stories to onboard new hires as “this is what we do here,” not just as inspirational anecdotes.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)

## 🔍 Additional Insights

- **Business model as quiet dissent:** Cole’s refusal to accept venture capital, IPO pressures, or exploitative pricing is framed as a critique of contemporary entrepreneurship’s obsession with growth and liquidity events over human value.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- **AI and mental health context:** Sinek connects the Bananas’ in-person communal joy to a broader concern that increasing AI integration and digital relationships, without guardrails, will correlate with higher loneliness and suicide rates—making analog, shared experiences more important.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- **Fear of irrelevance, not failure:** Cole’s primary fear is that the organization might one day stop _mattering_ emotionally to people, not that it will lose money. This drives his obsession with constant reinvention and depth of connection.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- **Identity loop: from fan to founder:** Cole’s own arc—from shy only child with cheap bleacher seats and a formative encounter with Lee Smith to “modern-day Walt Disney” in baseball—anchors the entire philosophy in lived experience of exclusion, aspiration, and being noticed.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
    
- **Ambiguities / potential tensions:**
    
    - Sustainability: The model depends on high creative throughput, intense staff engagement, and tight control of the experience; long-term scalability across multiple tours, possible theme parks, and global youth programs may strain these standards.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
        
    - Inclusivity vs. selectivity: While the ethos is widespread inclusion, the reality of multi-year ticket waitlists and limited capacity introduces scarcity that could, over time, conflict with the “for everyone” narrative if not carefully managed.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3JBn76zlC5E&list=WL&index=14)
