## 📝 Executive Summary

The conversation explores how Jesse Cole turned the Savannah Bananas from a bankrupt, ignored baseball team into a “modern-day Disney” by obsessively designing unforgettable fan experiences, eliminating friction, and nurturing an internal culture that matches the external show. He argues that belief, relentless experimentation, and putting “fans first” (customers and employees) are more important than short-term profit, and that you must push through messy early failures to build something truly remarkable.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)

---

## 🎯 Key Takeaways

- **Belief before ability:** Deep conviction in the vision—shared visibly and repeatedly—attracts people, capital, and opportunities long before there is proof of success.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Fans-first obsession:** Design every decision around how it makes fans feel, from pricing and food to post-game moments, even at the expense of short-term revenue.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Eliminate friction, then “plus” the experience:** Start by removing pain points in the customer journey, then continually add delight, treating every event as a prototype.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Experiment aggressively and embrace messy:** Commit to trying many new ideas in public, knowing the first versions will fail, and treat each “at bat” as a learning opportunity.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Culture symmetry:** The internal experience of employees must mirror the magic fans see externally, or engagement and loyalty will erode.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)

## 💡 Core Concepts

## 1. Fans First as Operating System

- Cole’s core identity is “Fans First Entertainment”: success is measured by creating “fans first moments” one person at a time, not by revenue or valuation.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- He explicitly prefers building “a billion fans” over being a billion-dollar brand and institutionalizes this through rituals: pre-game huddles about fans-first stories, post-show recognition, and weekly staff chats focused on fan moments.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- The brand’s purpose is emotional: to make people feel joy, belonging, and wonder together in a physical space, especially in a hyper-digital world.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    

## 2. Belief, Vision, and Narrative

- Cole emphasizes that belief is contagious and precedes ability; his job is to hold a bold vision (e.g., selling out Fenway, NFL stadiums) and communicate why it matters.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- His personal story—starting with $1.8 million in debt, years in low-level minor league baseball, then nearly going broke in Savannah—anchors the narrative of resilience and long-term vision.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- The Fenway Park story (from childhood Red Sox fan and failed pro prospect to selling out Fenway with the Bananas) illustrates the power of long arc dreams and “full-circle” moments.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    

## 3. Friction Elimination and Experience Design

- Cole frames innovation as systematically eliminating friction points and then designing the “perfect experience” backwards.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- In baseball, friction included: games too long and slow, boring downtime, nickel-and-diming with fees and concessions, and distant player-fan relationships.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- Banana Ball rules are engineered to fix those frictions: two-hour time limit, points per inning instead of cumulative runs, no bunting, no mound visits, no stepping out, walks replaced with a sprint sequence, and fans able to record outs by catching foul balls.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- Financial friction is removed via all-inclusive tickets (e.g., $15 including unlimited food and drinks in early days) and an explicit stance against junk fees; the organization even pays the taxes on tickets and merchandise.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    

## 4. Attention Plan vs. Marketing Plan

- Cole distinguishes between standard marketing (incremental and “loud”) and an “attention plan” (doing something no one else would dare do).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- Naming the team “Savannah Bananas” from a single suggestion in thousands was an intentional attention play, despite anticipating local backlash; they prepped responses to criticism before announcing.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- The initial result was hostility and ridicule (boos in the St. Patrick’s parade, calls to be “thrown out of town”), which he reframed as a sign they had finally become visible and now had to prove the experience.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    

## 5. Experimentation and the “Messy” Phase

- Cole describes a 10-year apprenticeship of failures before Savannah, then further failures after launch: selling only two tickets early on, running out of money, moving into a filthy garage, eating on $30/week, and nearly missing payroll.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- The first all-inclusive opening night had massive operational breakdowns: two- to three-hour food lines and supply miscalculations, though the show elements (dancing grandmas “Banana Nanas,” banana baby ceremony, players giving roses) delighted fans.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- He uses Walt Disney’s disastrous “Black Sunday” at Disneyland as a reference: greatness requires enduring and learning from chaotic, flawed first iterations.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    

## 6. Culture, People, and Structural Choices

- Cole argues that employees are the company’s “biggest fans” and designs the schedule and policies to serve them: no games Monday–Wednesday, two consecutive weekends completely off, and family inclusion on road trips.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- They invest heavily in people: surprise bucket-list trips (e.g., sending a long-time employee and her skeptical father to Ireland), personalized first-day experiences, and family-focused logistics on tour.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- The “thank you experiment” (daily handwritten letters for years, now also videos) operationalizes gratitude and personal recognition as part of leadership practice.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    

## 7. Input, Identity, and Drive

- Cole ties his drive partly to childhood: being an only child, parents’ divorce, a hardworking father who rewarded achievement (grades and athletic performance), and a formative empathetic moment with Red Sox pitcher Lee Smith.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- He sees himself as achievement-driven but intentionally shapes his mental environment by saturating his “input” with innovators (Disney, Jobs, Bezos, WWE, Cirque du Soleil, SNL), which he says directly shapes his “output.”[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    

## 8. Ideation vs. Execution

- The organization has a formal cadence: a full day each Tuesday devoted to ideation, influenced by SNL’s weekly creative process, but with a bias toward same-day or near-term experiments.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- Cole insists ideas must quickly cross into execution even when incomplete; he cites forcing a new intern to turn a “thank-you rap idea” into a live fan call by 4 p.m. on his first day as a pattern: remove red tape, shorten feedback loops, and normalize awkward first tries.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- He explicitly frames their goal as being “the fastest learning organization in sports,” which requires trying 10–15 new things every game and accepting that several will fail.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)

## 🔨 Actionable Tips

## For Entrepreneurs and Operators

- **Design an attention plan:** List 3–5 things you could do that competitors would never dare (name, uniforms, rituals, ridiculous guarantees) and commit to testing at least one in the next 90 days.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Map friction and design backwards:** Audit your customer journey for time-wasters, surprise charges, confusing rules, and emotional low points; pick one area and design a “perfect experience,” then work backwards to a minimal viable version you can deploy.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Institutionalize experimentation:** Create a weekly “idea to execution” ritual where at least one new idea must be shipped in the next cycle, even in rough form, and publicly debrief both successes and failures.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Shift metrics from revenue to fans:** Track tangible “fans first moments” (surprise upgrades, handwritten notes, proactive fixes after mistakes) alongside financial metrics, and celebrate these stories in internal meetings.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    

## For Leaders and Culture Builders

- **Align internal and external experience:** List the magic you promise customers; then assess whether employees experience similar care (time off, recognition, surprises, autonomy) and close the largest gap with one concrete policy change.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Protect energy with role design:** For yourself and key leaders, identify work that drains vs. energizes; offload at least one draining category (e.g., spreadsheets, ops) and increase time in “create, share, grow” type activities.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Codify values through rituals:** Borrow Cole’s practice of pre- and post-event huddles focused on stories that embody your values, plus a weekly all-hands segment dedicated to those principles.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    

## For Personal Development

- **Practice gratitude at scale:** Start your own “thank you experiment” with a daily note, email, or video to someone who impacted you (mentor, colleague, employee, family member), and track how it changes relationships and your own mindset.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Curate your inputs:** Replace a portion of your media diet with long-form content from innovators you admire; treat it as proximity that upgrades your own strategic imagination.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Reframe failure as “messy phase”:** For any current initiative, explicitly label current frustrations as the messy first version and identify a clear “next at bat” (next test, next prototype, next conversation) to keep momentum.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)

## 🔍 Additional Insights

- **Pricing and misperception:** Cole is acutely aware of the reputational risk around pricing; secondary-market markups create memes portraying him as greedy, despite fixed primary prices and no fees, which he experiences as painful misrepresentation. He counters this by over-communicating intent and personal transparency.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Secondary markets as a strategic threat:** He views ticket scalping and inflated resale prices as a problem to solve, not a windfall, and signals intent to “put a stop to that,” framing it as misalignment with fans-first values.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Self-disruption:** Rather than coast on successful exhibitions, the organization is deliberately “disrupting itself” by creating a competitive league with six teams, despite inevitable loss of some fans who preferred the original format; this is framed as serving future fans and a larger vision.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Resilience scripts:** Stories of rain-delay scripting, three-hour contingency shows, and moving families on the road illustrate using structure and planning to turn potential disasters (weather, delays) into legendary experiences.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
    
- **Ambiguous or debatable points:**
    
    - The long-term financial sustainability of extreme fan-centric pricing (all-inclusive low-cost tickets, high tax absorption, frequent time off for staff) is not unpacked in detail and could be contentious in more traditional business contexts.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
        
    - The degree to which their model generalizes to large incumbents (NFL, airlines, national restaurant chains) is discussed aspirationally rather than with hard data, leaving room for debate about scalability and regulatory/operational constraints.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EoIFhDNzV3o&list=WL&index=17)
