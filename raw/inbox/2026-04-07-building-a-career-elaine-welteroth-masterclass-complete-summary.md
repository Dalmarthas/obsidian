📋 OVERVIEW

This masterclass is taught by Elaine Welteroth, the youngest-ever editor-in-chief of Teen Vogue at age 29, who transformed the publication from a fashion magazine into a platform bridging social justice and pop culture. Now a New York Times bestselling author, talk show host, Project Runway judge, and multi-hyphenate creative entrepreneur, Elaine shares her blueprint for designing an unconventional career path.

The course is designed for creative entrepreneurs, mid-career pivoters, and big dreamers who want to break free from traditional career boxes and craft a fulfilling professional life aligned with their authentic selves. Rather than climbing a conventional corporate ladder, Elaine teaches how to become the "boss of your own life" by identifying your unique zone of genius, building strategic infrastructure, and creating multiple revenue streams across different industries.

Through personal stories of both triumphs and failures—including getting canceled for a well-intentioned story about Black hair, freezing when meeting Oprah, and navigating the terrifying decision to leave her dream job—Elaine provides honest, practical guidance on everything from overcoming imposter syndrome to building your team, managing finances, and marketing yourself authentically in the digital age.

💡 KEY CONCEPTS & FRAMEWORKS

THE ZONE OF GENIUS

Definition: The sweet spot at the intersection of your passions, talents, values, and skills—that which only you can do like nobody else can.

The Four Pillars:
1. PASSION: What you would do all night for free if your bills were paid
   - The thing that lights you up inside
   - What you can't stop thinking about
   - What energizes rather than depletes you

2. TALENTS: Your natural gifts that you may take for granted
   - Skills that come easily to you
   - What others compliment you on
   - Ask your best friend to list your talents—they'll mirror back gifts you overlook

3. VALUES: What matters more to you than money
   - Your non-negotiables
   - What you stand for and believe in
   - Your values become a filter for making hard decisions about opportunities

4. SKILLS: Specific competencies you've cultivated along your journey
   - Every job has taught you something useful
   - No experience is wasted—all tools in your toolkit will be needed
   - Technical abilities, soft skills, and lived experience all count

Goal: Spend as much of your day operating from your zone of genius

THE BLUEPRINT (Eagle Eye View)

Your 30,000-foot view of your career's lifespan spanning short-term goals to long-game plans.

Components:
• Who do you want to model your career after?
• What spaces/industries do you want to play in?
• What is the arc you want your career to take?
• How do these divergent interests connect to a singular purpose?

Benefits:
• Provides flexibility to make mistakes along the way
• Prevents freaking out when you veer off course
• Reminds you that where you are is temporary
• Keeps you focused on the destination while navigating detours

THE MIND MAP (Hawkeye View)

Your granular, specific business model showing exactly how you'll execute your blueprint.

Structure:
• CENTER: Your WHY—your purpose as the nucleus connecting everything
• CIRCLES: Different spaces/industries you want to play in (TV, editorial, speaking, nonprofit, etc.)
• SUB-CIRCLES: Specific projects or ideas within each space (book titles, show concepts, etc.)
• CONNECTIONS: Lines showing how different projects might intersect or feed each other

Evolution:
• Created before major transitions or big decisions
• Updated regularly as you gain clarity
• Becomes more defined with time and experience
• Can include financial figures next to each bucket (especially when planning to leave corporate job)

Inspired by: Tyra Banks' approach to architecting her mogul empire

THE POINT OF DIMINISHING RETURNS

The critical question to ask before making major career moves: "Have I reached the point of diminishing returns?"

Considerations:
• Is there something you still need to learn in your current role?
• Is there a skill you're still honing that you'll need in your next chapter?
• Is there a financial goal you haven't yet hit?
• Would you have regrets if you walked away today?
• What are you still getting in return for your time, energy, and hard work?

Purpose: Prevents rash decisions made from emotion while honoring genuine readiness to move on

FINANCIAL CONFIDENCE VS. FINANCIAL SECURITY

Financial Security: An illusion that keeps people stuck
Financial Confidence: Having a game plan that enables you to dream big

Building Financial Confidence:
• Set a specific savings target that will support your leap of faith
• The number will differ at different life stages and with different needs
• Write down figures you need monthly/yearly
• Map financial projections onto your mind map buckets
• Have conversations to understand how lucrative different spaces are
• Test waters with side hustles while still employed

The Five Pillars of Career Foundation:
1. Zone of Genius (passion, talents, values, skills)
2. Network/Community
3. Skills Development
4. Strategic Planning
5. Financial Confidence

EXCAVATION PROCESS

The process of digging deep inside yourself to uncover your most valuable traits and authentic self.

Methods:
• Examine early childhood memories and stories
• Look at who you were before the world imposed labels and limitations
• Ask: "What would I do if my bills were paid and I had total freedom?"
• Identify patterns in what has always energized you
• Find the origin stories that illuminate who you've always been

Example: Elaine's mother's memory of baby Elaine glaring at her when she disciplined Elaine's brother—an early sign of her lifelong commitment to being an active ally and advocating for justice

Purpose: Return to your authentic self before societal conditioning

HELL YES OR NO PHILOSOPHY

When deciding whether to take on a project or opportunity, it should be either "Hell Yes!" or "No."

"Hell Yes" Criteria (personalize these):
• Pays more than your bills
• Gives you a sense of value in spaces you want to operate in
• Energizes rather than depletes you
• Aligns with your zone of genius
• Moves you toward your long-term vision
• Feels fulfilling, not just financially viable

Avoid: Saying yes to everything out of hustle mentality or fear of missing out

Benefits: Prevents burnout and keeps you focused on fulfilling work

FOD (FIRST, ONLY, OR DIFFERENT)

Term coined by Shonda Rhimes describing people who are:
• First in their field or family to do what they're doing
• The only one who looks like them in the room
• Just plain different

Impact:
• More likely to experience imposter syndrome
• May feel paralyzed or intimidated in professional settings
• Often feel like frauds despite accomplishments
• Subject to internalized negative self-talk from systemic oppression

Reality: While the term speaks specifically to marginalized groups, feeling different is universal

INTENTION VS. IMPACT

Critical distinction when facing controversy or being called out:

Intention: What you meant to do or communicate
Impact: The actual effect your actions/words had on others

When Called Out:
• Don't focus on defending your intentions first
• Focus on acknowledging the impact and harm caused
• Show you understand why it was harmful
• Once people feel heard, defensive conversation becomes possible
• Only then can you explain your intentions and learning process

Common Mistake: Immediately defending with "That wasn't my intention" shuts down productive dialogue

CULTURAL APPROPRIATION VS. CULTURAL APPRECIATION

Understanding this distinction is essential for making resonant, respectful content.

Warning Signs You're Appropriating:
• Taking elements from marginalized cultures without proper context or credit
• Using cultural imagery/practices without understanding their significance
• Casting decisions that center privileged voices in marginalized stories
• Creating content without earning trust from the communities you're representing

Path to Appreciation:
• Educate yourself and your team on cultural sensitivities
• Center voices from the actual community
• Earn the right to celebrate diversity through consistent, authentic representation
• Allow marginalized communities to tell their own stories
• Be willing to have uncomfortable conversations about past mistakes

SCARCITY VS. ABUNDANCE MENTALITY

Scarcity Mindset:
• Hoarding every penny
• Afraid to invest in help
• Seeing team members as expenses
• Operating from fear of loss

Abundance Mindset:
• Every hire is a revenue-generating opportunity
• Team members are investors in your enterprise
• Spending money to make money
• Operating from faith in growth
• Understanding that 5% of nothing is nothing, but 5% of $3 million is substantial

Required Shift: Moving from employee mentality (protecting paycheck) to CEO mentality (strategic investment)

HIRE FOR BLIND SPOTS, NOT CULTURE FIT

Traditional Thinking: Hire people who look, sound, act, and think like you do
Better Approach: Identify gaps in your knowledge/perspective and hire specifically for those

Examples:
• Don't know about social justice? Add that to your job description
• Weak in areas outside your zone of genius? Hire support there
• Missing diverse perspectives? Intentionally seek out different backgrounds

Benefits:
• Strengthens your enterprise where you're weakest
• Brings perspectives you lack
• Creates work that resonates with broader audiences
• Prevents blind spots that lead to mistakes

JOY CONTAINERS

Concept from Priya Parker (author of "The Art of Gathering"): Scheduled appointments with joy

Implementation:
• Literally put joy on your Google calendar
• Treat it like any other meeting—don't skip or cancel
• Frequency: Once a day or once a week, depending on your needs
• Be present when the calendar alert goes off
• It doesn't have to be big: dance, cry, hug, eat pizza, chase a waterfall

Examples from Elaine:
• Standing up from writing to find a waterfall
• Eating a whole pizza when craving hit
• Any activity that brings genuine joy in the moment

Purpose: Prevents burnout, restores creative flow, increases productivity, maintains mental health

📊 STEP-BY-STEP METHODOLOGY

PHASE 1: DISCOVER YOUR ZONE OF GENIUS

Step 1: Excavate Your Passion
• Ask: "What would I do all night for free if my bills were paid?"
• If you draw a blank, that's okay—you've been conditioned to live on autopilot
• Look at childhood stories and early memories
• Notice what energizes vs. depletes you
• Give yourself permission to not know yet

Step 2: Identify Your Talents
• Ask your best friend to list what you're good at
• Notice what comes naturally to you that seems hard for others
• Pay attention to what people compliment you on
• Recognize gifts you've been taking for granted

Step 3: Define Your Values
• Ask: "What matters more to me than money?"
• Ask: "Why does that matter?"
• Write down your answers—these are your core values
• Use these as filters for decision-making
• Ensure opportunities don't negate your value system

Step 4: Catalog Your Skills
• List every skill you've cultivated in every job
• Include technical skills, soft skills, and lived experiences
• Trust that all tools in your toolkit will be useful
• Recognize that no turn was a wrong turn

Step 5: Find Your Intersection
• Where do your passions, talents, values, and skills overlap?
• What can you do that nobody else can do quite like you?
• This is your zone of genius—your compass going forward

PHASE 2: CREATE YOUR BLUEPRINT & MIND MAP

Step 1: Identify Role Models
• Who are people whose careers you want to model yours after?
• They don't need to look like you or work in your exact field
• Study their career trajectory
• What spaces do they operate in?
• How did they build their multi-hyphenate empire?

Step 2: Pick Your Lanes (Eagle Eye View)
• What industries/spaces do you want to play in?
• Examples: television, editorial, speaking, nonprofit work, podcasting, etc.
• Remember: It's never too late to pivot
• Choose multiple lanes that connect to a central purpose

Step 3: Define Your WHY
• What is your singular purpose that connects all your interests?
• This becomes the nucleus of your mind map
• Every project should tie back to this purpose
• Example: Elaine's purpose is bridging social justice and pop culture

Step 4: Create Your Visual Mind Map
• Put your WHY in the center
• Create circles around it for each space you want to play in
• Connect smaller circles to each main circle with specific projects
• Add lines showing how different projects might intersect
• Include financial figures when planning major transitions

Step 5: Update Regularly
• Revisit your mind map during transitions
• Refine it as you gain clarity
• Add new projects as they emerge
• Remove what no longer serves your vision
• Make it more specific over time

PHASE 3: BUILD YOUR NETWORK & BOARD OF ADVISORS

Step 1: Identify Your Board of Advisors
• Choose 5-10 people to serve as your informal board
• They're not financial investors, but investors in YOU
• Be intentional about where they sit in your life
• Tell them they're on your board (when appropriate)
• Remember: you're not paying them, so be mindful of their time

Step 2: Pursue Mentorship Strategically
• Don't ask "Will you be my mentor?" cold—it's like proposing marriage on first meeting
• Start with coffee and a specific, prepared ask
• Do your homework—don't waste their time
• Be clear and direct about what you need
• Court the relationship like any meaningful connection

Step 3: Consider Peer-to-Peer Mentorship
• Look around, not just up
• People in the trenches with you offer unique value
• They're figuring out what's never been done alongside you
• Early career connections can grow with you to executive levels
• Don't underestimate lateral relationships

Step 4: Curate Digital Mentors
• Follow people whose careers inspire you
• Study how they show up in the world
• Learn from their approach and spirit
• You can glean wisdom even without direct access
• It's okay to slide into DMs, but don't expect responses

Step 5: Build Community Through Giving
• Give without expecting anything in return
• Show up for people around you generously
• Maintain relationships—you never know when you'll need help
• Be the person others want to support
• Practice "lift as you rise"

Step 6: Differentiate Manager from Mentor
• Your boss is your boss—they have power to fire you
• A great boss who mentors is "extra credit"
• Have mentors outside your workplace who don't pay you
• When negotiating raises with your boss, remember they're not your friend
• Cultivate the workplace relationship around skill development and feedback

PHASE 4: DEVELOP FINANCIAL CONFIDENCE

Step 1: Set Your Savings Target
• Decide what figure you need to feel confident taking a leap
• This will differ at different life stages
• Be honest about your needs and lifestyle
• Don't just save the minimum—add a cushion
• Example: Elaine saved $10,000 before moving to NYC for $10/hour internship

Step 2: Create Your Financial Plan
• Revisit your mind map and add financial figures to each bucket
• Research how lucrative different spaces are
• Have conversations with people in those fields
• Understand what one can expect to make
• Your plan can be creative—it doesn't need to be a traditional business plan

Step 3: Identify Revenue Streams
• Which bucket will pay your bills?
• Which bucket funds your passion projects?
• Which bucket covers philanthropy?
• How do different revenue streams work together?
• Create a self-sustaining ecosystem

Step 4: Test Waters with Side Hustles
• Start small while you still have a day job
• Book a few gigs to build confidence
• See what lifestyle feels like before committing
• Gather financial data from real opportunities
• Build safety net while still employed

Step 5: Track Progress Toward Your Goal
• Know your monthly and yearly financial targets
• Before quitting your job, ensure you can match or exceed your current income
• Have multiple revenue streams identified
• Feel confident in your projections
• Trust your plan before taking the leap

PHASE 5: STRATEGIZE WORK-LIFE BALANCE

Step 1: Acknowledge the Reality of Hard Work
• Early on, you will work hard and long hours
• You'll say yes more than no at first
• But hard work doesn't mean burning out
• You're building something sustainable
• Marathon mindset, not sprint mentality

Step 2: Create Your Self-Care Strategy
• Identify one practice you can commit to
• Put it on a sticky note, on your calendar
• Make appointments with joy—"joy containers"
• Treat these appointments like any other meeting
• Don't skip or cancel last minute

Step 3: Develop Your Personal Criteria
• What fuels you vs. depletes you?
• What do you actually enjoy doing?
• Write down your "Hell Yes" criteria
• Get clear on what energizes you
• Spend more time on fulfilling projects

Step 4: Recognize Burnout Signs
• Anxiety you didn't know you had
• Physical toll on your body
• Strain on relationships
• Obsessing about work 24/7
• Feeling guilty when not working

Step 5: Build Sustainable Practices
• Prayer, meditation, or spiritual practice
• Reading devotionals in morning
• Regular exercise or movement
• Time with family and community
• Hobbies completely unrelated to work
• Therapy or professional mental health support

PHASE 6: EXPLORE SIDE HUSTLES & TEST OPPORTUNITIES

Step 1: Make Your Dream List
• If you could design life with no collateral damage, what would it look like?
• Write down what would make you happiest
• Don't filter based on what seems practical
• Let yourself dream freely
• Keep this list in your notebook

Step 2: Apply Dreams to Your Day Job
• Look for ways to bring more of yourself to current work
• Find outlets for passions within existing role
• Develop skills you want to use in future
• Example: Elaine reached out to fashion week publicists without permission to get Ebony magazine invitations

Step 3: Create a Job Within Your Job
• Identify gaps or opportunities in your workplace
• Propose solutions that allow entrepreneurial spirit
• Exercise creativity in your current position
• Get paid to develop skills for future ventures
• Add value outside your strict job description

Step 4: Start Small Outside Work
• One hour before work, one hour after
• Develop small business or hobby
• Build confidence with low stakes
• Create something you can grow over time
• Don't quit day job until side hustle proves viable

Step 5: Test Freelance Opportunities
• Over 1/3 of American workers are freelancers
• Speaking engagements, consulting, etc.
• Build confidence within safety net of day job
• Set financial targets for each opportunity
• See if you can reach income goals before leaving corporate job

PHASE 7: MAKE THE DECISION TO LEAVE (OR STAY)

Step 1: Ask the Critical Question
• Have I reached the point of diminishing returns?
• Is there more for me to learn here?
• Is there a skill I'm still honing that I'll need later?
• Have I hit my financial goal?
• Would I have regrets if I left today?

Step 2: Make a Pros & Cons List
• Write out all reasons to stay
• Write out all reasons to leave
• Be honest about external validation vs. internal fulfillment
• Consider steady paycheck vs. freedom
• Acknowledge the fear and do it anyway

Step 3: Consult Your Board of Advisors
• Go to someone who sees your potential
• Ask for their honest perspective
• Allow them to mirror back your courage
• Listen when they call you to be braver
• Trust their vision when fear clouds yours

Step 4: Know That You Know
• Do the self-evaluation thoroughly
• Trust your gut once you've done the work
• If you know you're ready, take the leap
• Don't look back once you've decided
• The universe is calling you to be braver

Step 5: Make Your Exit Gracefully
• Give proper notice if possible
• Leave on good terms
• Maintain relationships
• Express gratitude for what you learned
• Position yourself for future collaboration

PHASE 8: BUILD YOUR TEAM

Step 1: Define Your Company Values & Culture
• Sit down before hiring anyone
• Map out your core values
• Visualize the right environment for you to thrive
• Set intentions for the energy you want
• Write down deal breakers and non-negotiables

Personal Policies to Consider:
• No asshole policy
• No yes-man policy
• Want people who challenge and critique respectfully
• Seek people who strengthen your talents
• Find collaborators who help you connect with community

Step 2: Hire Incrementally
• Don't hire everyone at once
• You're still learning what support you actually need
• Start small and add as needs become clear
• Write job descriptions for each role
• Be specific about expectations

Step 3: Do a Needs Assessment
• Where are the blind spots in your ecosystem?
• Where are the holes?
• What takes you away from your zone of genius?
• What tasks drain your energy?
• Where do you need expert support?

Step 4: Think of Hires as Revenue-Generating
• Shift from scarcity to abundance mentality
• Every hire should add value to your enterprise
• Team members on percentage basis are investors in you
• They're taking a bet on you too
• Make sure revenue-generating roles actually generate revenue

Step 5: Hire for Blind Spots, Not Culture Fit
• Identify where you lack knowledge or perspective
• Be intentional about diversifying your team
• Add cultural sensitivity if that's missing
• Seek different backgrounds and experiences
• Complement your weaknesses with others' strengths

Step 6: Delegate Effectively
• Good CEOs can do many jobs but don't
• Bosses delegate; they don't do it all
• Free up your time to operate in zone of genius
• Trust people operating in their zone of genius
• Let go of tasks outside your sweet spot

Step 7: Fire Faster
• Have conversations about expectations
• Give adequate time to see if changes happen
• If not working out, pull the plug
• You're usually doing them a favor too
• Part ways graciously and respectfully
• Expedite finding the right person for your team

PHASE 9: DEVELOP ESSENTIAL WRITING SKILLS

Professional Writing (Emails, Business Communication):

Step 1: Study and Model
• Observe how your manager writes emails
• Match their format and tone
• If they say "dear," you say "dear"
• If they say "hey," you say "hi" (slightly more formal)

Step 2: Maintain Professionalism
• Avoid casual language and over-familiarity
• Limit exclamation points
• Err on side of formality
• Find your flow, then proofread
• Proofread again

Step 3: Be Concise
• No essays in emails
• People won't read long messages
• Brevity is key in professional writing
• Communicate points clearly without distraction
• Get to the point

Authentic/Creative Writing (Social Media, Books, Articles):

Step 1: Find Your Voice
• Be authentic to your true voice
• Make it "voicey"—does it sound like you?
• Say the thing out loud, then write exactly as you spoke it
• That's what pops off the page
• Don't try to sound like someone else

Step 2: Overcome Writer's Block
• Open an email instead of blank document
• Takes pressure off the creative perfectionist brain
• You write emails easily all day—start there
• Can even start your book in an email
• Transfer to proper document after flow starts

Step 3: Turn Off Perfectionism
• Especially for social media
• Your first thought is often most authentic
• Capture the thought as it comes to you
• That's usually the most voicey way to communicate
• Even bestselling authors spend embarrassing amounts of time on Instagram captions

Step 4: Embrace the Process
• Good writing takes time—even for professionals
• That pithy, clever tweet probably took lots of thought
• You're not alone if you overthink it
• Authenticity comes through in first thoughts
• If it doesn't feel right, it won't come through as authentic

PHASE 10: PRESENT YOURSELF FOR OPPORTUNITY

Step 1: Find Your Signature Staples
• Maximize decision-making time
• Don't labor over daily outfit choices
• Develop core signature style elements
• Elaine's staples: big curly hair, white boots, gold aviators, bold structured suits

Step 2: Balance Different Sides of Yourself
• Eccentric and bold
• Structured and business-minded
• Playful and colorful
• Professional and no-nonsense
• Let your staples communicate multiple aspects of your personality

Step 3: Develop Your Personal Style
• Make a mood board of looks you're drawn to
• Study people with similar body type or vibe
• Try new silhouettes, fabrics, cuts
• Take pictures and see what feels authentic
• Step outside comfort zone experimentally

Step 4: Prioritize Comfort and Confidence
• Most important thing is feeling comfortable
• Must feel confident in what you wear
• Doesn't matter if you're not a "fashion icon"
• Your look should support your business and work
• You don't need to work in fashion to care about presentation

Step 5: Understand the Impact
• How you present yourself influences how you're perceived
• How you're perceived influences opportunities that come your way
• Every aspect of showing up matters
• It's not shallow to be thoughtful about presentation
• Excellence in outfit is about intentionality

PHASE 11: MARKET YOURSELF & YOUR WORK

Social Media Strategy:

Step 1: Think of Instagram as Your Storefront Window
• You're the artisan in the back creating
• Your social media is the window that needs daily refreshing
• People need to see your brand regularly
• When product is ready, audience is already there
• Don't let the storefront go stale while you create

Step 2: Develop a Workflow
• Plan ahead, especially during busy seasons
• Use grid planners to pre-schedule posts
• Set consistent posting times (high traffic periods)
• Post in morning to capture "good morning scroll"
• Use preset filters to establish visual brand

Step 3: Organize Your Content
• Create shared album for Instagram-worthy images
• Don't dig through camera roll last minute
• Link Instagram to Twitter and Facebook for auto-publishing
• Save time with systems
• Manage look and feel from eagle-eye view

Step 4: Post Regularly with Goals
• Try to change content daily, or several times a week
• Set a target and work toward it
• Build it into your workflow
• Account for time management
• You can't just not do it

Step 5: Leverage Social Media Beyond Marketing
• It's not just about selling
• Express yourself authentically
• Hone your voice and writing craft
• Find and be who you really are
• Share parts of yourself and creativity

Authentic Marketing:

Step 1: Stay Connected to Your WHY
• Marketing can feel overwhelming like you're just a salesman
• Keep your purpose at center of every step
• Example: Elaine's book was invitation to conversation with community of women of color
• Let your why inform social media activation and events
• Authenticity in purpose prevents burnout

Step 2: Make It Resonate
• Think about your specific community
• Who will connect with your work?
• How can you reach them genuinely?
• What do they need to hear?
• How can you serve them?

Step 3: Show Your Process
• If you don't have finished product yet, show creation
• Set up phone, click hyperlapse, let audience watch you work
• Give 60-second reel of your process
• Let people into your creative journey
• Audiences connect with authenticity

Step 4: Disrupt Traditional Formulas
• Learn the typical approach in your field
• Then figure out how to rewrite the rules
• Infuse with more intention
• Make it interactive, noisy, resonant
• Don't be afraid to break all the rules

Case Study: Elaine's Book Tour
• Traditional: Author speaks, moderator asks questions, Q&A
• Elaine's disruption: Cards under every chair
• Asked audience to write what they're claiming space for
• Asked them to write what's holding them back
• Invited people to share on microphone
• Most magical moment of entire book launch
• Came from disrupting status quo

Step 5: Allow Yourself to Be Surprised
• Don't assume you know what will resonate
• Test different approaches
• Pay attention to what audience connects with
• People connect most with authenticity
• If it doesn't feel right to you, that comes through

PHASE 12: NAVIGATE CONTROVERSY & CANCEL CULTURE

Preparation:

Step 1: Accept the Reality
• If you're making disruptive content, you might get canceled
• It's not about avoiding controversy—it's about handling it responsibly
• You can't shy away from hot topics if you want to participate in cultural conversation
• Making resonant, relevant work requires engaging with difficult issues
• Be prepared to learn and bounce back

When You Get Called Out:

Step 1: DON'T Focus on Intention First
• Don't go defensive immediately
• Don't start with "That wasn't my intention"
• Don't justify with "My intentions were good"
• Natural reaction is to defend yourself—resist this
• Focusing on intention first shuts down productive conversation

Step 2: DO Pause, Take a Breath, Reflect
• Stop and breathe before responding
• Take time to understand the criticism
• Reflect on what actually happened
• Don't react immediately from defensive place
• Give yourself space to process

Step 3: DO Focus on Impact
• Acknowledge the harm you caused
• Acknowledge that you understand why it was harmful
• Start your response by addressing impact
• When people feel heard, defensive conversation becomes possible
• Only then can you explain intentions and learning

Step 4: Address It Head-On (Usually)
• Corporate communications might advise "let it blow over"
• News cycle is fast—sometimes ignoring works
• But engaging head-on can build deeper relationships
• Gives you chance to communicate who you are
• Shows what you've learned from controversy

Step 5: Write an Authentic Response
• Be genuine in your acknowledgment
• Express humility and empathy
• Explain what you've learned
• Don't make excuses
• Invite continued dialogue

Step 6: Learn and Course-Correct
• Mistakes are opportunities to improve
• Have team conversations about the issues
• Educate yourself and others
• Set new standards
• Rebuild trust with marginalized communities

Step 7: DON'T Shut Down
• It's not the end of the world
• The world still needs your perspective
• Learn lessons before re-engaging
• Use the experience to create better work
• Come back stronger and more informed

Best Practices Summary:
• DON'T be defensive
• DO pause, listen, and reflect
• DON'T ignore other perspectives
• DO empathize and ask questions
• DO apologize authentically with humility and empathy
• Focus on impact, not just intent
• Own your credibility and your work
• Have filters when venturing into sensitive territory around race, identity, gender
• Don't let fear prevent you from putting work out
• Learn and grow from every controversy

PHASE 13: OVERCOME IMPOSTER SYNDROME

Understanding Imposter Syndrome:

Recognize the Reality:
• Affects everyone at some point, especially FODs (First, Only, or Different)
• Even highly successful people feel it
• It's the internalized voice saying you don't belong
• Symptom of larger systematic racism, sexism, classism, ageism
• Like breathing polluted air—we're all infected with harmful ideas

Identify Negative Self-Talk:
• Pay attention to voices in your head
• Notice when you tell yourself your work isn't worthy
• Catch when you think your voice doesn't matter
• See the patterns in self-doubt
• Recognize these are learned, not truth

Combat Imposter Syndrome:

Step 1: Recognize and Name It
• See the negative thoughts: "I see you, I hear you"
• Acknowledge without letting them control you
• Understand you can unlearn harmful belief systems
• Know these thought patterns can be disrupted
• Realize imposter syndrome is not reality

Step 2: Create Counter-Narratives
• Develop a personal mantra to push back against negative thoughts
• Have ready responses to self-doubt
• Replace "I'm a fraud" with "I belong here"
• Replace "My voice doesn't matter" with "My perspective is needed"
• Practice these counter-thoughts

Step 3: Use Positive Affirmations
• Write them on sticky notes
• Place them where you spend time: desk, house, phone
• Plant positive messages in your life
• Reference them often
• Might feel "woo-woo" but it works

Step 4: Remember Your Power
• Your difference is your superpower
• Your unique perspective is why you're in the room
• People need your voice and lived experience
• Your differences inform your creativity
• Work resonates more when it includes diverse perspectives

Step 5: Understand Your Value
• You're not an outsider—you're essential
• Your dissenting opinion matters
• Speaking up even when scared is crucial
• You belong at every table you're at
• Your cultural identity is a strength

Step 6: Put Language to Your Experience
• Understanding your cultural identity is essential
• The more you know yourself, the better your work connects
• Name your experiences clearly
• Own your background
• Let it inform your art and work

Visualization & Preparation:

Step 1: Visualize Success
• Before intimidating moments, visualize how you want to feel
• Role play in your mind long before the moment comes
• Practice what you want to convey about yourself
• Build muscle memory to fall back on
• Plant ahead for moments of potential paralysis

• Your hands might shake, throat might ball up

• It takes courage and preparation
• Gets easier with practice

Step 3: Prepare What You Can
• Plan for what's controllable
• Have a few things to say ready
• Know what you want to communicate about yourself
• Don't wait until the moment to figure it out
• Preparation prevents paralysis

Step 4: Seize Opportunities
• When the moment comes, go for it
• Worst that can happen? You fumble—you won't be first or last
• You'll kick yourself more for not trying
• Better to attempt and fail than never try
• Give yourself permission to be imperfect

✅ ACTION ITEMS & EXERCISES

Self-Discovery Exercises:

□ THE EXCAVATION JOURNAL
• Write about early childhood memories
• Ask family members for stories about you before you could speak
• Identify patterns in what has always energized you
• Look for origin stories that illuminate who you've always been
• Write down: "If my bills were paid and I had total freedom, what would I do all night for free?"

□ ZONE OF GENIUS MAPPING
• PASSION: List 3-5 things you'd do for free if bills were covered
• TALENTS: Ask your best friend to list what you're good at
• VALUES: Write answer to "What matters more to me than money?" and "Why?"
• SKILLS: Catalog every skill from every job you've had
• INTERSECTION: Identify where all four overlap

□ EARLY LIFE PATTERNS
• What were you doing or interested in as a child?
• What games did you love to play?
• What subjects fascinated you in school?
• What did adults notice about you?
• What felt effortless and energizing?

Career Planning Exercises:

□ CREATE YOUR BLUEPRINT (Eagle Eye)
• Identify 2-3 people whose careers you want to model
• Research their career trajectories
• List 3-7 industries/spaces you want to play in
• Connect them to a singular purpose (your WHY)
• Write out the arc you want your career to take

□ BUILD YOUR MIND MAP (Hawkeye)
• Put your WHY in the center of a page
• Create circles around it for each space you want to play in
• Connect smaller circles with specific projects
• Add lines showing how projects might intersect
• Update quarterly or during major transitions

□ ROLE MODEL RESEARCH
• Find 3-5 people operating in your desired spaces
• Study their career paths in detail
• Note what spaces they operate in
• Identify how they monetize different ventures
• Look for patterns you can adapt

Financial Planning Exercises:

□ SET FINANCIAL TARGETS
• Calculate monthly expenses: $ _______
• Add 20% cushion: $ _______
• Determine yearly target: $ _______
• Set savings goal before making leap: $ _______
• Write these figures where you'll see them daily

□ MAP REVENUE STREAMS
• Return to mind map and add financial figures to each bucket
• Research typical earnings in each space
• Have conversations with people in those fields
• Identify which bucket will pay bills
• Determine which funds passion projects
• Calculate if you can match/exceed current income

□ SIDE HUSTLE TESTING
• List 3 ways to test your ideas while employed
• Book first freelance gig in your desired space
• Track time required vs. income generated
• Assess if lifestyle feels sustainable
• Build confidence with low-stakes opportunities

Network Building Exercises:

□ APPOINT YOUR BOARD OF ADVISORS
• List 5-10 people to serve as informal advisors
• Note where each person sits in your life (creative feedback, business strategy, emotional support, etc.)
• Identify gaps—where do you need different perspectives?
• Make plan to tell them they're on your board (when appropriate)
• Schedule quarterly check-ins with key advisors

□ MENTOR CULTIVATION
• Make list of 3 people you want to learn from
• Research each one thoroughly
• Prepare specific asks (not vague "pick your brain")
• Request 15 minutes of their time
• Come with clear questions about specific challenges

□ BUILD PEER NETWORK
• Identify 5 peers in similar situations
• Set up monthly peer mentorship calls
• Create accountability partnerships
• Share resources and opportunities
• Celebrate wins together

Team Building Exercises:

□ DEFINE COMPANY CULTURE
• Write down your core values (top 5)
• List your non-negotiables (no assholes, no yes-men, etc.)
• Describe ideal energy for your workspace
• Identify deal breakers
• Create your "company culture" document before any hires

□ NEEDS ASSESSMENT
• List everything you currently do
• Mark which tasks are in your zone of genius (keep these)
• Identify what drains your energy (delegate these)
• Note where you lack expertise (hire for these)
• Highlight blind spots in perspective (intentionally hire different backgrounds)

□ JOB DESCRIPTIONS
• Write detailed description for each future role
• Include specific responsibilities
• Note how this role generates revenue or saves time
• Define success metrics
• Clarify reporting structure and expectations

Self-Care & Balance Exercises:

□ CREATE JOY CONTAINERS
• Choose frequency: daily or weekly
• Block time on your actual calendar
• Treat it like any other meeting—don't skip
• List 5-10 activities that bring you genuine joy
• When calendar alert goes off, do one immediately

□ DEVELOP SELF-CARE STRATEGY
• Choose ONE practice you can commit to
• Options: meditation, exercise, therapy, devotional reading, etc.
• Write it on sticky note and place where you'll see it
• Put it in your calendar
• Start with just 10-15 minutes if that's sustainable

□ IDENTIFY YOUR "HELL YES" CRITERIA
• What energizes vs. depletes you?
• What feels fulfilling vs. just financially viable?
• Write specific criteria for saying yes to projects
• Post where you'll see when considering opportunities
• Commit to only "Hell Yes" or "No"—nothing in between

Writing & Communication Exercises:

□ EMAIL STYLE STUDY
• Read 10 emails from your manager
• Note their greeting style, tone, length, sign-off
• Practice matching their format
• Write 5 practice professional emails
• Get feedback on tone and clarity

□ OVERCOME WRITER'S BLOCK
• Open email program (not Word doc)
• Start writing as if to a friend
• Don't worry about perfection
• Transfer to proper format once flowing
• Practice this technique 3 times

□ FIND YOUR AUTHENTIC VOICE
• Write something, then say it out loud
• Write exactly as you spoke it
• Compare written vs. spoken version
• Keep the more natural-sounding one
• Practice with 5 different pieces

Presentation & Marketing Exercises:

□ DEVELOP SIGNATURE STYLE
• Create mood board of looks you're drawn to
• Identify 3-5 signature staples
• Try new silhouettes outside comfort zone
• Take photos and assess what feels authentic
• Commit to your signature elements

□ BUILD SOCIAL MEDIA WORKFLOW
• Choose grid planner app
• Plan one week of content in advance
• Set consistent posting time
• Create shared album for Instagram-worthy photos
• Link IG to Twitter/Facebook for auto-publishing

□ SHOW YOUR PROCESS
• Set up phone to record yourself working
• Create 60-second hyperlapse of creative process
• Share behind-the-scenes regularly
• Let audience into your journey
• Notice what they connect with most

Controversy Preparation:

□ PREPARE YOUR MANTRA
• Write counter-narrative to "I'm not good enough"
• Create response to "My work isn't worthy"
• Develop answer to "My voice doesn't matter"
• Practice saying these out loud
• Post where you'll see them when self-doubt hits

□ POSITIVE AFFIRMATIONS
• Write 5-10 positive affirmations about your work and worth
• Put on sticky notes
• Place on mirror, desk, phone lock screen
• Reference daily, especially before big moments
• Update as you grow and change

□ VISUALIZATION PRACTICE
• Identify 3 intimidating scenarios you might face
• Visualize how you want to feel in each
• Practice what you want to convey about yourself
• Role play conversations in your mind
• Build muscle memory before real moments

Decision-Making Exercises:

□ THE BIG QUESTION
• Ask yourself: "Have I reached the point of diminishing returns?"
• List what you're still learning in current role
• Identify skills you're still honing
• Note if you've hit your financial goal
• Write down potential regrets if you left today
• Review answers and reassess quarterly

□ PROS & CONS ANALYSIS
• Make detailed list of reasons to stay
• Make detailed list of reasons to leave
• Look for patterns in your reasoning
• Identify which list feels heavier emotionally
• Sleep on it, then revisit with fresh eyes
• Discuss with board of advisors

Ongoing Practices:

□ QUARTERLY REVIEWS
• Review and update mind map
• Assess progress toward financial goals
• Check in with board of advisors
• Evaluate if still operating in zone of genius
• Adjust strategy as needed
• Celebrate wins and learn from setbacks

□ MONTHLY SKILL DEVELOPMENT
• Identify one skill to improve this month
• Take course, read book, or practice deliberately
• Apply learning to current work
• Add to your skills inventory
• Share what you learned with peers

□ WEEKLY REFLECTION
• What energized you this week?
• What drained you?
• What opportunities arose?
• What did you learn?
• What will you do differently next week?

□ DAILY PRACTICES
• Morning: Set intention, review goals
• Throughout day: Notice when in/out of zone of genius
• Evening: Reflect on wins and lessons
• Track time spent on different activities
• Adjust schedule to maximize zone of genius time

🎯 KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. YOUR ZONE OF GENIUS IS YOUR COMPASS
Find the intersection of your passions, talents, values, and skills—this becomes your filter for all decisions and helps you spend more time doing what only you can do.

2. CREATE BOTH EAGLE EYE AND HAWKEYE VIEWS
Blueprint (30,000 feet) gives you long-term direction; Mind Map (granular) shows specific steps and revenue streams. Update both regularly during transitions.

3. FINANCIAL CONFIDENCE, NOT SECURITY, ENABLES DREAMS
Set specific savings targets and map revenue streams. Test side hustles while employed. Think abundance, not scarcity—every hire should generate revenue.

4. YOUR NETWORK IS YOUR SAFETY NET
Appoint 5-10 board of advisors intentionally. Seek peer mentorship alongside traditional mentors. Give without expectation. Lift as you rise.

5. HELL YES OR NO—NOTHING IN BETWEEN
Only take projects that energize you, align with your values, and move you toward your vision. Protect your energy and prevent burnout.

6. SCHEDULE JOY LIKE ANY OTHER MEETING
Make appointments with joy on your calendar. Show up for them like you would for clients. This prevents burnout and restores creative flow.

7. FOCUS ON IMPACT, NOT INTENTION, WHEN CALLED OUT
Pause before defending yourself. Acknowledge harm first. Let people feel heard. Then you can explain and course-correct.

8. YOUR DIFFERENCE IS YOUR SUPERPOWER
FODs (First, Only, Different) bring essential perspectives. Imposter syndrome is a symptom of systemic oppression, not truth. Create counter-narratives.

9. HIRE FOR BLIND SPOTS, NOT CULTURE FIT
Identify where you lack knowledge or perspective. Delegate everything outside your zone of genius. Fire faster when someone's not working out.

10. AUTHENTICITY CONNECTS MORE THAN PERFECTION
Show your process. Let audiences in. Say things out loud, then write exactly as you spoke. Your first thought is often most authentic.

11. SOCIAL MEDIA IS YOUR STOREFRONT WINDOW
Refresh it daily even while creating in the back. Build workflows, plan ahead, post consistently. But use it for authentic expression, not just selling.

12. DISRUPT TRADITIONAL FORMULAS WITH INTENTION
Learn how things are typically done, then rewrite the rules. Infuse everything with your WHY. Make it interactive, noisy, and resonant.

13. ASK "HAVE I REACHED THE POINT OF DIMINISHING RETURNS?"
Before leaving any job, assess honestly what you're still learning and getting. Don't make rash decisions from emotion, but trust when you know.

14. PREPARATION MEETS OPPORTUNITY
Visualize intimidating moments. Practice what you want to convey. Build muscle memory. When opportunity comes, seize it despite fear.

15. YOU ARE THE BOSS OF YOUR OWN LIFE
Design a career as unique as your fingerprint. You're in the driver's seat. You have permission to reinvent yourself repeatedly. The universe is calling you to be braver.
