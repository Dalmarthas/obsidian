## 📝 Executive Summary

The interview traces Jesse Cole’s journey from a lonely, baseball-obsessed kid to creating the Savannah Bananas, a fan-first entertainment brand that blends sport, circus, and Disney-style experience design. It shows how early family trauma, his father’s radical optimism, and years in obscure minor-league roles shaped both his business philosophy (bet everything on fans, long-term over short-term) and his personal commitment to foster care, adoption, and hands-on parenting.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

## 🎯 Key Takeaways

- **Fan-first, long-term thinking:** Cole consistently sacrifices short-term profit (ads, fees, big rights deals) to protect fan experience and build deep loyalty, which he considers the real asset.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- **Reframing failure and constraints:** A career-ending injury, a bankrupt team, and sleeping on an air mattress become forcing functions that drove creative risk-taking and unusual education (self-designed leadership major, circus/Disney inspiration).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- **Attention over traditional marketing:** He treats attention as a strategic asset, engineering stunts, rule changes, and show formats so they inherently generate stories and word-of-mouth instead of relying on ad spend.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- **Relentless experimentation culture:** The organization runs like SNL and Grateful Dead combined—weekly idea labs, constant show rewrites, rule experiments, and post-game “LCP” reviews institutionalize innovation.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- **Personal mission around belonging and care:** Childhood loneliness, a non-nurturing mother, and his dad’s positivity directly inform his fear of irrelevance, his desire to “make people feel like they matter,” and his family’s deep engagement with foster care and adoption.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

## 💡 Core Concepts

## 1. Fan-First Philosophy and “Attention Plan”

Cole explicitly rejects a “marketing budget” in favor of an “attention plan,” arguing that conspicuous, story-worthy ideas beat incremental ad spend. Influenced by P.T. Barnum and Walt Disney, he designs promotions (dig-to-China night, mascot HGH joke, midnight games, salute to underwear, community giveback) to be inherently talkable and visually memorable. This extends to structural choices: no ads on outfield walls, no ticket fees, all-inclusive concessions, and free broadcasts on YouTube, even at significant revenue cost.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

His view: conventional revenue levers (sponsorship, rights, fees) often degrade fan experience, so he’d rather leave “millions on the table” than compromise the emotional relationship. He tracks success by number of fans served (live, merch, content) rather than valuation, treating each interaction as compounding brand equity.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

## 2. Banana Ball as Product Innovation

Banana Ball is positioned as a reengineered product, not just a themed baseball game. Cole systematically mapped “friction points” in traditional baseball—length, dead time, walks, disengaged crowds—and designed rules to eliminate or invert them: two-hour time limit, no stepping out of the box, walks turned into sprints, fans catching foul balls for outs, and a scoring system that makes every inning matter, especially the last.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

He iterates rules empirically—e.g., discovering fans leave around 9 p.m. by photographing grandstands every 30 minutes for a year, then implementing the two-hour cap. When early scoring formats made late innings meaningless, he added a twist where every run in the final inning counts as a point, based on live testing and confusion from staff, then corrected communication. This “product manager” approach to sport aims to create a predictable emotional curve (no dead time, constant stakes, participatory moments).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

## 3. Creative Operating System: Idea Muscle and SNL Model

Cole treats creativity as a trainable muscle: since 2018 he has written 10 themed ideas every morning (promotions, merch, venues, etc.), evolving from an “idea box” inspired by Bill Veeck to a disciplined daily practice. He cascades that behavior through his entertainment leadership, spending the first 100 days with his director of entertainment in daily idea drills.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

The weekly creative cadence mirrors Saturday Night Live:

- Tuesday: multiple idea meetings (whole creative team, players, entertainment, broadcast) generate 100+ new concepts.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- Midweek: scripts, props, and sets are prepared.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- Late week: rehearsals, including test-runs in front of “VIP” fans to gauge reactions.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    

Everything is subject to the “LCP” review after each game—Learn, Change, Plus—where they dissect pre-game, in-game, and post-game experiences and log incremental improvements. The goal is 1–2k new bits per season, with Grateful Dead as a model for never repeating the exact same show in one city.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

## 4. Leadership, Standards, and Energy Management

Cole is explicit about being a **high**-standards, sometimes “microleading” leader who nonetheless wants to be a partner rather than a “boss.” He rejects managerial language, insisting no one “works for” him; they “work with” him. He sets expectations through “Vision–Challenges–Expectations” one-pagers before hires and tries to “scare” candidates with the pace, feedback intensity, and lifestyle so self-selection screens for aligned personalities.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

His leadership toolkit includes:

- Radical clarity: e.g., asking “Are you proud of this?” before reviewing work; encouraging interns to correct his body language with fans.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- Standards as non-negotiables: dirty details (bad audio, misaligned tent) genuinely distress him because they betray the promised fan experience.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- Energy list: he tracks personal energy against three verbs—creating, sharing, growing—and aggressively delegates operations (the internal “Jesse Ops” joke) that drain him.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    

Emily acts as a moderating co-leader, translating his fast, blunt feedback into more digestible communication, managing logistics and risk (e.g., pushing back on unsafe stunts like players skydiving to their positions), and helping him consider how comments land on newer staff.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

## 5. Long-Term Vision: League, Venues, and Fan-Funded Growth

The strategic roadmap mirrors Disney’s progression (from shorts to Disneyland to Disney World and, aspirationally, a city): start with one team, refine the game, add teams, then expand infrastructure.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

- Teams: Bananas (2016), Party Animals (2020), Firefighters (2024), each built as an independent brand with its own fanbase and tours.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- League: rather than starting with a rights deal and 8–16 teams, Cole wants to prove demand for each team first, then design league structures that make every game matter (avoiding “meaningless mid-season” games).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- Venues: expansion from small parks to minor-league parks to major-league stadiums (Houston, Fenway, DC, Cleveland), then potentially large football stadiums and, eventually, a dedicated “Banana Land / Banana World” complex.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    

Crucially, they have taken no outside equity financing; growth is funded via retained earnings and fans. Cole is open to creative structures like Disney’s early TV deals but resists ceding control to sponsors or broadcasters that would compromise fan-first principles.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

## 6. Personal Narrative: Family, Trauma, and Belonging

Cole’s childhood is framed by divorce, a largely absent mother battling addiction, and a father who raised him alone while modeling extreme positivity and work ethic. His dad’s mantra “swing hard in case you hit it,” originally about baseball, becomes a life philosophy of taking big swings despite failure.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

Key psychological threads:

- Loneliness and embarrassment: he lied to his dad about having friends and felt inadequate compared to his charismatic father, fueling lifelong efforts to be around large crowds and “40,000 people at a stadium” as an antidote to childhood isolation.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- Misattributed blame: as a child he thought the divorce was his fault, deepening a need to make others proud and a fear of being unloved or irrelevant.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- Reframing loss: a career-ending shoulder injury, initially devastating and filmed for his own leadership documentary, is later reframed as “the best thing that ever happened” professionally because it forced him out of conventional baseball and into reinventing the game.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    

His father’s later cancer battle, during which he remained relentlessly upbeat on the phone, becomes Cole’s template for resilient optimism under genuine duress.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

## 7. Foster Care, Adoption, and “Bananas Foster”

Emily’s long-standing desire to adopt and awareness of approximately 400,000 children in the U.S. without permanent homes pushed the couple into fostering despite an already chaotic travel schedule. They took training, then welcomed Kenna (2.5 years old, non-verbal, visibly traumatized) and Addison (six days old, exposed in utero to multiple drugs).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

Foster care revealed systemic bureaucratic friction (paperwork, hearings, rules) that clash with entrepreneurial lifestyles, but a core belief—“everything is figureoutable”—kept them engaged. The pivotal moment was a Mother’s Day text from Addison’s biological mother, asking Emily to accept the “privilege of raising this little girl forever,” reframing adoption as a joint act of care rather than a zero-sum loss.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

They founded Bananas Foster, a nonprofit arm that:

- Honors foster families at each game with on-field recognition and standing ovations.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- Drives awareness and sign-ups; some nights see double-digit new foster family inquiries through their site.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    

Cole envisions, long-term, a “waitlist of families wanting to foster,” paralleling his once-unimaginable ticket waitlist.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

## 🔨 Actionable Tips

For your own practice (ASO, agency, or product/brand building), these are directly portable:

1. **Define friction and invert it.**
    
    - List the top 5 friction points in your user journey (onboarding, pricing, feature bloat, complexity).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
    - For each, ask “What is the radical opposite?” (e.g., long contracts → cancel-anytime; hidden fees → all-in pricing; silent onboarding → celebratory onboarding).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
2. **Replace “marketing plan” with “attention plan.”
    
    - Allocate 10–20% of your effort to 1–2 bold, talkable experiments per quarter (stunts, unexpected collaborations, contrarian content) instead of spreading small spend across many channels.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
    - Design each experiment to create a clear story headline people would retell without you.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
3. **Institutionalize idea-generation.**
    
    - Adopt a daily 10-idea routine with themed prompts tied to your domain (feature ideas, retention levers, content hooks, app store experiments).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
    - Run a weekly “SNL-style” creative session: idea pitch → script → test on a small audience → full deployment → post-mortem.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
4. **Codify standards and expectations upfront.**
    
    - Before hiring, write a one-page “Vision–Challenges–Expectations” document that spells out both excitement and constraints (pace, feedback, resource scarcity).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
    - In reviews, ask “Are you proud of this?” before giving feedback; this nudges ownership and internal quality control.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
5. **Personal energy mapping.**
    
    - Divide your work into 3–4 verbs (e.g., creating, analyzing, selling, supporting).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
    - Track a week of activities and mark energy gain/drain, then systematically delegate or batch low-energy tasks; protect at least 50–60% of your time for high-energy verbs.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
6. **Design for emotional moments and belonging.**
    
    - Identify 2–3 “yellow moments” in your product or service—ritualized, emotionally charged beats where everyone participates (e.g., launch milestones, user anniversaries, in-app celebrations, community calls).[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
    - Make these experiences highly scripted and sensory (what they see, hear, feel) and then iterate them with LCP-style feedback.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
7. **Align business with a real social mission.**
    
    - If you adopt a cause (like foster care), integrate it into the core product experience rather than treating it as side philanthropy—e.g., spotlight partners in-app, weave their stories into events, create rituals that honor them.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
    - Track a simple impact metric (e.g., sign-ups, introductions, funds raised) and communicate it as prominently as growth metrics.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)

## 🔍 Additional Insights

- **Ambition vs. safety:** Cole’s instinct is always to push scale (major leagues, football stadiums, Banana Land), but Emily and operations leaders act as brakes, forcing due diligence on logistics, safety, and whether fans in the “worst” seats still feel cared for. This duality is central to sustainable hypergrowth.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- **Scalability vs. uniqueness tension:** Traveling with 200+ people and designing non-repeatable shows is deliberately “unscalable,” but Cole believes that _because_ it is hard to copy, it creates defensible differentiation in a commodity entertainment space.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- **Irrelevance as existential threat:** His stated biggest fear is becoming irrelevant—losing the sense that what he does matters to people—more than losing money. That fear drives both constant reinvention and intense scrutiny of execution details.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
    
- **Ambiguity/Unclear risk areas:**
    
    - Exact financial health and leverage levels are not disclosed; the narrative emphasizes optimism and fan-funded growth but doesn’t quantify debt or margins.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
    - Long-term viability of rejecting most sponsorship/rights money at larger scales (NFL-sized venues, Banana Land) is aspirational and unproven.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
        
    - The foster system specifics (case histories, institutional practices) are deliberately high-level for privacy, so some causal inferences about early trauma and later behavior remain interpretive rather than fully evidenced.[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qdLjan3yfX8&list=WL&index=16)
