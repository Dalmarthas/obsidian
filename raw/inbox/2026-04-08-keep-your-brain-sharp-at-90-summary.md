Here's a summary of the video **"Do These 3 Things NOW to Keep Your Brain Sharp at 90"**:

1. **Aerobic Exercise**:
    - Regular aerobic exercise, even for older adults, can _not only prevent brain shrinkage_ (atrophy) but can actually help the hippocampus (critical for memory) grow by 1–2%. This was observed in a study where participants exercised at 70–75% max heart rate three times a week for 30 minutes. In contrast, those doing only stretching experienced expected shrinkage.
    - Cardiorespiratory fitness is linked to dramatically lower dementia risk (up to 80% reduction).
2. **Good Sleep**:
    - Deep sleep activates the brain’s glymphatic system, which clears waste, including amyloid plaques associated with Alzheimer's. Long-term poor sleep increases Alzheimer’s risk due to this waste not being properly cleared.
3. **Multivitamin Use**:
    - Recent large randomized controlled trials showed that taking a standard multivitamin (like Centrum Silver) for a few years improved cognition, processing speed, and episodic memory in older adults, effectively reducing cognitive aging by about 5 years. This approach is simple, evidence-based, and much easier than other interventions.

**Additional Insights**:

- Other lifestyle factors—like avoiding alcohol, not smoking, keeping a healthy weight, and avoiding traumatic brain injuries (especially if you have genetic risk factors like APOE4)—are important for reducing Alzheimer’s and dementia risk.
- The APOE4 gene variant significantly increases Alzheimer’s risk, but lifestyle choices can still make a big difference.
- Diet high in refined carbohydrates can disrupt glucose metabolism in the brain, contributing to Alzheimer’s risk.

**Bottom Line**:

To keep your brain sharp into old age:

- Stay physically active (aerobic exercise is especially powerful).
- Prioritize quality sleep.
- Take a daily multivitamin.
- Bonus: Avoid risk factors (alcohol, smoking, obesity, brain injury) and get genetic testing if you’re concerned about APOE4.

These habits can _prevent cognitive decline, reduce your risk of dementia, and even physically improve brain structure as you age_.
