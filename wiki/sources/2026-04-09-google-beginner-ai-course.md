# Summary

This source is a beginner-oriented map of the modern AI stack. Its main value is structural clarity: it explains how artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, generative AI, and large language models relate to each other, and it frames the major training distinctions in approachable language.

# Key Claims

- AI is the umbrella field; machine learning is a subdomain of AI; deep learning is a subdomain of machine learning.
- Generative AI and LLMs sit inside the deep-learning layer rather than outside the stack.
- Machine learning is best understood through how models learn from data, especially the distinction between supervised and unsupervised learning.
- Deep learning extends pattern-recognition capacity through neural-network architectures and can support semi-supervised use cases.
- LLMs are typically pre-trained broadly and then fine-tuned for narrower tasks or industries.

# Important Details

- The source uses intuitive analogies and simple examples to make the stack legible to non-technical audiences.
- It contrasts discriminative systems that classify inputs with generative systems that create new outputs.
- It treats fine-tuning as the bridge from general-purpose models to domain-specific applications in areas like healthcare, retail, and finance.
- It recommends pairing conceptual literacy with direct hands-on tool use.

# Tensions / Caveats

- This is a secondary summary of a beginner-friendly overview, not a technical primary source.
- The source prioritizes conceptual simplification over mathematical or engineering depth.
- Product examples in the raw text should be treated as illustrative rather than as a current market map.
