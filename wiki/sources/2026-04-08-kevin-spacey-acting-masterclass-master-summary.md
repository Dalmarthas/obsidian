# Summary

This source is a large secondary summary of an acting masterclass identified by the raw filename as Kevin Spacey's. It presents acting as truthful reaction under pressure, disciplined service to the writing, continuous craft practice, strong text analysis, and professional preparation for stage, screen, auditions, and career development. Raw source: [[raw/inbox/2026-04-08-kevin-spacey-acting-masterclass-master-summary|Raw source]].

# Key Claims

- Acting is fundamentally reacting rather than demonstrating emotion or technique for its own sake.
- The actor's job is to serve the writing rather than force the writing down to the actor's habits or ego.
- High stakes, variation, contradiction, and disappointment make performances feel alive.
- Strong performances often hold emotion under pressure instead of releasing everything for the audience.
- Text analysis matters at the word level: actors have to know what they are saying, why they are saying it, and what each shift is doing.
- Screen actors need to map arc and knowledge progression explicitly because scenes are shot out of order.
- Monologues, auditions, and careers improve when craft work continues outside moments of immediate opportunity.

# Important Details

- The source gives a broad acting model:
  - authenticity over affectation
  - reacting over `acting`
  - high stakes without necessarily high volume
  - multiple shifts instead of one emotional note
  - serving the text instead of serving vanity
- It also offers practical training methods:
  - carve out specific words
  - mark shift points
  - ground poetic material with tasks
  - try opposite interpretations
  - use masks and mirrors to break habitual choices
  - trust words before adding excessive physical business
- The screen and stage distinction is unusually concrete:
  - theater teaches arc through repetition and duration
  - screen work requires intellectual arc mapping
  - the actor must track what the character knows in each scene
  - shooting order can distort truth if arc work is weak
- The source is also rich on audition craft:
  - prepare multiple approaches
  - take direction fully
  - study the sides for story clues
  - ask to restart if a read goes badly
  - remember the room wants you to solve a problem
- Career strategy is a real secondary layer:
  - train consistently
  - avoid atrophied craft
  - build relationships directly
  - choose roles strategically
  - do not rush to be seen in work you are not ready to carry

# Tensions / Caveats

- This is a secondary summary rather than a primary transcript.
- The raw file contains encoding noise.
- The instructor is identified by the raw filename; the body of the summary mostly refers generically to `the instructor`.
- The source is strongest on dramatic realism, text-based acting, and professional discipline; actors working in highly stylized, comedic, or devised traditions may need different emphases.
- Career advice is more time-sensitive than the core craft advice.
