# Summary

This source is a secondary summary of sports-science work on the `quiet eye`: the final stable gaze fixation or tracking period before and during a key movement. Its strongest addition to the vault is a perceptual-performance model in which elite execution depends not just on physical repetition or raw eyesight, but on visual attention that stays steady long enough to support movement planning, last-moment correction, and calmer performance under pressure. Raw source: [[raw/inbox/2026-04-11-quiet-eye-visual-attention-and-skill-performance|Raw source]].

Note: the raw note contains unrelated appended material after the main quiet-eye section. This source page preserves only the quiet-eye / visual-attention contribution because the later pasted content overlaps with existing motivation and expertise branches.

# Key Claims

- Longer quiet-eye duration is consistently associated with better performance across multiple sports.
- Elite performance depends more on perceptual-cognitive integration than on eyesight quality alone.
- Quiet-eye behavior appears trainable and can improve performance in both novices and skilled performers.
- The mechanism remains debated: quiet eye may support pre-programming, online correction, or act as a marker of deeper expertise rather than a simple cause.
- Visual attention under pressure is part of skill, not just an accessory to skill.

# Important Details

- The source defines `quiet eye` as:
  - the final steady fixation or tracking gaze
  - occurring just before and during the critical phase of movement
  - longer in higher-skill performers across tasks like golf putting and basketball free throws
- The source pushes back on a common misconception:
  - experts do not simply look faster and shorter
  - in many cases they hold gaze longer and more stably
  - the performance edge seems to come from better coupling of perception with action
- The training implication is practical:
  - visual attention can be coached
  - excessive gaze variability and unstable head movement may degrade execution
  - realistic, variable practice conditions matter more than sterile repetition alone
- The mechanism remains unresolved:
  - quiet eye may help movement planning before action
  - it may support online micro-corrections during action
  - or it may indicate a broader integration skill rather than directly causing success
- The source's durable value is cross-domain:
  - pressure performance depends partly on where and how attention stabilizes
  - perception quality is not reducible to optical sharpness
  - expertise includes seeing the task environment in a more usable way

# Tensions / Caveats

- This is a secondary summary rather than a direct research paper or primary interview with the researchers.
- The source itself admits causal uncertainty: quiet eye may correlate with expertise without fully explaining it.
- The raw note includes unrelated appended material on motivation, habits, and expertise that appears to come from other sources; those sections are not treated as part of the canonical contribution here.
- Cross-domain transfer is plausible, but the current evidence in the note is strongest for sports and motor tasks rather than symbolic or abstract performance.
