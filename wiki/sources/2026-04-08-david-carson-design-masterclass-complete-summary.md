# Summary

This source is a large secondary summary of David Carson's masterclass on graphic design and typography. It presents design as emotional communication that works before literal reading, and argues for intuition, risk, rule-breaking with purpose, expressive typography, white space, physical collage, and visible humanity in the work. Raw source: [[raw/inbox/2026-04-08-david-carson-design-masterclass-complete-summary|Raw source]].

# Key Claims

- Design should intrigue and communicate before someone reads a word.
- Intuition is a real design tool and should not be replaced entirely by systems or grids.
- Great work usually requires risk and some willingness to alienate rather than universal approval.
- Typography is visual meaning, not just neutral text delivery.
- Legibility and readability are not the same thing; audience interest changes what can be read.
- Work feels stronger when a human being is perceptible behind it.

# Important Details

- The source repeatedly defines design as emotional communication:
  - before reading
  - through type
  - through space
  - through cropping, texture, and color
- Carson's core practical principles include:
  - trust intuition
  - avoid safe committee work
  - question literalism
  - choose either simple or fully complicated, not mushy middle ground
- The typography section is especially strong:
  - the word is an image
  - spacing and arrangement matter
  - hand lettering signals human presence
  - breaking typographic rules can strengthen meaning when done deliberately
- The source treats grids and guides as optional at best and deadening at worst:
  - start from a blank page
  - do not let software make placement decisions for you
- Photography and collage are also central:
  - crop unexpectedly
  - look for accidental or found images
  - work physically with paper and texture
  - let shadows and materiality create authentic depth
- The life philosophy underneath the craft is explicit:
  - take chances
  - avoid regret through action
  - put your own life and taste into the work

# Tensions / Caveats

- This is a secondary summary rather than a primary transcript.
- The source is overtly anti-safe and anti-system, so some advice may overshoot when translated into constrained design systems or accessibility-heavy contexts.
- Carson's philosophy is intentionally provocative and should not be flattened into a ban on all structure.
- The raw file contains encoding noise.
