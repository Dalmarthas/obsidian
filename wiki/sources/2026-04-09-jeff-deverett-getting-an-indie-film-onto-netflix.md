# Summary

This source adds a practical commercial layer that the current filmmaking branch mostly lacked. Jeff Deverett frames getting an indie film onto Netflix not as a pure merit contest, but as a chain of compressed pitching, approved intermediaries, technical delivery compliance, credibility, and persistence. Its strongest durable contribution is the rule that finishing a film is not the same thing as getting it commercially delivered.

# Key Claims

- Streamer-facing pitches are judged quickly, so short high-signal presentations outperform long windups.
- Independent filmmakers often still need approved distributors or delivery intermediaries even when a buyer is interested.
- Delivery requirements can be technically complex and expensive enough to materially change the economics of a deal.
- Track record, on-time delivery, and prior performance matter more than enthusiasm when buyers consider repeat business.
- First-time filmmakers often need established partners to clear credibility and access barriers.
- Persistence and professional follow-up are structural parts of the process rather than optional personality traits.

# Important Details

- Deverett's own lesson is to pitch in roughly two to three minutes, with the core hook front-loaded.
- Supporting materials such as key art, a trailer, and a tight logline help the project feel real faster.
- Netflix is described as professional and responsive, but still operationally prefers vetted distributors and delivery partners for indie titles.
- Delivery includes subtitles, multiple language assets, and other compliance work that can exceed a newcomer's expectations and budget.
- Industry networking is framed as partly chemistry and timing, not just transaction or hustle.
- Confidential platform metrics influence whether a producer gets future opportunities.

# Tensions / Caveats

- This is a secondary summary rather than a direct interview transcript or a formal distribution handbook.
- The source is strongly Netflix-facing, so some claims may not transfer cleanly to every buyer, platform, or release path.
