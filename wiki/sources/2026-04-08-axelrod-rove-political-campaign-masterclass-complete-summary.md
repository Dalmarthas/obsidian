# Summary

This source is a large secondary summary of a political campaign masterclass taught by David Axelrod and Karl Rove. It presents campaigning as a disciplined system built on authentic purpose, clear framing, research, targeting, message repetition, organized field operations, and relentless execution across media and time. Raw source: [[raw/inbox/2026-04-08-axelrod-rove-political-campaign-masterclass-complete-summary|Raw source]].

# Key Claims

- Every campaign begins with the candidate answering `Why are you running?` in terms of what they want to do, not what they want to be.
- The campaign that defines the choice for voters usually wins.
- Message quality depends on authenticity, relevance, connection, and contrast.
- Serious self-research and opposition research are mandatory because surprise vulnerabilities are campaign-killing.
- Resources should focus on target voters rather than the whole electorate.
- Strong campaigns are planned, monitored, and adjusted rather than improvised day by day.
- Ground game, media, fundraising, and timing only work when they all serve the same strategic premise.

# Important Details

- The planning stack is unusually explicit:
  - define the campaign's purpose
  - map target voters
  - write a plan
  - spread budget over time
  - align fundraising with actual needs
  - monitor progress continuously
- The research layer is deep and two-sided:
  - biography and vulnerabilities of your own candidate
  - front-page test before entering
  - full public-record review of the opponent
  - self-opposition research before the other side does it
  - attack only when fair, relevant, true, and well-documented
- The targeting layer is also operational:
  - base voters
  - opponent base
  - overlap zone of swing and weakly attached voters
  - turnout scores
  - support scores
  - issue-priority segmentation
  - microtargeting without becoming too narrow
- The field layer gives a full four-phase model:
  - early volunteer recruitment
  - persuasion contact
  - mixed persuasion and mobilization
  - final pure GOTV
- The communication layer is highly reusable:
  - stump speech as repeated core argument
  - stay on message under pressure
  - everyone is a reporter
  - debates require preparation and message discipline
  - slogans and logos should express the irreducible core of the campaign
- The source also includes crisis and brand lessons:
  - do not let the opponent pull you off your brand
  - major crises test whether the candidate can say what needs to be said under pressure
  - campaigns reveal character rather than hiding it

# Tensions / Caveats

- This is a secondary summary rather than a primary transcript.
- The file contains encoding noise.
- Some lesson content is missing, especially around polling and parts of fundraising.
- The source is rich in campaign operations but thinner on the ethical limits of persuasion, attack, and data use than the tactical detail might suggest.
- Because the instructors come from high-level U.S. national politics, some advice may not transfer cleanly to non-U.S. or low-resource campaigns.
