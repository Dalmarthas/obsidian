# Summary

This source is a tighter derivative summary of Matt Abrahams's spontaneous-speaking method. It overlaps strongly with the existing Abrahams branch, but it adds a compact `3S` anxiety protocol, a cleaner anti-perfectionism metaphor of offering `bricks` instead of `cathedrals`, stronger anti-filler guidance through `stick your landings`, and a few extra structure examples such as `IRAC` and the Pixar story spine. Raw source: [[raw/inbox/2026-04-13-think-faster-talk-smarter-by-matt-abrahams-core-message|Raw source]].

# Key Claims

- Impromptu speaking improves when anxiety regulation, permissive mindset, and lightweight structure are combined rather than handled separately.
- The `3S` plan offers a more memorable anxiety toolkit: separate from anxiety, slow your exhales, and stick your landings.
- `Dare to be dull` works partly by lowering the felt obligation to produce brilliance and instead contributing one useful `brick`.
- Repeatable structures make spontaneous speech sound smarter because they lower cognitive load.
- Daily rehearsal of one simple structure can train clearer speaking in ordinary meetings and Q and A.

# Important Details

- The source packages regulation into a compact `3S` model:
  - `separate` from anxiety by treating it as a state rather than an identity
  - `slow` your exhales to regulate pace and nervous-system activation
  - `stick your landings` by finishing sentences cleanly, pausing, and avoiding filler at line endings
- It adds a cleaner anti-filler and silence rule:
  - silence after a completed point can signal control rather than failure
  - breathing rhythm can be used to make filler less likely
- The source sharpens anti-perfectionism with a stronger image:
  - conversations usually need solid `bricks`, not architectural masterpieces
  - useful, ordinary contributions often serve the room better than strained originality
- The structure layer widens the branch slightly:
  - `What-So what-Now what` remains the default
  - `Problem-Benefit-Solution` is emphasized as a persuasive variant
  - `IRAC` appears as a more analytical legal structure
  - the Pixar spine appears as a narrative structure for story-shaped answers
- The source adds a practical training loop:
  - after consuming content, summarize it using `What-So what-Now what`
  - repeated low-stakes use turns structure into a reflex under pressure

# Tensions / Caveats

- This is a compressed derivative of material already represented in the vault, so much of its value is in packaging and memorability rather than wholly new ideas.
- The source adds memorable labels, but not much new evidence for why the techniques work.
- The `brick not cathedral` framing is useful for ordinary meetings, but some speaking contexts still demand more polish than this metaphor implies.
- The structure examples are helpful, though the branch still needs clearer guidance on when one structure fits better than another.
