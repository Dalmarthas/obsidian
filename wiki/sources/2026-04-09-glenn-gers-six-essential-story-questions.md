# Summary

This source adds a pragmatic, question-driven storytelling layer to the filmmaking branch. Glenn Gers argues that stories can be understood and built through six core questions that work across scales, from full narratives down to scenes. Its strongest new vault contribution is not a strict structural formula but a durable interrogation method: story quality improves when the writer keeps asking who the story is about, what the character wants, what blocks them, what they do, why it fails, and how the story resolves.

# Key Claims

- The six essential questions form a portable backbone for narrative design.
- Story is fundamentally about a character pursuing something through obstacles.
- Obstacles may be internal or external, but without them story force collapses.
- Every character can be treated as the protagonist of their own story, which increases depth and plausibility.
- Writing process is personal, so craft should be adapted to the writer's real rhythms rather than forced into one universal method.
- Iteration, questioning, and rough placeholders are often more productive than waiting for perfect execution.

# Important Details

- The six questions are: who is it about, what do they want, why can't they get it, what do they do about that, why doesn't that work, and how does it end.
- Gers presents these questions as usable across story scales, not only at feature level.
- The `theory of mind` layer sharpens character writing by assuming that even minor characters are pursuing their own objectives.
- Obstacles are framed as both physical and psychological, which helps connect plot pressure with character interiority.
- The process advice is unusually anti-dogmatic: what matters is learning the conditions that actually let you work.
- Placeholder scene notes are treated as legitimate progress because they preserve momentum and create revision material.

# Tensions / Caveats

- This is a secondary summary rather than a primary transcript.
- The source is strong as a diagnostic and development method, but less distinct on advanced style, tone, or medium-specific form.
- Some of its questions overlap with existing vault pages, so its value lies partly in sharpening and organizing current knowledge rather than introducing wholly new doctrine.
- Its process advice is intentionally flexible, which is useful, but also less prescriptive than writers who want a tightly engineered workflow may prefer.
