# Summary

This is an English secondary summary of an unnamed video organized around three practical moves for preserving brain function into old age: aerobic exercise, good sleep, and daily multivitamin use. In this vault, the source is strongest as a brain-health and cognitive-aging branch that ties hippocampal protection, glymphatic cleanup during deep sleep, dementia-risk reduction, and a simple older-adult cognition protocol into one compact preventive model. Raw source: [[raw/inbox/2026-04-08-keep-your-brain-sharp-at-90-summary|Raw source]].

# Key Claims

- Regular aerobic exercise is presented as one of the strongest protective moves for brain health because it can reduce shrinkage and even improve hippocampal volume.
- Deep sleep matters for long-term cognition because the glymphatic system clears waste products, including amyloid linked to Alzheimer's disease.
- A standard daily multivitamin is presented as an evidence-based cognitive support with randomized-trial support for processing speed, memory, and reduced apparent cognitive aging.
- Dementia risk is also shaped by broader lifestyle variables such as alcohol use, smoking, healthy weight, refined-carbohydrate intake, brain-injury avoidance, and sleep quality.
- Genetic risk such as `APOE4` matters, but the source still frames lifestyle as materially meaningful rather than irrelevant.

# Important Details

- The branch revolves around four reusable layers:
  - aerobic exercise and brain structure
  - deep sleep and glymphatic clearance
  - multivitamins and cognition
  - broader risk reduction for dementia and cognitive aging
- The most concrete exercise detail in the source is:
  - around `30` minutes of aerobic work
  - `3` times per week
  - at roughly `70-75%` max heart rate
- The sleep layer is tightly linked to long-run brain health:
  - deep sleep supports waste clearance
  - poor long-term sleep is framed as increasing Alzheimer's risk
- The multivitamin claim is unusually direct:
  - a standard older-adult multivitamin is described as improving cognition and reducing effective cognitive aging by roughly `5` years
- The source also includes practical risk modifiers:
  - avoid alcohol and smoking
  - maintain a healthy weight
  - avoid traumatic brain injuries
  - be careful with refined-carbohydrate-heavy diets that worsen brain glucose metabolism

# Tensions / Caveats

- This is a secondary summary, not a primary transcript or a bundle of the underlying studies.
- The video speaker is not identified in the raw source, so the branch is intentionally source-centered rather than person-centered.
- The multivitamin claim is one of the most surprising parts of the source and likely needs more replication or direct paper review before being treated as high-certainty doctrine.
- The source is strongest as a practical prevention branch, but it simplifies a large topic and does not fully separate what is robustly causal from what is strongly correlated.
