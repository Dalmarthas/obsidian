# Summary

This source is a broad diagnostic summary of recurring screenwriting mistakes rather than a single named-author method. Its strongest value is not one new theory of story. It offers a practical error map: weak scripts commonly fail because the protagonist's external goal is blurry, the concept is impersonal or soft, setup and genre promises are mishandled, stakes and surprise do not escalate, characters are too thin or invulnerable, and the writer mistakes first-draft effort for professional readiness.

# Key Claims

- Clear external goals are essential because audience investment weakens quickly when the protagonist's visible aim is unclear.
- Scripts often fail upstream when the concept is generic, emotionally unowned, or mismatched to the chosen genre.
- Setup, character development, and genre expectations cannot be rushed without damaging story coherence.
- Rising stakes, conflict, surprise, and growth points are required to prevent flat or meandering narrative movement.
- Professional screenwriting requires multiple drafts, note-taking, self-honesty, and resisting the urge to circulate immature work.

# Important Details

- The source treats `personal connection to the concept` as a major quality filter:
  market-awareness matters, but scripts feel dead when the writer has no real emotional or experiential stake in the material.
- It treats flaws, vulnerability, and emotional honesty as central to memorable characters.
- It warns against soft concepts, weak setup, wrong-genre choices, passive character design, shallow stakes, and superficial emotional beats.
- It frames rewriting as targeted diagnosis rather than cosmetic cleanup:
  the writer should actively check goal clarity, stakes, surprise, growth, and scene-level engagement after each draft.
- It also adds a professionalism layer:
  send out only work that is clean, complete, and respectful of other people's time.

# Tensions / Caveats

- This is a generalized secondary summary and does not preserve the original 29 mistakes as a formally numbered canonical list.
- Because the source is broad and diagnostic, some advice is compressed and may overlap heavily with other existing vault concepts rather than introducing totally new categories.
- The strongest value of the source is as a reusable checklist layer, not as a singular worldview or auteur framework.
