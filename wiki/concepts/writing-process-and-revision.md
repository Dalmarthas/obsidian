# What It Is

Writing process and revision is the larger workflow that governs how a writer prepares, drafts, evaluates, and reshapes a work over time. In the current source base, the strongest pattern is not mystical inspiration but a managed cycle of idea capture, structural development, drafting, diagnosis, cutting, and recommitment to the core engine of the work. Main sources: [[wiki/sources/2010-01-08-5-simple-rules-for-turning-your-cool-idea-into-a-screenplay|5 Simple Rules For Turning Your Cool Idea Into A Screenplay]], [[wiki/sources/2026-04-07-aaron-sorkin-masterclass-complete-summary|Aaron Sorkin Masterclass Complete Summary]], [[wiki/sources/2026-04-07-dan-brown-masterclass-complete-summary|Dan Brown Masterclass Complete Summary]], [[wiki/sources/2026-04-08-duffer-brothers-masterclass-complete-summary|Duffer Brothers Masterclass Complete Summary]], [[wiki/sources/2026-04-08-david-lynch-writing-summary|David Lynch Writing Summary]], and [[wiki/sources/2026-04-08-david-baldacci-masterclass-summary|David Baldacci Masterclass Summary]].

# Why It Matters

- It determines whether craft knowledge becomes repeatable work rather than occasional luck.
- It connects pre-draft planning, draft generation, and revision into one system.
- It gives the vault a cross-source concept for process discipline rather than leaving that knowledge split across source-local pages.
- It helps separate source-specific habits from transferable process principles.
- It now captures the full arc from fragile idea to finished narrative instead of only the drafting and rewriting stages.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The 2010 screenplay article emphasizes outlining before drafting and learning formatting early to avoid preventable cleanup later.
- Sorkin emphasizes finishing the draft before trying to perfect it and then rewriting diagnostically against concrete problems.
- Brown emphasizes protecting the process through routine, draft-stage separation, placeholder use, and permission to write badly before distilling.
- The Duffer Brothers emphasize refusing to draft too early, moving through increasingly specific outline layers, and backing out to the outline when pages break.
- Lynch emphasizes receptivity, daydreaming, and preserving psychic room for ideas before forcing them into rigid form.
- Baldacci emphasizes the writer's prism, flexible but serious discipline, immersive research, and editorial collaboration that sharpens plausibility and pace.
- Both Sorkin and Brown treat deletion and cutting as central rather than incidental to good writing.
- Across the sources, preparation is important, but so is avoiding paralysis or perfectionism before there is enough material to shape.

# Source Comparisons

- The 2010 screenplay article is strongest on pre-draft discipline: outline, structure, and format first.
- Sorkin is strongest on post-draft discipline: finish the block of marble, then cut and diagnose.
- Brown covers the whole arc most explicitly: process protection, side-by-side drafting modes, and later distillation.
- The Duffer Brothers are strongest on layered development before pages exist: big idea, paragraph test, blue-sky ideation, and recursive outlines.
- Lynch is strongest on creative conditions: protect daydreaming, catch ideas immediately, and keep negativity from shrinking the signal.
- Baldacci is strongest on how working writers sustain output over long books: observation as lifestyle, practical research, and useful editor relationships.
- Together they suggest a layered workflow: catch a live idea, test whether it can carry real weight, develop structure before expensive pages, draft far enough to have something real, and then revise without sentimentality.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Too much planning can become avoidance; too little planning can create expensive chaos later.
- Some writers edit while drafting more than these sources recommend, so the right balance may vary by temperament and medium.
- Process discipline is transferable, but exact rituals are probably not.
- The main live tension is between receptivity and control: the vault now contains both outline-heavy and intuition-heavy models, and the right blend likely depends on medium and maker.
