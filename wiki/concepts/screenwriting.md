# What It Is

Screenwriting is the practice of writing a production blueprint for a visual medium. In this vault's current evidence, the screenplay is defined less as literary prose and more as a structured set of directions that can support actors, directors, and crew during the making of a film. Source: [[wiki/sources/2010-01-08-5-simple-rules-for-turning-your-cool-idea-into-a-screenplay|5 Simple Rules For Turning Your Cool Idea Into A Screenplay]].

# Why It Matters

- The medium places pressure on visual clarity, story economy, and scene-level functionality.
- Up-front preparation affects whether setups, payoffs, and character turns can land cleanly.
- Format matters because a screenplay is used operationally, not just interpretively.
