# What It Is

Mirroring is the practice of repeating a small fragment of the other person's words so they keep talking, clarify themselves, or hear their own position more fully. In this vault, it appears most clearly in the Chris Voss branch as a lightweight verbal move that gathers information without launching into interrogation.

# Why It Matters

- It encourages elaboration without direct argument.
- It buys time when you need to think under pressure.
- It lowers the odds that the other person feels challenged too early.
- It gives the vault a concrete micro-skill under tactical empathy rather than leaving empathy abstract.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The more detailed workplace Voss source teaches a simple pattern: repeat the last one to three words, then use silence.
- That same source also says better practitioners stop mirroring only the final words and start selecting implication-heavy words worth exploring.
- The earlier Voss source already identified mirroring as a core information-gathering tool rather than conversational filler.
- Across both Voss sources, mirroring works best when it is paired with calm tone and immediate silence.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Overuse can sound mechanical, manipulative, or socially strange.
- Bad tone can make the mirror sound mocking rather than attentive.
- Mirroring alone is rarely enough; it usually has to feed into labels, summaries, or better questions.
