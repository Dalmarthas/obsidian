# What It Is

AI roleplay simulation is the use of AI to model a counterpart, run a simulated interaction, and evaluate the transcript so a user can rehearse a difficult conversation before the real one.

# Why It Matters

It turns AI from a drafting tool into a practice environment. Instead of only asking for advice about a conversation, the user can experience likely friction, test responses, and refine talking points under lower stakes.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-5-step-playbook-for-10x-your-ai-productivity]] describes a three-stage simulation loop: profile the other person, roleplay the conversation, and then get structured feedback on the transcript.
- The source shows that realism can be tuned by asking the simulated counterpart for more edge, confrontation, or specificity.
- It also implies a multi-agent pattern: one model or chat can profile, another can play the counterpart, and another can evaluate the result.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/thinking-on-your-feet|Thinking on Your Feet]], AI roleplay simulation is a training environment rather than the live communication skill itself.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/frame-control|Frame Control]], roleplay simulation can be used to practice staying composed when the other side applies pressure, bait, or emotional escalation.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/human-machine-collaboration|Human-Machine Collaboration]], this is a specific workflow pattern inside the broader collaboration category.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Simulated people can become caricatures if the profile is thin or biased.
- A roleplay may create false confidence if it trains against a simplified version of the real conversation.
- The strongest use may be rehearsal of structure, timing, and composure rather than prediction of exactly what the other person will say.
