# What It Is

Artificial intelligence is the umbrella field concerned with building systems that perform tasks associated with human intelligence.

# Why It Matters

This is the top-level category that keeps the rest of the stack legible. Without this umbrella framing, people often confuse AI with one narrow technique such as LLMs or generative image tools.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-google-beginner-ai-course]] presents AI as the outermost layer of the stack, with machine learning nested beneath it and deeper specializations further down.
- The source frames AI as a broad field of study and system design rather than as one specific model family.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-11-yuval-noah-harari-21-lessons-for-the-21st-century]] adds a governance-oriented frame: AI matters not only as a technical field but as an infrastructure that can reshape autonomy, politics, and social coordination when fused with biological and behavioral data.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The branch still needs richer sources that distinguish symbolic AI, classical AI approaches, and modern statistical AI.
- Future sources may justify a more explicit separation between "AI as a field" and "AI as a product category."
- The newer Harari source adds a different tension: some of the most important AI questions may be less about model taxonomy and more about institutions, data asymmetry, and the political conditions under which AI systems are deployed.
