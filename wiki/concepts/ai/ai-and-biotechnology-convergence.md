# What It Is

AI and biotechnology convergence is the idea that the most consequential digital disruption comes from combining computational intelligence with detailed access to human bodies, emotions, and behavior. In the current vault, the concern is not merely smarter software. It is the prospect that outside systems may model, predict, and influence people at levels that outstrip ordinary self-knowledge.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the AI branch a governance problem much deeper than automation alone.
- It reframes the central risk from `machines replacing tasks` to `systems steering choices`.
- It makes self-knowledge, privacy, and institutional accountability part of the AI conversation rather than optional adjacent topics.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-11-yuval-noah-harari-21-lessons-for-the-21st-century]] argues that AI becomes politically and socially transformative when fused with biological and behavioral data.
- The same source claims that this convergence threatens liberal assumptions about autonomy because outside systems may infer desires or vulnerabilities better than people can themselves.
- It therefore treats emotional intelligence and self-awareness as defensive capacities in a manipulated environment, not just as wellness practices.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/artificial-intelligence|Artificial Intelligence]], this concept is narrower and more political. The umbrella AI page defines the field; this page names one especially consequential direction of development.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/human-machine-collaboration|Human-Machine Collaboration]], convergence is less about augmenting judgment and more about the possibility of bypassing or steering it.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/shared-fictions|Shared Fictions]], this concept is more technical and infrastructural, while shared fictions explains the narrative layer through which institutions justify and deploy such power.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The framing is powerful, but the branch still needs stronger material on concrete mechanisms, timelines, and institutional variation.
- `Systems know you better than you know yourself` can become rhetorical overreach if not tied to real capabilities and limits.
- The vault still needs future sources on privacy, AI safety, and democratic governance to keep the concept from remaining purely diagnostic.
