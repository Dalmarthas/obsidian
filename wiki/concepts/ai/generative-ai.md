# What It Is

Generative AI is the part of the modern model landscape focused on producing new outputs such as text, images, audio, video, or other artifacts rather than only classifying or scoring inputs.

# Why It Matters

This is the part of the AI stack that most visibly changed public perception of AI. It is also the key distinction that separates content-producing systems from purely discriminative ones.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-google-beginner-ai-course]] defines generative AI through output creation and contrasts it with discriminative models that only classify or predict.
- The source uses text, image, video, and other media examples to make the category practical and concrete for beginners.
- It also places generative AI inside the deep-learning layer rather than treating it as a separate outer category.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-23-how-large-language-models-work]] adds a useful internal distinction: some generative systems are best understood as foundation models first and application tools second, with LLMs as the language-focused branch of that broader family.
- The same source also adds a more practical business-use framing by connecting generative text models to support automation, drafting, and code assistance.
