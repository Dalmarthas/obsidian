# What It Is

OKRs, short for objectives and key results, are a goal-setting and review system that pairs one ambitious objective with a small set of measurable results used to judge progress. In the current vault, the concept matters as a structured bridge between aspiration and execution: it turns important goals into a visible operating rhythm rather than leaving them as vague intent. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-measure-what-matters-by-john-doerr|Measure What Matters by John Doerr]].

# Why It Matters

- It gives the execution branch a stronger model for turning ambition into measurable weekly or quarterly reality.
- It links motivation to explicit scorekeeping without collapsing into one-dimensional KPI chasing.
- It makes course correction easier because each key result can be reviewed separately instead of hiding under one vague objective.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The source defines the system through one objective and a small set of key results.
- Objectives are meant to stretch, not merely document easy commitments.
- Key results are strongest when they combine quantity and quality rather than rewarding output alone.
- Color-coded check-ins make slippage visible early enough to trigger recovery rather than denial.
- The branch now supports a stronger anti-sandbag rule:
  - if teams always hit 100 percent
  - the system may be rewarding caution more than growth

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/success-metrics|Success Metrics]], OKRs are narrower and more action-oriented. Success metrics asks what the scoreboard should count; OKRs define one concrete way to structure that scoreboard around goals.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/strategic-focus|Strategic Focus]], OKRs are downstream. Focus chooses what matters most; OKRs operationalize how that chosen priority will be tracked.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/4dx-execution-framework|4DX Execution Framework]], OKRs allow a slightly richer goal structure and more explicit stretch calibration, while 4DX is simpler and more rigidly weekly in its behavioral defense of one WIG.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The system can become bureaucratic if too many objectives or key results are installed at once.
- Stretch logic is useful, but the branch still needs stronger guidance on how to prevent chronic yellow/red status from becoming demoralizing noise.
- The current source is lighter on cross-team alignment, sequencing, and how OKRs should differ for exploratory versus stable work.
