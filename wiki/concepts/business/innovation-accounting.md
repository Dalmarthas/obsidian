# What It Is

Innovation accounting is the discipline of measuring progress in uncertain product or business work through learning milestones and behavior-grounded movement rather than through standard mature-business KPIs. In this vault, it means creating accountability for experimentation without pretending that a still-uncertain venture can already be judged by the same scorecards used for optimized operations.

# Why It Matters

- It prevents teams from confusing visible effort with real progress.
- It gives experimentation a managerial spine, so "learning" is not used as an excuse for drift.
- It helps organizations fund uncertainty honestly instead of starving new work because it does not yet resemble mature-business performance.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current Eric Ries source argues that ordinary accounting and KPI systems often fail under uncertainty because they assume stable demand and repeatable forecasting.
- That source treats learning milestones as a real accountability system:
  - define the hypothesis
  - run the experiment
  - measure actionable customer behavior
  - use the result to decide whether the direction deserves more investment
- The same source explicitly warns against vanity metrics and success theater.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/validated-learning|Validated Learning]] provides the underlying rule for what should count as progress, while innovation accounting provides the governance layer that records whether that progress is actually happening.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/validated-learning|Validated Learning]], innovation accounting is less about discovering truth and more about scoring whether the discovery process is serious enough to trust.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/business-strategy|Business Strategy]], innovation accounting operates at a lower level: strategy chooses direction, while innovation accounting tests whether the uncertain direction is earning the right to continue.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/framework-driven-action|Framework-Driven Action]], innovation accounting is a safeguard against rhetorical frameworks that sound coherent but produce no credible evidence.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Early-stage metrics can become a new vanity theater if teams optimize shallow clicks or weak signals instead of meaningful commitment.
- Large organizations often praise experimentation in principle while still rewarding only short-term predictability in practice.
- Some domains need longer cycles and richer evidence, which raises the question of how patient innovation accounting can be before it stops protecting against drift.
