# What It Is

Social entrepreneurship is building economically sustainable systems that solve real human problems while treating impact and human delight as central rewards, not as decorative side effects. In Loh's framing, it is not anti-business. It requires finding a problem that costs someone something, creating enough value that people will pay, and using that value engine to expand access or impact.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the business branch a home for impact-oriented venture design that still respects financial robustness.
- It distinguishes sustainable value creation from charity that cannot compound and from profit-seeking that ignores human welfare.
- It links entrepreneurship to AI-era thoughtfulness: people remain valuable collaborators when they care enough to create value for others.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-only-trait-for-success-in-the-ai-era-how-to-build-it-po-shen-loh]] argues that the social entrepreneur's main reward is seeing many people helped or delighted, not maximizing personal wealth.
- The same source insists that the enterprise must still be sustainable by solving a problem with real economic cost and capturing some of the created value.
- Loh's education company is presented as an example: paying after-school math enrichment creates surplus that can subsidize broader access.
- The source also adds a generate-destroy-refine idea process: produce many possibilities, attack their failure modes, and scale only the rare idea that survives.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/validated-learning|Validated Learning]], social entrepreneurship is more mission-centered. Validated learning explains how to test business assumptions; social entrepreneurship explains what kind of human value the business is trying to create.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/creative-problem-solving|Creative Problem Solving]], this concept adds the economic sustainability requirement: the solution must survive outside the idea stage.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-era-thoughtfulness|AI-Era Thoughtfulness]], this is the business expression of the same orientation toward helping real people.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- "Delight" can become vanity if it is not tied to durable improvement, willingness to pay, or measurable benefit.
- Economic sustainability can pull a mission toward higher-paying customers unless subsidy or access design is explicit.
- The vault needs more sources comparing social entrepreneurship with nonprofits, public services, and conventional venture-backed startups.
