# What It Is

Strategic quitting is the practice of treating quitting as a decision-quality skill rather than as automatic failure. In this vault, the concept means distinguishing between a real dip that rewards persistence and a bad game that only consumes time, attention, and identity. The goal is not maximal grit. The goal is to stay with worthwhile hard things and exit low-probability lottery logic before it becomes self-deception.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the business branch a cleaner answer to when perseverance is intelligent and when it is wasteful.
- It connects time horizon, scaffolding, odds, and emotional honesty instead of reducing success to either grit worship or quit-early cynicism.
- It protects process language from becoming romanticized suffering with no strategic edge.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The newer Seth Godin source argues that quitting is not the opposite of strategy. It is part of strategy when the current path is a lottery, has no real scaffolding, or is no longer aligned with the system's gravity.
- That same source distinguishes a dip from a dead end:
  - a dip is a predictable hard middle on the way to being among the best
  - a dead end is a low-odds path sustained mostly by fantasy
  - the decision should depend on evidence, not only on pride or fear
- The source also adds scaffolding as a quitting variable:
  - mentorship, examples, and visible lights on the road increase the rationality of persistence
  - their absence raises the chance that the effort is blind hope rather than compounding skill
- [[wiki/concepts/business/process-over-outcomes|Process Over Outcomes]] overlaps here, but that concept is about staying oriented to contribution instead of applause. This page is narrower and focuses on whether the game itself is worth continuing.
- [[wiki/concepts/business/business-strategy|Business Strategy]] overlaps on choosing what to stop, but this page makes the stopping logic more personal and more explicit under uncertainty.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/process-over-outcomes|Process Over Outcomes]], this concept is less about emotional steadiness and more about strategic exit criteria.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/business-strategy|Business Strategy]], this page is less organizational and more behavioral. It asks whether the person or project is staying in the right game.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/success-metrics|Success Metrics]], this page is less about what to measure and more about how to interpret difficulty without mistaking pain for proof.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Strategic quitting can become self-serving if someone labels every uncomfortable stretch a bad fit before compounding has a chance to start.
- Grit language can become equally distorting if people stay because quitting feels shameful rather than because the evidence is good.
- The branch still needs stronger cross-source material on how to set explicit quit criteria before emotion and sunk costs take over.
