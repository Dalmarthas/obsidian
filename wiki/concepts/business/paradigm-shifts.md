# What It Is

Paradigm shifts are changes in the mental frame through which people understand a situation, compare options, and decide what matters. In this vault, the concept refers to high-leverage interventions that change behavior by changing the map rather than by changing the underlying mechanics first.

# Why It Matters

- It explains why some low-cost framing, categorization, or visualization moves can create value far beyond their implementation cost.
- It gives the business branch a cleaner account of `soft power` that is more precise than vague persuasion language.
- It helps distinguish parameter optimization from deeper redesign of how a problem is perceived.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-rory-sutherland-soft-power-in-a-hard-world|Rory Sutherland Soft Power in a Hard World]] argues that changing paradigms is often more powerful than tweaking parameters because once people see the system differently they start behaving differently.
- That source uses the paceometer, Tube-map integration, room recategorization, and printed-price examples to show that a reframed decision environment can create major economic change with minimal physical change.
- It also implies that many strategic arguments are stuck because teams are choosing within a bad frame rather than questioning the frame itself.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/perception-driven-value|Perception-Driven Value]], paradigm shifts are narrower and more structural. Perception-driven value explains why framing changes value; paradigm shifts focus on the highest-leverage changes to the interpretive system itself.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/psychological-leverage|Psychological Leverage]], paradigm shifts are narrower and higher-level. Psychological leverage includes many interventions that improve experience; paradigm shifts are the subset that reconfigure the model through which choices are understood.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/fat-tailed-marketing|Fat-Tailed Marketing]], paradigm shifts explain one important class of fat-tailed win. Fat-tailed marketing describes the return distribution; paradigm shifts describe a recurrent source of those outsized outcomes.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/business-strategy|Business Strategy]], paradigm shifts are one lever inside strategy, not the whole discipline. Strategy decides where and why to compete; paradigm shifts can change the field of comparison inside that larger game.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Teams can overclaim ordinary messaging changes as `paradigm shifts` when they are really minor packaging moves.
- A frame change can be powerful while still being deceptive if it hides too much of the underlying reality.
- The vault still needs better tests for when a paradigm shift is durable versus when competitors or users adapt quickly and erase the advantage.
