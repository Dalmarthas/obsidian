# What It Is

Social heuristics are simple, memorable, socially legible rules, labels, and scripts that guide behavior in environments where people do not act as isolated rational calculators. In this vault, the concept refers especially to binary or role-based rules that spread through imitation, visibility, and shared expectations rather than through detailed personal optimization.

# Why It Matters

- It explains why some behavior-change interventions work better when they are easy to copy than when they are technically precise.
- It gives the business branch a stronger bridge between psychology, coordination, product adoption, and policy design.
- It clarifies that many frictions are social and cognitive rather than merely economic.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-the-lost-genius-of-irrationality-rory-sutherland|The Lost Genius of Irrationality Rory Sutherland]] argues that named heuristics such as `designated driver` and `zipper merge` outperform abstract recommendations because they create recognizable social scripts.
- That source also claims binary rules such as fixed no-drinking days or no-carb diets are often easier to sustain than continuous numeric thresholds because transgression is obvious and compliance can be socially reinforced.
- The same source broadens Rory's branch beyond individual framing into collective behavior design: norms, market share, visible adoption, and status contagion can become part of the offer itself.
- It also sharpens a diagnosis rule: what looks like irrationality or weak will can instead be a problem of poor defaults, weak peer reinforcement, or asymmetrical network incentives.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/psychological-leverage|Psychological Leverage]], social heuristics are narrower and more collective. Psychological leverage includes any experience redesign that changes behavior; social heuristics focus on rules and scripts that become easier because other people can see, name, and repeat them.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/perception-driven-value|Perception-Driven Value]], this concept is less about what something means and more about how behavior propagates through norms and shared expectations.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/business-strategy|Business Strategy]], social heuristics are not the whole strategy. They are one implementation layer for making adoption, coordination, and compliance easier at low cost.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Binary rules gain power from simplicity, but that same simplicity can become distortion or dogma.
- The vault still needs more examples showing when a named heuristic measurably beats a price incentive or a richer educational intervention.
- Social heuristics can be used for coordination or manipulation; the ethical line needs clearer treatment elsewhere in the branch.
