# What It Is

Ownership Ladder is the progression model in the current business branch for moving from selling time to owning increasingly leveraged outcomes. The concept matters because it turns abstract Naval language about `leverage`, `accountability`, and `specific knowledge` into a sequence: worker, manager of labor, owner-operator, allocator of capital, and eventually system or platform owner. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-14-naval-ravikant-how-to-go-from-employee-to-financially-independent|Naval Ravikant How To Go from Employee To Financially Independent]].

# Why It Matters

- It gives the wealth branch a more practical sequencing model than `productize yourself` alone.
- It clarifies why upside usually stays capped when compensation remains tightly tied to hours.
- It helps distinguish more responsibility from real ownership, since some jobs add stress without adding meaningful upside.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current Naval wealth source already established the core triad:
  - specific knowledge
  - accountability
  - leverage
- The newer source makes that triad sequential through a real-estate ladder:
  - laborer sells time
  - contractor manages other people's time and owns a deliverable
  - developer combines judgment, coordination, and capital risk to own project upside
  - fund manager scales through capital allocation across multiple projects
  - platform owner adds code leverage on top of labor and capital
- The concept therefore tracks a rise across three linked dimensions:
  - more distinct judgment
  - more direct consequence-bearing accountability
  - more scalable leverage
- The ladder also implies that moving upward is not only about harder work:
  - each level changes the unit of value from hours to outcomes to assets to systems
  - the person captures more upside because they control more of the stack
- The source also adds a compounding pattern:
  - long periods of invisible, excellent work may be prerequisites for later step-change opportunities

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/career-capital|Career Capital]], Ownership Ladder is more about where upside sits in the economic structure. Career capital explains how rare skill is earned; ownership ladder explains how that skill gets attached to nonlinear rewards.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/specific-knowledge.md|Specific Knowledge]], this concept is more positional and structural. Specific knowledge explains what makes someone unusually valuable; ownership ladder explains how that value is situated so it can compound.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/permissionless-creation|Permissionless Creation]], ownership ladder is less about starting without approval and more about climbing from contribution to control. Permissionless creation may be one way onto the ladder, especially through media or code, but it is not the ladder itself.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The concept is clarifying, but can become naive if it treats every salaried role as a mistake rather than as a capital-building or skill-building stage.
- More accountability is only better when downside and upside stay meaningfully connected; otherwise the person may absorb risk without ownership.
- The branch still needs stronger material on when capital leverage is wise, when it is fragility disguised as scale, and how platform leverage changes with distribution dependence.
