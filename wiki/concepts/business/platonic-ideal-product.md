# What It Is

Platonic ideal product is the practice of first defining the best theoretically possible version of a product, then working backward to discover which tools, methods, materials, and manufacturing systems would be required to approach it. In the current vault, it is the opposite of tool-driven design.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the Musk branch a cleaner way to talk about design ambition that is not trapped by current workflows.
- It separates ideal-target definition from implementation convenience.
- It helps explain why some teams never escape local optimization: they design around what they already know how to make.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-how-elon-musk-solves-problems|How Elon Musk solves problems]] argues that teams should imagine the theoretically best arrangement first and only then decide what manufacturing and process innovations are necessary.
- The same source treats current tools and methods as common hidden constraints that can deform the product before thinking really begins.
- It also makes the concept iterative:
  - the ideal is not static
  - better knowledge changes the target
  - teams can therefore approach a moving but clarifying horizon rather than a one-time perfect spec

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/first-principles-problem-solving|First-Principles Problem-Solving]], this concept is more design-target oriented. First principles questions assumptions and strips systems down; platonic ideal product asks what the best end state would be if conventions were temporarily suspended.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/operational-productivity|Operational Productivity]], this page is less about how a system currently creates leverage and more about what future state the system should be built toward.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/business-systems|Business Systems]], this concept is less diagnostic and more aspirational. Business systems maps the machine; platonic ideal product clarifies what the machine is trying to make at its limit.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Ideal-product thinking can unlock breakthroughs, but it can also become detached from market timing, manufacturability, or realistic sequencing.
- The current branch still needs stronger examples outside physics-heavy engineering contexts.
- The main open question is how to preserve ambitious target definition without letting it become perfectionist fantasy.
