# What It Is

Stretch goals are objectives set high enough to force growth, creativity, and capability expansion without crossing into absurdity or learned helplessness. In the current vault, the concept means aiming just short of impossible so that the target produces real lift rather than conservative compliance. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-measure-what-matters-by-john-doerr|Measure What Matters by John Doerr]].

# Why It Matters

- It gives the metrics branch a more honest answer to the problem of sandbagging.
- It links ambition to capability-building rather than to vanity slogans alone.
- It helps explain why hitting every target is not automatically evidence that a goal system is healthy.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The Doerr source treats objectives as moonshot-like rather than merely reasonable.
- The right target is described as big but believable, not comfortably safe.
- The source also shows that stretch has to be recalibrated:
  - if goals are impossible, they demotivate
  - if goals are always fully hit, they are likely too soft
- The branch now supports a useful range logic:
  - healthy ambition often shows progress plus strain
  - not effortless green across every metric

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/calibrated-challenge|Calibrated Challenge]], stretch goals are more organizational and metric-linked. Calibrated challenge is a general difficulty principle; stretch goals apply it to explicit targets and scoreboards.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/strategic-focus|Strategic Focus]], stretch goals are less about what to choose and more about how hard the chosen target should pull.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/success-metrics|Success Metrics]], stretch goals are less about scoreboard honesty and more about target difficulty.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Stretch can become theater when leaders demand ambition without support, revision rights, or good metric design.
- Some domains need reliability more than stretch, so the vault still needs better boundaries for where moonshot framing helps versus harms.
- The current branch is stronger on individual or team target-setting than on portfolio-level stretch across many coupled objectives.
