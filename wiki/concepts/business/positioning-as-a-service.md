# What It Is

Positioning as a service is the idea that positioning should reduce confusion for the buyer instead of merely increasing noise for the seller. In this vault, the concept means helping people understand what the offer is for, who it is for, and what corner of the category it deliberately occupies so they can choose honestly. Strong positioning therefore serves both fit and exclusion.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the business branch a cleaner ethical defense of differentiation.
- It connects audience choice, category design, and empathy more tightly than generic `stand out` advice.
- It reframes saying `not for everyone` as a useful service to the market rather than as a branding flourish.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The newer Seth Godin source argues that positioning is a service because overwhelmed buyers need help making sense of choices.
- That source also adds a corner-selection rule:
  - categories can often be mapped on a few meaningful axes
  - strong offers choose a real corner instead of drifting toward average
  - clear positioning includes referring mismatched prospects elsewhere
- The source ties positioning to empathy rather than only to competitive aggression:
  - the useful question is what the audience believes, wants, and seeks to become
  - a good promise helps the right people choose quickly
  - a good exclusion helps the wrong people avoid wasting time
- [[wiki/concepts/business/brand-strategy|Brand Strategy]] overlaps with this page, but that concept is broader and includes long-run meaning, story, and creative coherence. This page is narrower and focuses on the practical service function of strategic difference.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/brand-strategy|Brand Strategy]], this concept is more buyer-facing and more tactical. It asks how the offer is made legible at the moment of choice.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/smallest-viable-audience|Smallest Viable Audience]], this page is less about who to serve and more about how to clarify the offer once that audience is chosen.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/perception-driven-value|Perception-Driven Value]], this page is less about framing value psychologically and more about helping people self-sort honestly into the right offer.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Positioning can help the buyer, but it can also become manipulative if the signal outruns the actual experience.
- A sharply chosen corner can improve clarity, but it can also overconstrain the business if the category map is too simplistic.
- The branch still needs stronger cross-source evidence on how to test category axes empirically rather than relying only on founder intuition.
