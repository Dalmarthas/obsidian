# What It Is

WYSIATI, short for `what you see is all there is`, is Kahneman's claim that the mind readily builds coherent judgments from the evidence immediately available while failing to feel the weight of what is missing. In the current vault, it is the sharpest name for tunnel-vision confidence: the visible story feels complete even when base rates, alternatives, and disconfirming data are absent. Main sources: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-10-daniel-kahneman-two-systems-thinking|Daniel Kahneman Two-Systems Thinking]], [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-thinking-fast-and-slow-by-daniel-kahneman|Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman]].

# Why It Matters

- It explains why incomplete evidence can still produce high confidence.
- It gives the vault a cleaner bridge between bias and overconfident narrative-making.
- It helps translate `slow down and think` into a more specific question: what relevant evidence is currently absent from the story?

# Evidence In This Vault

- The earlier Kahneman source already implied that associative coherence turns partial evidence into convincing stories.
- The newer source makes that logic more explicit with the farmer-versus-librarian example:
  - stereotype-consistent detail captures attention
  - base rates disappear from felt relevance
  - the resulting answer feels obvious even when it is statistically weak
- The current branch now supports a practical correction:
  - ask why the opposite might be true
  - look for base rates
  - treat absent information as a real part of the decision rather than as empty space

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/two-systems-thinking|Two-Systems Thinking]], WYSIATI is a more specific mechanism. Two-systems thinking explains the fast-slow architecture; WYSIATI explains one of the most damaging ways fast coherence outruns evidence.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/expert-intuition|Expert Intuition]], WYSIATI names a failure mode. Expert intuition is what fast judgment looks like when pattern recognition has earned trust; WYSIATI is what fast judgment looks like when it outruns the available evidence.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/mere-exposure-effect|Mere-Exposure Effect]], WYSIATI is more inferential and less affective. Mere exposure makes repetition feel good or safe; WYSIATI makes the visible story feel sufficient.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Base rates are not always the right guide when a case contains genuinely diagnostic detail, so the concept can be over-applied if statistical caution replaces all contextual judgment.
- The branch still needs stronger procedural companions such as forecasting methods, outside-view planning, and explicit evidence templates.
- The current source base is strong on recognizing the trap but thinner on how teams should institutionalize protection against it.
