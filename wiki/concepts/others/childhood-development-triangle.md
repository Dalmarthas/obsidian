# What It Is

The Childhood Development Triangle is a heuristic model for tracing adult behavior back to three early adaptive tasks: staying safe, making and keeping friends, and earning reward or love. In the current vault, it functions as a bridge between Chase Hughes's behavioral-reading material and his identity-and-influence material.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the vault a more developmental explanation for recurring adult scripts.
- It helps connect defensive behavior, people-pleasing, conflict avoidance, and status hunger back to older survival contracts rather than only present-day choices.
- It also creates a link between self-understanding and influence ethics: the more these scripts are visible, the harder they are to exploit unconsciously.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current Chase Hughes source says children learn what they must do to feel safe, to belong, and to receive reward.
- Those strategies are then carried forward into adult work, relationships, ambition, and stress reactions.
- The source frames the ongoing voice of these patterns as a child's voice that can be recognized and reduced in authority rather than fully deleted.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/mask-behavior|Mask Behavior]], the Childhood Development Triangle is more origin-focused: it asks where the defensive strategy first became useful.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/projection-as-self-signal|Projection as Self-Signal]], it is less about what current judgment reveals and more about the old contract that current judgment may be protecting.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/emotional-gravity|Emotional Gravity]], it is more explanatory than dynamic: the triangle describes the early script, while emotional gravity describes how old scripts keep pulling new scenes into their orbit.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The model is psychologically intuitive but still heuristic in the form presented here.
- Developmental explanations can become too totalizing if every present behavior is forced into childhood origin stories.
- The vault still needs stronger overlap with attachment, developmental, and trauma-informed sources to test where this model is solid and where it is overly schematic.
