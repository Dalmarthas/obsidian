# What It Is

Naturalistic meaning is the claim that human value, purpose, and mattering can be real inside a fully physical universe. In the current vault, the key move is that meaning does not need to be written into the cosmos from the outside to count as genuine. It can arise from conscious beings, relationships, projects, and finite lives lived at the human scale. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-11-sean-carroll-physics-philosophy-and-meaning|Sean Carroll Physics, Philosophy, and Meaning]].

# Why It Matters

- It answers a recurring false dilemma: either the universe is purpose-built for us or nothing matters.
- It gives the vault a cleaner way to connect scientific realism with existential seriousness.
- It turns finitude into a source of significance rather than an argument for despair or metaphysical escape.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The Sean Carroll source argues that physicalism does not erase value; it changes where value is located.
- The same source claims that meaning is emergent rather than fundamental, but still fully real at the human level.
- It also treats mortality and finitude as intensifiers of significance because experiences are limited, not endless.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/steve-jobs-stanford-framework|Steve Jobs Stanford Framework]], Carroll is less biographical and more cosmological. Jobs uses mortality to cut through borrowed scripts; Carroll uses finitude to defend meaning inside a nonteleological universe.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/emergence-and-levels-of-explanation|Emergence and Levels of Explanation]], naturalistic meaning is the existential consequence of that broader explanatory model.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/arrow-of-time-and-entropy|Arrow of Time and Entropy]], this concept sits at the human scale rather than the thermodynamic one.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The current branch shows that meaning can survive naturalism, but it does not yet specify how to choose among competing sources of meaning.
- Some readers will still feel that emergent meaning is thinner than transcendent meaning; the vault needs future sources that engage that objection directly.
- The line between `constructed` meaning and `merely arbitrary` meaning still needs stronger treatment.
