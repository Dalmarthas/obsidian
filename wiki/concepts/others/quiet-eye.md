# What It Is

Quiet eye is the final stable visual fixation or tracking gaze that occurs just before and during the critical phase of a skilled movement. In the current vault, the concept matters because it reframes attention as part of execution itself, not only as a vague preparation ritual before performance begins.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the vault a cleaner model of how perception supports skilled action under pressure.
- It sharpens the distinction between raw sensory ability and usable attention.
- It offers a practical bridge between performance science, training design, and the recurring vault theme that high performance depends on better coupling between perception, decision, and action.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source reports that higher-skill performers often show longer quiet-eye durations than less skilled peers in tasks such as golf putting and basketball free throws.
- That same source argues that the real edge is not simply better eyesight, but more effective integration of visual information with movement planning and adjustment.
- It also claims that quiet-eye behavior can be trained, which makes visual steadiness a potential intervention target rather than a fixed trait.
- The source further suggests that unstable gaze and excessive visual variability may weaken performance under pressure.
- [[wiki/concepts/productivity/effective-practice|Effective Practice]] overlaps because quiet eye turns attention into one more concrete lever of practice quality rather than leaving `focus` as a vague instruction.
- [[wiki/concepts/chess/board-vision|Board Vision]] overlaps because both concepts concern what the performer is actually seeing, but quiet eye is more about gaze stability during decisive execution than about mapping a whole symbolic field.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/effective-practice|Effective Practice]], quiet eye is narrower and more perceptual. Effective practice covers repetition quality broadly; quiet eye focuses on the attentional behavior at the point of skilled execution.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/leadership/high-performance-mindset|High-Performance Mindset]], this page is less motivational and more sensorimotor. High-performance mindset explains the broader operating system; quiet eye explains one specific attentional mechanism inside elite execution.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/chess/board-vision|Board Vision]], quiet eye is less about surveying a whole problem space and more about stabilizing gaze at the critical point where perception must guide action cleanly.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The source base does not yet establish whether quiet eye is mostly causal, partly causal, or mainly a marker of expertise.
- The branch still needs stronger evidence on transfer from sports to non-motor or more abstract domains.
- Visual steadiness may matter, but the vault still lacks a sharper model for when longer fixation helps versus when faster scanning is the correct behavior.
