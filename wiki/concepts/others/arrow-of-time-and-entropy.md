# What It Is

The arrow of time and entropy is the claim that time feels directional because entropy increases from an unusually low-entropy past. In the current vault, this is the bridge between abstract thermodynamics and ordinary asymmetries like memory, irreversibility, decay, growth, and the temporary rise of complexity. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-11-sean-carroll-physics-philosophy-and-meaning|Sean Carroll Physics, Philosophy, and Meaning]].

# Why It Matters

- It explains why the world contains `before` and `after` in a meaningful way even though many fundamental equations are more symmetric than human experience.
- It helps the vault connect complexity and life to physical law without treating them as exceptions.
- It provides a cleaner backdrop for discussing finitude, change, and the temporary window in which conscious life exists.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The Sean Carroll source ties the direction of time to entropy increase rather than to a built-in metaphysical purpose.
- It uses irreversibility examples, such as eggs breaking rather than unbreaking, to show how macroscopic asymmetry emerges.
- The same source argues that local complexity can increase during an overall entropy rise, making life and structured systems physically intelligible rather than anomalous.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/emergence-and-levels-of-explanation|Emergence and Levels of Explanation]], this concept is narrower and more physical. Emergence explains explanatory layering; entropy explains why dynamic asymmetry and temporary complexity exist in the first place.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/naturalistic-meaning|Naturalistic Meaning]], this page supplies the cosmological backdrop while naturalistic meaning handles the human-scale consequence.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The source does not solve why the universe began in such a low-entropy state.
- The vault still needs better material on statistical mechanics, coarse-graining, and whether entropy explanations are being simplified too aggressively for philosophical use.
- The current branch explains directionality better than lived temporality, so future sources on phenomenology or memory could sharpen the human side.
