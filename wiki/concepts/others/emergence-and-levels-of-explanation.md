# What It Is

Emergence and levels of explanation is the idea that higher-level realities can be real and explanatory even if they are not fundamental ingredients in the lowest-level physics. In the current vault, the point is not that tables, organisms, minds, purposes, and causes float free of physics. It is that they become intelligible at the scales where those vocabularies actually do useful work. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-11-sean-carroll-physics-philosophy-and-meaning|Sean Carroll Physics, Philosophy, and Meaning]].

# Why It Matters

- It prevents the false choice between `only particle physics is real` and `higher-level human concepts must be supernatural`.
- It gives the vault a cleaner way to talk about causality, consciousness, and meaning without pretending those words belong to the deepest layer of description.
- It supports intellectual pluralism across scales: biology, psychology, and ethics can be valid without competing with physics on physics's own terms.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The Sean Carroll source argues that everyday causal language is a higher-level way of describing stable patterns rather than the fundamental grammar of the universe.
- The same source treats life, mind, and human-scale value as emergent features built from simpler lawful processes.
- It also insists that different descriptive vocabularies can coexist if each is doing real explanatory work at its proper scale.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/two-systems-thinking|Two-Systems Thinking]], this concept operates at a more ontological level. Kahneman explains how human cognition works; Carroll explains why different descriptive layers can all be legitimate.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/expert-intuition|Expert Intuition]], this page is less about when judgment can be trusted and more about why different kinds of explanation can all be real at once.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/naturalistic-meaning|Naturalistic Meaning]], emergence is the structural claim that makes naturalistic meaning possible.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The current source uses `emergence` broadly, but the vault still needs sharper distinctions between weak emergence, strong emergence, and simple scale-dependent description.
- It remains easy to smuggle hand-wavy explanation into the language of emergence if the intermediate mechanism is never made clearer.
- Future sources should test how far this concept can travel before it becomes a vague permission slip rather than a precise explanatory move.
