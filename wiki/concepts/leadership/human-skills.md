# What It Is

Human skills are the trainable interpersonal capacities that let people lead, collaborate, and handle difficulty without relying only on technical competence. In the current vault, the term comes from Simon Sinek's rejection of "soft skills": active listening, feedback, empathy, confrontation, vulnerable help-seeking, and difficult conversations are not decorative extras. They are core leadership infrastructure.

# Why It Matters

- Technical skill does not prepare a person to become responsible for other people.
- Trusting teams require repeated human behaviors, not only values statements.
- Avoided conversations often become culture problems because untrained leaders either stay silent or use authority clumsily.
- Human skills often spill over beyond work into family, friendship, and civic life.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-business-is-an-infinite-game-full-speech-simon-sinek]] argues that organizations over-invest in early technical training and under-invest when people become managers.
- The source names active listening, giving and receiving feedback, empathy, effective confrontation, and difficult conversations as leadership skills that should be practiced explicitly.
- It gives a difficult-conversation opener that does three things:
  - names the leader's fear of saying the wrong thing
  - states that the conversation matters more than avoidance
  - asks the other person for help navigating the conversation together
- The same source argues that command-and-control can work in crises only when trust was built beforehand, which makes ordinary human-skill practice a precondition for emergency authority.
- The broader Sinek branch supports the same point through vulnerability safety, asking for help, phone calls for tense topics, weekly connection rituals, and feedback adapted to the person.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/effective-communication|Effective Communication]], this page is narrower and more leadership-facing. Effective communication covers conversational tools broadly; human skills are the subset that makes responsibility for people livable.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/leadership/trust-based-collaboration|Trust-Based Collaboration]], this page names the trained behaviors. Trust-based collaboration names the relational condition those behaviors help create.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/leadership/leadership|Leadership]], this page is a component concept. Leadership includes direction, decisions, ethics, and environment design; human skills are the interpersonal operating skills that let those responsibilities survive contact with people.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Human skills can become scripted performance if organizations train phrases without changing incentives or leader behavior.
- The term can be used too broadly unless specific observable behaviors are named.
- Emotional skill does not replace hard decisions; it should make hard decisions more honest, humane, and trust-preserving.
- The vault still needs stronger evidence on which training methods reliably change leadership behavior over time.
