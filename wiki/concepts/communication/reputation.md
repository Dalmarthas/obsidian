# What It Is

Reputation is the accumulated story other people carry about your character, competence, consistency, and likely future behavior before they verify anything directly. In the current vault, it is not treated as cosmetic image. It is treated as social leverage that shapes trust, intimidation, access, and the interpretation of later actions.

# Why It Matters

- It explains why perception can move outcomes before direct evidence arrives.
- It gives the power branch a cleaner home for credibility, consistency, and public-record effects.
- It links personal reputation to the broader brand and authority branches without collapsing them into the same thing.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-10-robert-greene-the-48-laws-of-power|Robert Greene The 48 Laws of Power]] treats reputation as compound leverage because expectation often precedes proof.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-master-psychology-to-win-any-competition|Master Psychology to Win Any Competition]] sharpens that model:
  - reputations can intimidate in advance
  - they do not need to be fully accurate to shape outcomes
  - visible inconsistency can rapidly flip interpretation against the person
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-the-path-to-power-ambition-status-strength-and-respect|The Path To Power: Ambition, Status, Strength & Respect]] reinforces the same branch from a broader angle:
  - power in organizations is described as highly psychological rather than purely merit-based
  - reputation can attract or intimidate before direct contact
  - overexposure can dilute status, so visibility may need rhythm rather than constant presence
- The newer source also adds a digital-permanence layer:
  - public posts become part of a durable archive
  - credibility is now affected not only by current behavior but by searchable history
- It also adds an offensive implication:
  - if a rival's projected identity can be contradicted publicly, their reputation may deflate faster than their underlying competence changes

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/power-dynamics|Power Dynamics]], reputation is narrower. Power dynamics covers the whole strategic field; reputation is one of the strongest pieces on that field.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/interactional-authority|Interactional Authority]], reputation is slower and more cumulative. Interactional authority is felt in the room; reputation often arrives before the room.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/brand-strategy|Brand Strategy]], reputation is less about what story should be communicated and more about what story the world already believes.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/brand-stewardship|Brand Stewardship]], reputation is more socially interpreted and power-relevant, while stewardship is more deliberate long-run care of identity and promise.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Reputation can be defensive wisdom, but it can also tempt people into image obsession and strategic inauthenticity.
- The current branch is stronger on how reputations are built and broken than on how damaged credibility can be rebuilt.
- The line between prudent curation and manipulative optics still needs sharper ethical treatment.
