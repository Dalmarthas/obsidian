# What It Is

Opposition research is the disciplined collection and interpretation of publicly available information about your opponent, paired with equally serious self-research on your own side. In the current source base, the point is not gossip. The point is to understand vulnerabilities, prepare responses, and avoid learning essential truths from the other side's attack.

# Why It Matters

- It prevents surprise from becoming the opponent's strategic weapon.
- It clarifies which attacks are legitimate, documentable, and connected to the race.
- It expands the vault's research branch into a political and adversarial context.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Axelrod and Rove insist on researching your own candidate as hard as the opponent will.
- The front-page test helps determine whether a vulnerability is survivable before entering the race.
- The source emphasizes public records, repeated patterns, and the opponent's own words over thin insinuation.
- Attack material should be fair, relevant, true, and credible rather than overheated.
- The best attacks advance the campaign's larger argument instead of existing as random negativity.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Research protects against naivete, but campaign logic can push it toward cynicism or reduction of people to exploitable liabilities.
- Publicly available information can still be used irresponsibly if context or proportionality disappears.
