# What It Is

Ground game and GOTV are the volunteer-driven, voter-contact parts of a campaign that identify supporters, persuade reachable voters, and then turn likely supporters into actual votes. In the current source base, field work is not an afterthought to ads. It is the mechanism that converts campaign theory into ballots.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the vault a durable page for the campaign source's four-phase field model.
- It shows where persuasion and mobilization stop being abstractions and become tracked activity.
- It helps connect targeting, volunteer management, and election-day execution.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Axelrod and Rove give a clear four-phase field sequence:
  - early volunteer recruitment
  - persuasion contact
  - mixed persuasion and mobilization
  - late pure turnout
- The source emphasizes specific targets for doors, calls, volunteers, and captured information.
- Every event is framed as a recruitment opportunity rather than just a visibility moment.
- The final stage abandons broad persuasion and focuses on identified supporters who still have not voted.
- Early voting changes the field logic by letting the campaign check voters off gradually and narrow the final election-day universe.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Ground game can humanize politics through direct contact, but it can also become hyper-instrumental if every relationship is treated only as a turnout mechanism.
- Strong field programs require trust and local initiative, which can clash with overcentralized campaign control.
