# What It Is

Interactional authority is the felt sense of groundedness, comfort, and credibility that changes how other people interpret your words before your explicit argument has fully landed. In the current vault, it is not treated as a posture trick. It is treated as the outward consequence of internal steadiness and off-camera life congruence.

# Why It Matters

- It explains why the same sentence lands differently depending on who says it and in what state.
- It gives the communication branch a clearer concept than generic confidence language.
- It links composure, pace, and everyday self-management to influence without collapsing them into dominance theater.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current Chase Hughes source uses `ACSS` to argue that people over-focus on skills when their real bottleneck is authority or comfort.
- The same source treats comfort as a decisive interactional advantage: the most comfortable person often controls the conversation.
- Authority is framed as leaking out from how someone manages environment, time, appearance, social life, and finances, not only from what they do in the moment.
- Slower, smoother movement and calmer speech are presented as visible authority cues because they imply lower fear and lower urgency.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/frame-control|Frame Control]], interactional authority is less about who defines the meaning of the conversation and more about the embodied credibility that makes frame control easier.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/leadership/high-performance-mindset|High-Performance Mindset]], it is more social and situational: performance becomes visible in the room as calm, pace, and congruence.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/effective-communication|Effective Communication]], it is not the content of the message but the state from which the message is delivered.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Authority language can easily slide into status cosplay if it gets detached from substance and ethics.
- The source is strong on practical intuition but weak on rigorous evidence for how reliably others read these cues.
- The branch still needs stronger comparison with evidence-based work on presence, emotional regulation, and nonverbal communication.
