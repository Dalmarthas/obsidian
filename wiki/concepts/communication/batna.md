# What It Is

BATNA means `best alternative to a negotiated agreement`. In the vault, it matters as the outside option that sets your practical walk-away floor: if the available deal is worse than your next-best credible alternative, the agreement is strategically weak no matter how persuasive it sounds. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-game-theory-and-negotiation|Game Theory and Negotiation]].

# Why It Matters

- It converts negotiation from vague persuasion into comparison against a concrete alternative.
- It protects against accepting dominated deals because of pressure, momentum, or bad framing.
- It links negotiation practice to game theory by treating fallback options as part of the payoff structure rather than as afterthoughts.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source treats BATNA as the negotiation equivalent of an outside option in a game.
- That means BATNA does three kinds of work:
  - it sets a reservation floor for what you should accept
  - it reveals how much leverage you actually have
  - it helps infer how constrained the counterpart may be by their own alternatives
- The source also implies that BATNA is useful before tactics:
  - define your alternative first
  - then interpret offers against it
  - then choose whether the real task is claiming value, creating value, or walking away
- BATNA also sharpens package design:
  - when both sides have weak alternatives, integrative trades become more important
  - when one side has a strong outside option, bluff-heavy tactics become less credible
  - reservation-value clarity can prevent emotional overcommitment to a bad bargaining process

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/negotiation-and-mediation|Negotiation and Mediation]], BATNA is narrower. Negotiation names the whole interaction; BATNA names one structural anchor inside it.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/business/game-theory|Game Theory]], BATNA is more practical and less general. Game theory studies interdependent payoff structures broadly; BATNA is one negotiation-level expression of outside-option logic.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/communication/chris-voss-negotiation-framework|Chris Voss Negotiation Framework]], BATNA is less about moment-to-moment interaction and more about the underlying leverage map that should already be clear before advanced tactics begin.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Strong BATNA awareness can create clarity, but it can also harden someone into premature positional rigidity.
- An imagined or inflated BATNA is worse than no BATNA at all because it produces false confidence.
- The current branch still needs stronger sources on how to improve BATNA in advance rather than merely measure it during the conversation.
