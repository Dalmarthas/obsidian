# What It Is

An accusation audit is a preemptive cluster of negative labels that names what the other person may already be thinking about you, your ask, or the situation before those fears harden into resistance. In the current source base, it is one of the most advanced and distinctive tools in the detailed Chris Voss branch.

# Why It Matters

- It surfaces negative assumptions before they poison the conversation.
- It reduces defensive shock when bad news or a difficult ask arrives.
- It shows unusual emotional courage because it names what most people try to dodge.
- It deepens the vault's model of tactical empathy beyond one-off labels.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The detailed workplace Voss source defines accusation audits as a sequence of negative labels such as `you probably think...`.
- That source recommends using them at the beginning of difficult conversations, before important asks, and before delivering bad news.
- It also stresses preparation: write out at least six likely negative labels in advance and use synonyms if needed.
- Voss treats the audit as inoculation. By naming the negative story first, you reduce its force when the conversation turns difficult.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- If the labels are wildly inaccurate, the move can create negativity that was not there.
- The technique requires emotional steadiness; done badly, it can sound melodramatic or manipulative.
- It is powerful enough that the vault should keep treating ethical constraint as part of the method, not as an optional extra.
