# What It Is

Political campaigns are organized attempts to define an election, persuade voters, mobilize supporters, and win enough votes to govern. In the current source base, a campaign is not just a media exercise. It is a full operating system built from purpose, framing, research, targeting, fundraising, field work, and disciplined repetition.

# Why It Matters

- It creates a durable home for one of the vault's most operationally rich political sources.
- It links persuasion, leadership, messaging, planning, and execution inside a single environment.
- It shows how strategy becomes real only when the plan, budget, staff, message, and field program all align.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Axelrod and Rove treat `Why are you running?` as the governing campaign question and the filter for all later choices.
- Campaigns are described as battles of definition where the side that frames the choice usually wins.
- The source insists on a written plan with target voters, communications strategy, timeline, budget, and monitoring.
- Strong campaigns combine media, direct contact, fundraising, and timing rather than hoping one channel will carry the race.
- The campaign itself is framed as an early test of whether a candidate can actually lead.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Campaign logic can make politics look more managerial and instrumental than democratic deliberation really is.
- The source treats winning as central, but democratic legitimacy also depends on how campaigns handle truth, fairness, and public trust.
