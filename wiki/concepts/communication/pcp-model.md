# What It Is

The PCP Model is a three-step influence heuristic built from Perception, Context, and Permission. In the current vault, it describes how behavior changes more easily when a person's interpretation of the situation shifts first, then the meaning of the situation shifts, and only then a new action starts to feel natural rather than forced.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the Chase Hughes branch a named causal model rather than a pile of tactics.
- It explains why some influence attempts fail even when the argument itself is decent: the perception and context layers were never changed.
- It also creates a bridge between communication, identity, environmental design, and self-programming.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current Chase Hughes source presents PCP as the core sequence underneath powerful influence.
- `Perception` is about how the person is framing what is happening.
- `Context` is about what kind of situation the person believes they are in and what norms follow from that.
- `Permission` is about what now feels acceptable, expected, or automatic.
- The source uses PCP across manipulation, persuasion, self-change, and radicalization examples, not only in one narrow domain.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/frame-control|Frame Control]], PCP is more structural and sequential. Frame control is about holding meaning in the interaction; PCP is about how changed meaning opens the door to changed behavior.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/permission-bridge|Permission Bridge]], PCP is broader. A permission bridge is one micro-transition tool, while PCP is the wider model of why such transitions matter.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/persuasion|Persuasion]], PCP is less about verbal argument quality and more about the hidden architecture that makes argument easier or harder to receive.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The model is operationally useful, but the source leans heavily on dramatic examples that can make the mechanism sound more precise than it is.
- PCP can be used for humane reframing or for autonomy bypass, so it needs ethical boundaries from outside the source itself.
- The branch still needs comparison with better-supported research on framing, context effects, and behavior change.
