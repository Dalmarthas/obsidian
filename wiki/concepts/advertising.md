# What It Is

Advertising is the practice of shaping messages, stories, images, and experiences that connect a brand to people's attention and desire. In the current source base, the strongest version of advertising is not crude persuasion but culturally aware communication that people voluntarily engage with because it feels human, memorable, and true. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-07-advertising-goodby-silverstein-masterclass-summary|Goodby Silverstein Masterclass Summary]].

# Why It Matters

- It reframes advertising from interruption toward relevance and attention-worthiness.
- It links creativity and commerce without pretending the commercial context disappears.
- It establishes a durable base for future marketing, brand, media, and communication sources in this vault.
- It clarifies that great advertising depends on both craft and strategic empathy.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Advertising should create things people want to see.
- The best work should not feel like typical advertising even when it still serves commercial ends.
- Truth, authenticity, and cultural observation are treated as non-negotiable sources of ideas.
- Modern advertising can include PR, experiences, social acts, and digital behavior, not just traditional commercials.
- Craft details such as timing, casting, typography, sound, and production are treated as load-bearing rather than decorative.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The line between genuine cultural participation and manipulative appropriation is always unstable.
- Work that avoids "looking like advertising" still has to perform within a business system.
