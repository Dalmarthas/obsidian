# What It Is

Voter targeting is the process of deciding which voters matter most, what kind of contact they need, and how to allocate scarce resources accordingly. In the current source base, targeting is not just demographic sorting. It is the strategic bridge between political landscape analysis and actual persuasion or turnout work.

# Why It Matters

- It turns the abstract electorate into actionable segments.
- It prevents campaigns from wasting resources on people who will never move or who need a different intervention.
- It gives the vault a clear page for one of the source's most operational contributions.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Axelrod and Rove divide the electorate into your base, the opponent's base, and the overlap zone of persuadable or weakly attached voters.
- The source stresses that elections are usually decided in the overlap rather than by preaching only to committed supporters.
- The targeting model becomes more granular through turnout scores, support scores, and issue-priority segmentation.
- Microtargeting is treated as powerful but risky if the campaign gets so narrow that it misses reachable voters.
- The message should be tailored to the issues and channels that matter to specific target groups.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Better targeting improves efficiency, but it can also fragment public discourse into tiny customized messages.
- Precision helps campaigns, but extreme narrowing may cause a campaign to misread the full electorate.
