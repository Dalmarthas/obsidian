# What It Is

Whole-food, plant-based diet refers here to a dietary pattern centered on vegetables, legumes, fruits, and whole grains while minimizing or avoiding animal products and resisting the slide into highly processed "vegan" substitutes. In this vault, the emphasis is not merely on plant identity but on whole-food quality and repeatable daily practice.

# Why It Matters

- It is the central operating pattern in the current nutrition branch.
- It turns preventive health from abstract advice into a concrete food system.
- It matters because the branch argues this pattern is one of the strongest levers available for shifting chronic-disease risk and long-term function.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The Michael Greger source frames whole-food, plant-based eating as a pattern that can prevent, treat, and in some cases reverse leading chronic diseases.
- The summary repeatedly distinguishes beneficial plant-heavy eating from junk-food veganism.
- The branch also treats the pattern as workable rather than theoretical: athletes can still meet demands, conventional produce is still worth eating, and incremental movement toward the pattern matters.
- Nutritional safeguards remain part of the concept, especially `B12` and possibly algae-derived omega-3s.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The branch is strongly plant-forward, but the current source does not fully map dose-response nuance or the strongest counterarguments.
- The concept can become misleading if "plant-based" is interpreted as a label rather than as a whole-food pattern with nutritional discipline.
- The vault still needs stronger primary evidence and additional nutrition sources to test which claims remain strongest across contexts and populations.
