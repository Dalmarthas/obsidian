# What It Is

Executive-function scaffolding is the deliberate use of external supports to compensate for unreliable initiation, working memory, inhibition, prioritization, and self-regulation. In this vault, the concept is not about making people more dependent on systems for its own sake. It is about offloading fragile mental steps onto visible structures so action does not depend entirely on willpower in the moment.

# Why It Matters

- It replaces the vague advice to `try harder` with concrete support design.
- It helps explain why capable people can still fail at starting, sequencing, remembering, or slowing down under pressure.
- It gives the health branch a bridge between neurocognitive difficulty and daily practice rather than leaving the branch at diagnosis language alone.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current ADHD-anxiety source treats calendars, checklists, routines, and visual reminders as working-memory supports rather than mere productivity preferences.
- Structured writing and journaling are framed as ways to externalize mental noise so reflection becomes possible.
- Exercise and mindfulness are treated as supports for slowing and regulating attention, not as purity rituals.
- Pause buffers such as `sleep on it` or deliberate waiting periods are presented as compensations for impulsive action under overwhelm.
- The source also treats social help, especially [[wiki/concepts/health/body-doubling|Body Doubling]], as a live scaffold rather than a sign of weakness.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/adhd-and-anxiety-overlap|ADHD and Anxiety Overlap]], this concept is more intervention-focused. The overlap page explains why the problem arises; this page explains how to build around it.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/stress-mindset|Stress Mindset]], executive-function scaffolding is more structural and less interpretive. One changes the meaning of arousal; the other changes the environment and workflow around fragile cognition.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/premortem|Premortem]], both rely on externalized thinking, but premortem is risk-analysis-focused while scaffolding is about everyday functioning.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/peak-hours|Peak Hours]], this page is less about using your best time well and more about preventing collapse when initiation, memory, or inhibition are weak regardless of time block.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The current branch does not yet separate short-term support tools from longer-term skill-building clearly enough.
- Scaffolding can be misapplied if it becomes an ever-growing system that itself becomes overwhelming to maintain.
- The vault still needs stronger material on how scaffolding should adapt across severity levels, medication response, and different life contexts.
