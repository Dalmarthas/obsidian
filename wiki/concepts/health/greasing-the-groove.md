# What It Is

Greasing the groove is a high-frequency, submaximal practice model where strength is trained more like a skill than like a series of exhausting events. In this vault, the method means repeating technically clean sets often enough to improve neural efficiency and pattern quality while avoiding major fatigue buildup.

# Why It Matters

- It offers a concrete alternative to the "destroy yourself to improve" model of training.
- It helps explain why strength progress can come from repeated quality rather than repeated breakdown.
- It is especially useful for people who need sustainable progress, technical refinement, or lower fatigue cost.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The Pavel Tsatsouline source explicitly describes improvement as spaced, skill-like practice.
- The branch recommends using submaximal sets performed frequently across the week.
- Technical quality is treated as central, not optional.
- The method is presented as one reason strength can improve without constant soreness, failure, or recovery debt.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/minimal-effective-dose-training|Minimal Effective Dose Training]], this page is more specific: it is one concrete implementation of low-cost, high-quality training.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/recovery-and-heterochronicity|Recovery and Heterochronicity]], this page explains a method that respects recovery rather than overspending it.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The method is compelling for strength skill and frequent practice, but the vault still needs more exercise sources to test where it is strongest and where more concentrated fatigue is useful.
- Greasing the groove can be misunderstood as "always easy"; the deeper point is repeated precision, not laziness.
