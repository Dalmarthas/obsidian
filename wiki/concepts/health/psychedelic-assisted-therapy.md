# What It Is

Psychedelic-assisted therapy is a structured therapeutic model in which psychedelic compounds are used inside a carefully designed process of screening, preparation, guided experience, and post-session integration. In this vault, the key idea is not simply that psychedelics can be powerful. It is that their effects are unusually dependent on context, meaning, and support, which makes the therapeutic container part of the treatment itself.

# Why It Matters

- It opens a new health branch that treats mental-health intervention, consciousness, and clinical structure as linked rather than separate topics.
- It adds a stronger model for why some intense experiences become healing while others become destabilizing.
- It creates a durable place in the vault for set-and-setting logic, integration, and the distinction between therapeutic promise and hype.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The Michael Pollan source argues that the best evidence-supported use cases involve clinically supervised or otherwise highly structured settings rather than casual experimentation.
- That same source treats `set and setting` as causally central:
  - mindset and intention shape the experience
  - environment and guidance shape the risk profile
  - integration helps convert a powerful event into lasting behavioral or therapeutic change
- The source also highlights a neuroscience layer around temporary default mode network quieting and ego-softening, though it presents that more as a plausible explanatory frame than as a fully settled theory.
- The branch is deliberately cautious:
  - physiological toxicity appears low
  - addiction risk appears low
  - psychological risk can still be meaningful, especially with poor screening, unstable settings, or exploitative guidance

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/stress-mindset|Stress Mindset]], this page is more intervention-specific and less about everyday interpretation of pressure. McGonigal changes the meaning of normal arousal; this branch is about unusually intense altered states used therapeutically.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/biohack-skepticism|Biohack Skepticism]], this page sits in productive tension: the Pollan source is open to transformative potential, while skepticism is needed to prevent the branch from sliding into glamour, weak evidence, or reckless self-experimentation.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/healthspan|Healthspan]], this page is narrower and more psychologically focused. It is not a full health model, but it may matter for function and quality of life where entrenched mental-health burdens are involved.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The therapeutic branch is promising but still vulnerable to overstatement, commercialization, and weakly governed enthusiasm.
- A meaningful part of the effect may depend on suggestibility, which is both a therapeutic opportunity and a manipulation risk.
- Microdosing remains especially uncertain here: popular enthusiasm is stronger than the evidence captured in the current source.
- The main open question is how the vault should rank psychedelic-assisted therapy relative to more established interventions when evidence, access, and risk vary by condition.
