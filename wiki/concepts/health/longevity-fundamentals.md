# What It Is

Longevity fundamentals are the high-confidence daily levers that seem to matter more than trend-chasing for preserving long-term function. In the current branch, these fundamentals include exercise, sleep, diet quality, metabolic health, not smoking, maintaining blood pressure and lipids, and protecting social and emotional life.

# Why It Matters

- It keeps the longevity branch grounded in repeatable behavior instead of speculation alone.
- It helps separate durable health work from the seduction of edge-case interventions.
- It gives the vault a reusable summary page for what "do the basics well" actually means inside the current health sources.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The composite source repeatedly returns to exercise, diet quality, insulin sensitivity, blood pressure, lipids, and smoking avoidance as the most durable heart-disease and longevity levers.
- Sleep quality, regular routines, and medical diagnosis for sleep problems remain important through the overlap with the existing Rhonda Patrick branch.
- The source also adds social connection, happiness, family, and purpose as real contributors to long-horizon well-being.
- One of the clearest meta-claims in the source is that what you do decades earlier matters more than dramatic rescue efforts late in the game.
- The Pavel Tsatsouline branch sharpens the exercise layer by arguing that durable strength, grip, submaximal progression, and recoverable practice are more useful than chronically exhausting training.
- The new brain-health source sharpens the cognitive layer by making aerobic exercise, deep sleep, and broad risk reduction feel directly relevant to preserving memory and late-life brain function.

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/sleep-quality|Sleep Quality]], this page is broader and includes sleep as one part of a full preventive stack.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/diet-and-chronic-disease|Diet and Chronic Disease]], this page is less nutrition-specific and more whole-system.

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/strength-training|Strength Training]], this page is more general; the strength branch provides one of the clearest operational models for how to turn movement into durable long-term capability.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/brain-health-and-cognitive-aging|Brain Health and Cognitive Aging]], this page is broader and less outcome-specific; the brain-health branch names one especially important reason the fundamentals matter.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- "Fundamentals" can become too vague unless they stay tied to concrete markers, routines, and thresholds.
- The source contains some supplement enthusiasm alongside its fundamentals language, so the vault should keep asking when an intervention genuinely improves the fundamentals versus distracts from them.
- The vault still needs more primary or higher-resolution health material to turn this from a good orientation page into a more evidence-stratified one.
