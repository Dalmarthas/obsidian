# What It Is

Minimal effective dose training is the idea that the best training dose is not the maximum tolerable amount, but the smallest amount that keeps adaptation moving reliably. In the current branch, this means choosing a small number of high-transfer exercises, keeping sets submaximal, and resisting the urge to confuse exhaustion with progress.

# Why It Matters

- It protects consistency by lowering unnecessary biological cost.
- It makes training more portable across busy schedules, older age, and long-horizon health goals.
- It creates a cleaner hierarchy: quality, repetition, and recoverability first; quantity and suffering second.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The Pavel Tsatsouline source repeatedly argues for a small exercise menu instead of endless variation.
- The branch recommends working around `75-85%` effort and stopping well before failure.
- It frames good sessions as energizing and adaptive rather than depleting.
- The same logic also supports long-term maintenance: once a strength base exists, one well-placed weekly session may preserve much of it.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Minimal effective dose can become underdosing if used as an excuse to avoid progression.
- Different goals may require different minimums; health maintenance, maximal strength, hypertrophy, and sport preparation are not identical.
- The vault still needs broader exercise sources to distinguish what is universally durable from what is primarily Tsatsouline's preferred style.
