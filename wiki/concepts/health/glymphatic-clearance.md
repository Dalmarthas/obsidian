# What It Is

Glymphatic clearance is the brain's waste-removal process that is especially active during deep sleep. In this vault, the concept matters because it gives the sleep branch a direct mechanism-level link to long-term brain health rather than treating sleep as only a mood or energy variable.

# Why It Matters

- It explains why deep sleep may matter for more than next-day recovery.
- It connects sleep quality to Alzheimer's-related waste handling.
- It gives the health domain a concrete mechanistic bridge between nighttime restoration and cognitive aging.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source says deep sleep activates glymphatic cleanup.
- The same branch links that cleanup to removal of waste, including amyloid associated with Alzheimer's disease.
- Long-term poor sleep is therefore framed as a cognitive-risk amplifier rather than a mere comfort problem.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/sleep-quality|Sleep Quality]], this page is narrower and more mechanistic: sleep quality is the systems outcome, while glymphatic clearance is one reason that outcome matters.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/brain-health-and-cognitive-aging|Brain Health and Cognitive Aging]], this page zooms into one pathway rather than the full prevention picture.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The source presents a persuasive mechanism, but the vault still needs broader evidence on how strongly glymphatic effects translate into real-world dementia prevention.
- It is also still unclear how much of the benefit comes specifically from deep sleep quantity versus broader sleep continuity and overall sleep architecture.
