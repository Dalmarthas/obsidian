# What It Is

Reward-based habit loops are repeated behavioral patterns reinforced by an expected payoff. In the current vault, the core structure is `trigger -> behavior -> perceived reward -> repetition`. The important claim is that habits persist not because they are rationally endorsed, but because the brain has learned to predict relief, pleasure, or control from repeating them.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the health branch a concrete learning mechanism for why people repeat behaviors they already know are harmful.
- It explains why insight alone often fails to change action.
- It shifts behavior change from moral struggle to prediction updating: if the expected reward changes, the loop weakens.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The Brewer source treats reward-based learning as the governing engine behind repetitive habits.
- Emotional states such as stress, boredom, sadness, and craving are treated as valid triggers, not just environmental cues.
- The same source argues that the loop weakens when the person discovers through direct observation that the real reward is smaller, dirtier, or less satisfying than expected.
- The source also implies that stress makes the loop harder to interrupt because top-down control is less available under pressure.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-how-casinos-are-able-to-predict-human-behavior|How Casinos Are Able to Predict Human Behavior]] adds a product-design variant:
  - opportunity plus unpredictable reward plus quick repeatability can make a loop unusually sticky
  - near misses and losses disguised as wins can preserve reward expectation even when the objective payoff is poor
  - richer environments and alternative rewards may weaken compulsive preference for variable schedules

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/stress-mindset|Stress Mindset]], this concept is less about how pressure is interpreted and more about how repeated behavior gets reinforced under that pressure.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/adhd-and-anxiety-overlap|ADHD and Anxiety Overlap]], reward-based habit loops are less diagnostic and more behavioral. The overlap page explains mechanism differences between conditions; this page explains the learning logic that can trap behavior inside either condition.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/mindful-curiosity|Mindful Curiosity]], this page describes the loop itself while mindful curiosity describes the intervention stance used to update it.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/scarcity-loop|Scarcity Loop]], this page is broader. Scarcity loops are a special case of reward-based learning designed around uncertain opportunity and fast repetition.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The current branch is strongest on everyday compulsive patterns and still needs stronger coverage of addiction severity, withdrawal, and social reinforcement.
- A habit-loop model can become reductive if it ignores trauma, identity, environment, or physiology.
- The vault still needs better distinction between `habit`, `compulsion`, `addiction`, and `coping behavior`.
- The scarcity-loop source adds an environment problem: internal observation may not be enough when the surrounding product or context is engineered for rapid repetition.
