# What It Is

Biohack skepticism is the discipline of distrusting intervention glamour when the evidence is thin, the effect size is small, or the practice distracts from the fundamentals. In the current branch, this skepticism is not anti-experimentation in general; it is anti-confusion about what deserves priority.

# Why It Matters

- It protects the health branch from becoming a pile of trendy but weakly grounded interventions.
- It clarifies that "advanced" does not automatically mean "important."
- It creates a durable check against supplement, gadget, and protocol inflation.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The composite source explicitly says many biohacks are overhyped and that exercise, sleep, diet, and emotional well-being still dominate.
- Cold plunges are treated as mood-supportive at best rather than as proven life-extension tools, and no magic protocol is presented as settled.
- The source is cautious about supplements in general, recommending testing and deficiency logic rather than automatic stacking.
- At the same time, the same branch still entertains `NMN`, metformin, fish oil, creatine, and other interventions, which makes skepticism itself one of the source's key organizing tensions.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/longevity-fundamentals|Longevity Fundamentals]], this page is the defensive half of the same operating logic: protect the basics from being displaced by novelty.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/healthspan|Healthspan]], this page is not about the goal but about better decision hygiene on the path toward it.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Healthy skepticism can become excessive conservatism if it dismisses promising interventions too early.
- The current branch itself mixes optimism about specific interventions with warnings against hype, so priority-setting matters more than purity.
- The vault still needs better source separation and primary literature if it wants to rank specific supplements or protocols with confidence.
