# What It Is

Aerobic exercise and brain health is the claim that regular cardiovascular training protects cognition and brain structure rather than merely improving general fitness. In the current branch, it is the clearest exercise-specific route to lower dementia risk and better late-life brain resilience.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the vault a direct link between exercise and cognition.
- It adds a brain-specific aerobic layer to a health domain that already contains sleep, nutrition, longevity, and strength.
- It matters because the source frames aerobic exercise as one of the strongest practical moves available right now.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source says regular aerobic training can help prevent brain shrinkage and even improve hippocampal volume.
- The most concrete protocol in the source is about `30` minutes, `3` times per week, around `70-75%` max heart rate.
- Cardiorespiratory fitness is also framed as strongly associated with lower dementia risk.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/strength-training|Strength Training]], this page is more cognition-specific and aerobic rather than force-focused.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/longevity-fundamentals|Longevity Fundamentals]], this page is one operational exercise slice inside the larger prevention stack.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/healthspan|Healthspan]], it is one route to preserving mental function rather than a description of the whole goal.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The branch currently emphasizes aerobic work more than resistance work for brain outcomes, but the vault still lacks enough sources to compare their relative effects with confidence.
- The dementia-risk figure in the source is directionally strong, but likely needs closer paper-level scrutiny.
