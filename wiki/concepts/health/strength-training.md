# What It Is

Strength training is the deliberate practice of building force production, structural capacity, and usable physical resilience. In this vault, the current branch treats it less as physique work and more as a foundational health and capability system that supports function across sport, aging, and everyday life.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the health branch a concrete movement layer instead of abstract advice to "exercise more."
- It links physical capability to long-horizon independence, durability, and functional confidence.
- It matters because the current source treats strength as a base quality that supports many other adaptations rather than as a niche athletic concern.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The Pavel Tsatsouline source presents strength as the "mother quality" that supports endurance, flexibility, and daily performance.
- The branch favors a small menu of high-transfer compound exercises over constant novelty.
- It repeatedly argues that skill, consistency, and nervous-system adaptation matter as much as muscular effort.
- The source also ties strength back to healthspan through grip strength, sustainable training, and joint-friendly exercise choice across life stages.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/longevity-fundamentals|Longevity Fundamentals]], this page provides a more specific training model for one major pillar of long-term health.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/healthspan|Healthspan]], this page is more operational: healthspan is the target, while strength training is one durable route to preserving function and autonomy.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/sleep-quality|Sleep Quality]], this page works on capacity through training while the sleep branch protects restoration and adaptation quality.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Strength training is easy to oversimplify into "just lift heavy," but the current branch argues that method, frequency, and recovery quality matter as much as load.
- The branch strongly favors submaximal practice, but the vault still needs broader evidence and more sources to map where that remains optimal versus where it is merely one strong style.
- The vault also needs more explicit coverage of flexibility, power, and endurance to test the branch's claim that strength is the primary base.
