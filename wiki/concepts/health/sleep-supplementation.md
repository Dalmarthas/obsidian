# What It Is

Sleep supplementation is the use of compounds such as melatonin, magnesium, L-theanine, glycine, or myo-inositol to support sleep onset, calmness, or continuity. In this vault, the current branch treats supplements as targeted aids, not as substitutes for diagnosis, light timing, or behavior change.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the vault a practical sleep-tool layer for cases where timing, anxiety, travel, or mild fragmentation need extra support.
- It also creates a useful distinction between better-supported and weaker-supported supplement choices.
- It helps keep supplement use proportional instead of turning it into a catch-all response.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Melatonin is framed as best for jet lag and shift work at lower doses rather than habitual high dosing.
- Magnesium glycinate is presented as a relaxation and stress-recovery support.
- L-theanine is positioned as especially useful for mental chatter and mild anxiety.
- Glycine is presented as one of the stronger practical candidates for reducing awakenings and improving subjective sleep quality.
- Myo-inositol is described as more indirect and less well supported for sleep itself.
- The branch repeatedly implies that supplements should sit below cause-identification and circadian/behavioral corrections in the intervention hierarchy.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/sleep-quality|Sleep Quality]], supplementation is a narrower layer inside the larger sleep system.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/nighttime-awakenings|Nighttime Awakenings]], supplements can help but do not resolve apnea, nocturia, or other medical causes.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Sleep supplement advice is easy to overapply because it feels actionable even when the real problem is timing, stimulants, alcohol, or a medical condition.
- The current branch gives practical direction, but the vault still lacks direct source pages for the underlying trials.
- One open question is when supplementation meaningfully adds value beyond better timing and environment, and when it mostly papers over deeper problems.
