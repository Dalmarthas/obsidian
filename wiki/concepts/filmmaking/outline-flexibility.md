# What It Is

Outline flexibility is the principle that a screenplay outline should function as a revisable scaffold rather than as a rigid law code. In this vault, the shared view is that structure matters deeply, but the right amount of advance planning varies by writer, project, and phase of development.

# Why It Matters

- It prevents a false choice between `pure discovery` and `total preplanning`.
- It makes room for both macro-clarity and live creative surprise.
- It lets writers choose the lightest planning method that still exposes structural weakness.
- It reduces the chance that rules, beats, or cards become substitutes for actual dramatic life.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The 2010 screenplay article is the most prescriptive: outline before drafting and know the major structural turns.
- The Duffer Brothers use layered development, repeatedly refining the story at different zoom levels before pages become expensive.
- Lynch presents the lightest version: use skeleton drafts and simple scene cards to preserve shape without overfixing the work too early.
- Truby stresses stronger pre-draft architecture, but even in his model the point of planning is to pressure-test the story, not to worship act labels.
- The current outlining-comparison source makes the cross-source pattern explicit:
  - some writers outline heavily
  - some only to the midpoint
  - some delay formal outlining because too much structure too soon can suffocate the story
  - nearly all still treat structure as foundational
- The same source also broadens the toolset beyond cards:
  - beat sheets
  - corkboards
  - sequence pages
  - notebooks
  - visual rhythm checks

# Source Comparisons

- The 2010 article is strongest on pre-draft certainty and classical structural readiness.
- The Duffer Brothers are strongest on recursive development and progressive refinement.
- Lynch is strongest on low-friction structure that protects freshness and intuition.
- Truby is strongest on the idea that outlines should solve deeper architecture problems, not just place turns on a page count.
- The current outlining-comparison source is strongest on pluralism: outline density differs, but structure still has to become visible somehow.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Too much early outlining can freeze the material before the writer has discovered what is alive inside it.
- Too little outlining can hide pacing, escalation, and midpoint problems until revision becomes much more expensive.
- `Rules are tools` is useful advice, but it can also become an excuse for shapelessness if the draft never develops a readable macro-design.
- The right balance probably shifts by genre, by medium, and by the writer's own strengths.
