# What It Is

Personal doorway is the specific emotional, thematic, psychological, or experiential entry point through which a writer can honestly inhabit a story. In the current source base, the point is not that every story must be autobiographical. The point is that the writer needs a genuine internal connection to some live part of the material, otherwise the work tends to become sterile, generic, or mechanically executed.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the vault a concrete way to connect authenticity to assignment writing.
- It helps explain why two writers can handle the same premise with very different levels of energy and originality.
- It turns project selection into a craft question, not only a career question.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Martell's central question is `How is this my story?`
- He argues that even in work-for-hire situations, the writer needs a real doorway into the material.
- The source suggests that emotional equivalence can matter more than literal life overlap: the writer may not share the plot circumstances but can still share the underlying human feeling.
- The same concept also acts as a refusal filter: if the writer cannot find a doorway and the material violates core values, the assignment may be worth declining.
- Genre does not cancel personal meaning; a writer can still inject a subversive or deeply felt angle into restrictive material.

# Source Comparisons

- Martell is more pragmatic about assignments than Lynch, who speaks more from the standpoint of originating work from pure inner necessity.
- He overlaps with creative-integrity sources but adds a specific operational test: find the doorway or reconsider the project.
- He also overlaps with Rushdie's worldview layer, but Martell is more focused on how personal meaning enters externally given material rather than only self-originated work.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Personal doorway can be a strong filter, but it can also become too restrictive if the writer refuses all work that does not immediately feel intimate.
- Some writers may discover the doorway only after working on the project for a while.
- The line between `adapting the assignment through your voice` and `forcing the wrong self onto the material` remains unstable.
