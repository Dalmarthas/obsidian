# What It Is

Premise generation is the upstream practice of producing many possible story engines before choosing which one deserves serious development. In the current vault, it is not treated as passive inspiration but as an active system built from observation, structured prompts, playful recombination, and the willingness to generate more ideas than will ever be used.

# Why It Matters

- It prevents the writer from overcommitting to the first merely adequate idea.
- It creates a larger menu of possible stories before premise testing begins.
- It helps separate abundance from judgment so early ideation is not strangled by premature evaluation.
- It gives the vault a cleaner home for source material about generating premises, not only testing them.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Simon Rich is the strongest current source on volume: premise generation is a habit, numerical targets help, and most ideas should be expected to fail.
- He also contributes the clearest framework layer: `what if?`, genre twists, fear literalization, satirical reframing, and mining ordinary life for absurd story seeds.
- The Duffer Brothers overlap through blue-sky ideation and refusing to draft too early when the central engine is still weak.
- Rhimes overlaps through constant idea capture and waiting for the ideas that continue to feel alive under pressure.
- The current generic storytelling source overlaps through idea collection, simple logline distillation, and filtering for concepts that hold attention.
- The anti-boredom roundtable overlaps through alternative generation drills and the warning that first-thought ideas are often too imitative or emotionally dead.

# Source Comparisons

- Simon Rich is strongest on abundance, playfulness, and comedy-specific premise distortion.
- The Duffer Brothers are strongest on scaling a promising idea upward into a pilot or series engine.
- Rhimes is strongest on deciding whether an idea can actually sustain recurring dramatic life.
- The generic storytelling source is strongest on turning raw ideas into a simple communicable logline.
- The roundtable source is strongest on pushing past cliche by forcing multiple alternatives before trusting a concept.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- High-volume ideation improves odds, but it can also become productivity theater if the writer never commits to deeper development.
- Strong premise generation still needs later testing for conflict, stakes, and transformation.
- Comedy-friendly ideation frameworks may not transfer cleanly to more solemn genres without adjustment.
