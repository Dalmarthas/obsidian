# What It Is

Film distribution is the practical bridge between a finished film and actual platform or audience access. In this vault, it includes not only sales placement but also the intermediaries, delivery requirements, packaging standards, and trust filters that determine whether a completed project can move from finished asset to commercially delivered release.

# Why It Matters

- Finishing a film does not automatically make it available, discoverable, or deliverable.
- Distribution shapes leverage, margins, and what kind of release path is even possible.
- Technical compliance can matter as much as creative quality once a buyer becomes interested.
- It gives the filmmaking branch a home for the post-production commercial pipeline rather than stopping at craft.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Streamer-facing buyers often want the project's value proposition fast, with minimal preamble.
- Approved distributors or aggregators can function as mandatory gatekeepers even when a platform likes the film.
- Delivery specifications such as subtitles, multi-language assets, and compliance packages create hidden cost and operational complexity.
- Prior delivery track record reduces perceived risk for buyers and partners.
- New filmmakers often need established intermediaries to borrow access and credibility.
- Persistent, professional follow-up remains part of the workflow because silence and rejection are normal.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Distributor-heavy workflows can simplify platform operations while diluting filmmaker leverage and margins.
- Strong gatekeeping may reduce delivery failure for buyers while making access much harder for first-time filmmakers.
- The vault still lacks primary-source detail on windows, contracts, recoupment, and downstream revenue mechanics.
