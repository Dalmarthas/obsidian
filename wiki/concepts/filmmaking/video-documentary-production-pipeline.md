# What It Is

Video documentary production pipeline is the end-to-end workflow that turns a topic into a researched, visual, edited explainer or documentary. In the current vault, the clearest model comes from Johnny Harris: a repeatable sequence of packaging promise, reporting questions, research dossier, visual-first script, asset and art-direction planning, fact-checking, filming, rough assembly, iterative cuts, and final polish.

# Why It Matters

- It prevents video craft from being reduced to either writing or editing alone.
- It shows how research, marketing promise, visual grammar, and factual rigor have to be coordinated early.
- It gives creators a practical operating model for complex nonfiction videos.
- It clarifies why premium explainers take longer than topic selection plus narration: much of the work is in evidence design, visual planning, and iterative post-production.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-25-johnny-harris-reveals-how-he-writes-youtube-videos|Johnny Harris Reveals How He Writes YouTube Videos]] describes a pipeline that starts with pitches and a reporting brief before any final script exists.
- The researcher's information document functions as the raw material layer: facts, visuals, contexts, expert inputs, and unresolved questions are gathered before the story is locked.
- Harris's scripting week joins writing and directing. The script is not only narration, but a two-column audiovisual plan for shots, maps, animations, sound, and future editor tasks.
- The source's `shame cut -> rough cut -> fine cut -> lock` rhythm shows that the video continues to be written in the edit.
- The pipeline includes fact-checking as a production layer rather than as a final cosmetic pass.
- The pipeline also includes bespoke art direction: each story receives its own visual references, map language, typography, archival treatment, and symbolic system.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/writing-process-and-revision|Writing Process and Revision]], this concept is more medium-specific: it includes asset collection, animation, fact-checking, and edit-stage locks.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/visual-storytelling-first|Visual Storytelling First]], this concept covers the operational workflow that makes visual-first writing possible at team scale.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/anchor-bridge-structure|Anchor-Bridge Structure]], this concept is less about explainer sequence and more about the production system that discovers and builds the anchors and bridges.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- A strong pipeline can sharpen quality, but it can also become a content machine if it drains the creator's curiosity.
- Early title and thumbnail work can clarify the promise, but it may bias the inquiry if the creator becomes too attached to the packaging.
- Solo creators may need a lighter version of the pipeline: reporting brief, info document, two-column script, evidence check, and cut review may matter more than copying every team role.
