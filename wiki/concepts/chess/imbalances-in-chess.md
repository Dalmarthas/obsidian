# What It Is

Imbalances in chess are the meaningful asymmetries in a position that tell a player what kind of plan makes sense: piece-type differences, pawn-structure differences, open versus closed positions, castling asymmetry, file control, weak squares, and similar structural facts.

# Why It Matters

Without imbalances, planning becomes vague. Stronger practical play often comes from identifying what is unequal in the position and then choosing plans that make that inequality more important.
