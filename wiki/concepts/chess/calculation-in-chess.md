# What It Is

Calculation in chess is the deliberate search through candidate moves and likely responses to understand what a move actually leads to before committing to it.

# Why It Matters

Without calculation, players confuse attractive ideas with sound ideas. Calculation is what turns tactical awareness and board vision into verified decisions instead of guesses.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-chess-calculation-guide]] defines calculation as systematic search, evaluation, and anticipation of both threats and opportunities for both sides.
- The source separates a universal checklist layer from a deeper process layer built around candidate moves, falsification, and opponent-best-response thinking.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-trades-in-chess]] shows how calculation changes the value of trades through zwischenzug possibilities, tactical interruptions, and hidden structural consequences.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-essential-chess-principles]] adds the shorter heuristic version of the same discipline: do not rely on "hope chess," calculate for both sides, and keep looking for a better move after the first acceptable move appears.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-recurring-beginner-chess-mistakes]] reinforces the same calculation layer from the beginner side by showing how tactics fail when players do not test forcing replies and in-between moves before committing to a combination.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-polgar-long-term-chess-improvement-plan]] adds the long-term training prescription: mate problems, studies, slow physical-board work, and written solutions are treated as a way to build visualization and calculation discipline rather than chase shallow puzzle volume.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The current branch now distinguishes calculation from simple tactical scanning, but not yet from long strategic planning.
- Later sources may justify separating calculation depth, visualization, and candidate-move selection into distinct pages.
