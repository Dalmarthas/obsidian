# What It Is

Pawn play is the strategic management of the pawn skeleton: where pawns stand, what weaknesses they create, what space they claim, and which breaks are available.

# Why It Matters

Pieces often look active or passive because of the pawn structure around them. Pawn play gives the branch a deeper strategic layer by explaining why some plans are available and others are illusions.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-pawn-play-in-chess]] treats pawns as the building blocks of chess strategy and shows how structures guide plans, targets, and break opportunities.
- The source also emphasizes that static weakness labels are incomplete unless they are checked against actual activity and coordination.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-piece-and-pawn-coordination]] adds the simpler operational side of the same idea: pawn moves should help the pieces, and even opening placement choices such as the relation between the `c`-pawn and knight structure can shape long-term coordination.
