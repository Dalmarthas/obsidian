# What It Is

Undefended pieces are pieces that lack adequate protection and therefore become immediate tactical liabilities or practical targets.

# Why It Matters

This is one of the fastest practical filters for improving play. Many avoidable losses come not from deep strategic misunderstanding, but from failing to notice that a piece has become loose, overworked, or suddenly exposed.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-undefended-pieces]] gives the clearest current vault treatment of the concept by making two-sided scanning for loose pieces a central training habit.
- The source also stresses overprotection and "healthy paranoia" as useful practical heuristics until piece safety becomes more automatic.
- It adds the useful nuance that a loose piece is not always exploitable immediately, so the player still needs to judge access, tactical context, and whether the weakness can actually be punished.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The branch still needs a stronger source on how to balance overprotection with activity and initiative.
- Future sources may justify separating loose pieces, overloaded defenders, and overprotection into more specific tactical pages.
