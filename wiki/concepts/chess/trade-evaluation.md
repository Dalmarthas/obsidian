# What It Is

Trade evaluation is the discipline of asking whether an exchange improves or worsens the position before playing it.

# Why It Matters

Automatic trades hide a large share of club-player mistakes. A trade can relieve pressure, damage structure, lose a defender, or erase a winning attack depending on context, so it needs to be judged rather than reflexively accepted.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-gothamchess-how-to-win-at-chess]] argues that exchanges need a strategic or tactical justification rather than a vague sense of simplification.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-gothamchess-essential-chess-tips]] adds a more explicit imbalance lens: bishop-versus-knight judgments, pawn-structure differences, files, and weak squares should influence whether a trade strengthens or weakens the plan.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-gothamchess-beginner-chess-guide]] sharpens the beginner version of the same lesson: do not auto-recapture, count attackers and defenders, and notice positional consequences.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-gothamchess-beating-beginner-chess-bots]] adds the simplest practical conversion version: when the opponent is already unstable and you are ahead, simplification is often stronger than flashy continuation because it removes cheap counterplay and reduces chances to throw the game.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-trades-in-chess]] adds the strongest current positional version of the concept by centering structure, activity, bishop-versus-knight judgment, and control over the resulting trade flow.
- The branch now also has a clearer pawn-structure context for trade decisions through [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-pawn-play-in-chess]], even when the trade itself is not the explicit focus.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-essential-chess-principles]] adds the cleanest heuristic version currently in the vault: when ahead, trade pieces more willingly than pawns; when behind, trade pawns more willingly than pieces to preserve practical chances.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-piece-and-pawn-coordination]] adds a useful tempo-sensitive warning: when behind in development, an exchange can be bad not because trades are inherently wrong, but because they may solve the opponent's problems before your coordination deficit is repaired.

# Source Comparisons

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-gothamchess-how-to-win-at-chess]] frames trade discipline through middlegame plans, structural weaknesses, and endgame transitions.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-gothamchess-beginner-chess-guide]] frames it more simply through counting, piece value, defenders, and avoiding instinctive captures.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-trades-in-chess]] frames it most strategically: exchange only with a concrete reason, account for structure and activity, and recalculate for in-between moves before assuming the trade is clean.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The branch still needs more detailed material on when to trade into specific endgames or color-complex imbalances, even though the Bartholomew source improves the bishop-versus-knight layer.
- Future sources may justify splitting piece trades from pawn-structure trades.
