# What It Is

Creative problem solving is the ability to generate, test, and adapt methods when the problem cannot be handled by memorized patterns alone. In the current vault, Loh's contribution is to make novelty central: creativity grows when learners face unfamiliar problems, preserve enough struggle to invent, and then learn to evaluate whether a proposed solution actually works.

# Why It Matters

- It prevents creativity from being reduced to artistic self-expression alone.
- It gives the vault a bridge between math learning, AI-era judgment, entrepreneurship, and creative work.
- It clarifies why over-drilling can hurt transfer: the learner may become faster at known patterns while less prepared for genuinely new situations.
- It gives AI use a creativity standard: the human must still inspect, adapt, and improve generated answers.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-how-to-be-a-creative-thinker-carnegie-mellon-university-po-shen-loh]] argues that math competitions trained creativity when they forced students to confront problems they had not seen before.
- The same source criticizes test-prep systems that try to eliminate surprise by exposing students to every strange question type in advance.
- Loh's "grade the homework" AI metaphor makes evaluation part of creative problem solving: the creative act is not only producing an answer, but checking, contextualizing, and improving it.
- His matchstick-puzzle example illustrates reframing: a problem that seems impossible in a flat plane becomes solvable when the solver changes dimensional assumptions.
- The source also broadens problem solving into entrepreneurship: real-world interaction reveals constraints and pain points that purely theoretical ideation misses.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-how-to-master-ai-powered-creativity-in-just-13-minutes-jeremy-utley]] adds an AI-era anti-satisficing rule: creative problem solving should not stop at the first coherent answer, especially when AI makes coherent answers cheap.
- The Utley source also adds inspiration discipline: distinctive solutions depend partly on the user's cultivated inputs, examples, metaphors, experiences, and references.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-only-trait-for-success-in-the-ai-era-how-to-build-it-po-shen-loh]] adds generate-destroy-refine: brainstorm many unconventional approaches, attack their failure modes, and invest only in the few ideas that survive scrutiny.
- The same source adds "world simulation" as a problem-solving meta-skill: model stakeholders, incentives, biases, and consequences before acting.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-jeremy-utley-ai-ignites-human-creativity]] adds idea flow: creative problem solvers should generate enough options, including bad or wild ones, that unexpected high-quality solutions can emerge from the distribution.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-habit-that-built-every-genius]] adds a habit system around problem solving: seek unexpected input, capture bugs and observations, delay closure through white space, and then generate enough options to find non-obvious solutions.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/idea-development|Idea Development]], this concept is more problem-centered and evaluative. Idea development asks how raw creative signals become workable material; creative problem solving asks how a person finds a method under novelty and uncertainty.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/idea-flow|Idea Flow]], this concept includes the full problem-solving loop. Idea flow is specifically the divergent generation and variation layer.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/inspiration-discipline|Inspiration Discipline]], this concept is about solving under uncertainty; inspiration discipline is the upstream habit of expanding the material available for solution search.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/creative-white-space|Creative White Space]], this concept is more active and full-cycle. White space is one condition that supports deeper problem recombination.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/effective-practice|Effective Practice]], this concept is less about repetition quality and more about preserving enough unfamiliarity that practice transfers to new problems.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-critical-thinking-partner|AI Critical Thinking Partner]], this concept includes AI judgment but is not limited to AI. It also covers math, education, and entrepreneurial discovery.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-powered-creativity|AI-Powered Creativity]], this concept is broader and tool-agnostic. AI-powered creativity is the AI-specific collaboration pattern inside the wider problem-solving frame.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-era-thoughtfulness|AI-Era Thoughtfulness]], this concept is narrower and more method-focused. Thoughtfulness adds empathy, value orientation, bias handling, and social trust.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Too much novelty can overwhelm beginners before they have enough primitives to reason with.
- Too much repetition can build brittle pattern recognition without flexible transfer.
- Too much AI-generated variation can feel creative while avoiding the harder work of choosing, testing, and refining.
- Destroying ideas too aggressively can also kill fragile early possibilities before they have enough shape to be evaluated fairly.
- The vault needs more examples of how to dose novelty across different domains: math, writing, entrepreneurship, leadership, and technical work.
- Idea volume can mask indecision if the solver never selects, tests, or destroys weak candidates.
- White space can help difficult problems mature, but it can also mask avoidance if the problem has not been engaged directly first.
