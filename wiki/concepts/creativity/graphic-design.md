# What It Is

Graphic design is the shaping of type, image, space, texture, and hierarchy so that meaning starts working before explicit reading begins. In the current source base, design is not just presentation. It is emotional communication, attention-shaping, and human signal-making.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the vault a new branch for visual communication instead of treating design as a side note inside advertising.
- It clarifies that layout, cropping, white space, and typographic choices are meaning-bearing, not merely decorative.
- It creates a home for future sources on typography, art direction, editorial design, and interface aesthetics.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Carson argues that design's job begins before the reader consciously reads the text.
- Great design should intrigue, provoke, and help people enter the content.
- Readability is partly a function of desire and audience fit, not only of formal legibility.
- Intuition and risk are treated as valid design tools when they are guided by purpose.
- White space, cropping, texture, collage, and human irregularity all contribute to communication.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Anti-safe design can be memorable, but it can also fail when audience, context, or accessibility needs are ignored.
- Systems and order can clarify communication, so the question is not structure versus freedom in the abstract, but what kind of structure serves the message.
