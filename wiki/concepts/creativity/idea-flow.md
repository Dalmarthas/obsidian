# What It Is

Idea flow is the practice of generating a high enough volume and variety of candidate ideas that rare good ideas have room to appear. In this vault, the concept is distinct from inspiration: it treats creativity as a repeatable production-and-selection system rather than as a single moment of brilliance.

# Why It Matters

- It explains why "one perfect idea" pressure often underperforms repeated generation and selection.
- It gives AI-powered creativity a concrete mechanism: models are useful because they can expand the number and diversity of candidate ideas.
- It normalizes bad ideas as part of the creative distribution instead of treating them as evidence that the process is failing.
- It connects creative practice to funnel logic: many rough candidates narrow into a few prototypes, and only some prototypes become successful work.
- It gives teams an anti-favorite rule: bring multiple options into a decision rather than treating one cherished idea as the only candidate.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-jeremy-utley-ai-ignites-human-creativity]] argues that idea quantity is one of the strongest predictors of creative quality.
- The source uses examples from photography education, Dyson prototypes, Doritos Locos shell variation, and pharmaceutical discovery to show that excellent outcomes often sit inside very large search spaces.
- Utley frames bad ideas as the price of delight: a system that suppresses poor ideas too early also suppresses surprising high-end outcomes.
- The same source treats generative AI as a volume-and-variation engine that can help users sample more possibilities, roles, analogies, drafts, and angles before selecting.
- It recommends practical quantity moves: ask for 20 to 50 ideas, invite wild or poor options, regenerate multiple times, compare outputs, and then give feedback about what worked.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-habit-that-built-every-genius]] reinforces the same principle through Linus Pauling, Bob Sutton's approximate idea funnel, Astro Teller's "give me five" practice, and examples from Dyson, pharma, SNL, and Taco Bell.
- The newer source adds a team habit: when people are required to bring several ideas, the supposedly secondary or "dummy" ideas can outperform the initial favorite.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/idea-development|Idea Development]], idea flow is earlier and more generative. Idea development includes capture, testing, incubation, and refinement after raw candidates exist.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/creative-problem-solving|Creative Problem Solving]], idea flow is the divergent-search part of the process; creative problem solving also includes reframing, evaluation, and method testing.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-powered-creativity|AI-Powered Creativity]], idea flow is not inherently AI-specific. AI-powered creativity is one modern way to accelerate or diversify idea flow.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/inspiration-discipline|Inspiration Discipline]], idea flow is downstream. Inspiration discipline improves the inputs; idea flow expands the candidate set.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- More ideas do not automatically produce better outcomes if the user lacks taste, criteria, or a willingness to choose.
- Volume can become procrastination when it replaces selection, prototyping, or shipping.
- AI can increase idea flow, but it can also anchor users on plausible average outputs unless they deliberately ask for variance and critique.
- The vault needs stronger sources on how much idea volume is useful by domain before returns diminish.
- Idea quantity is not a substitute for prototyping; the funnel has to narrow at some point.
