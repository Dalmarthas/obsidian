# What It Is

Creative pitching is the craft of helping an idea survive contact with clients, executives, collaborators, and new-business situations. In the current source base, it involves more than persuasive talk: it means empathy for the listener, material that makes the idea feel real, and enough strategic clarity that unusual work stops seeming arbitrary. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-07-advertising-goodby-silverstein-masterclass-summary|Goodby Silverstein Masterclass Summary]].

# Why It Matters

- Good ideas fail if they are presented badly or to the wrong concerns.
- Pitching determines whether bold work gets protected or flattened.
- It links strategy, storytelling, and interpersonal judgment.
- It is broadly reusable outside advertising wherever creative work needs sponsors or decision-makers.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Do not present ideas as "crazy"; make sense out of them.
- Use strategy as cover and proof: if the client asked for bold, show how the idea fulfills that.
- Never tell the audience how they are supposed to feel about the work.
- Mood boards, music, GIFs, self-recorded presentations, and idea videos help clients feel the concept instead of merely hearing about it.
- Read the room, adapt to personalities, and remove what is clearly not landing.
- Approach the client as a partner or therapist: understand their actual problem rather than barging in with self-regard.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Strong presentation can make a weak idea feel stronger than it is, so rhetoric can distort judgment.
- The more polished a speculative pitch asset becomes, the easier it is for clients to mistake it for the final solution.
