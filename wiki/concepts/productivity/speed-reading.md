# What It Is

Speed reading is a set of methods for increasing reading pace without collapsing comprehension into superficial scanning. In the current vault, the concept is narrower and more practical than `read faster`: define a purpose, preview the structure, reduce fixation on individual words, use peripheral vision to capture clusters, and keep testing whether meaning survives the higher pace. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-speed-reading-by-kam-knight|Speed Reading by Kam Knight]].

# Why It Matters

- It reframes reading speed as an attention-and-strategy problem rather than a raw willpower problem.
- It adds a practical bridge between focus design and learning efficiency.
- It gives the productivity branch a durable way to discuss when faster reading improves engagement versus when it becomes disguised skimming.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source argues that many reading bottlenecks start before the eyes move:
  - vague purpose leads to wandering attention
  - wandering attention invites regression and boredom
  - boredom then gets misread as a need to slow down even more
- The source therefore treats purpose and preview as part of speed reading, not as optional preparation:
  - define a specific reading target
  - preview major structure through T.H.I.E.V.E.S.
  - let those questions determine what deserves slow attention and what can be skimmed
- The mechanical layer focuses on fixation count:
  - looking at every word separately creates unnecessary stops
  - reading can accelerate by capturing clusters of words around each fixation
  - peripheral-vision drills are meant to make this less forced over time
- The source also adds a useful caution:
  - higher pace can improve comprehension when the main problem is attention drift
  - the same claim is less trustworthy for highly technical or symbol-dense material
  - comprehension checks therefore matter more than headline speed

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/effective-practice|Effective Practice]], speed reading is narrower and task-specific. Effective practice covers learning-quality repetition broadly; speed reading applies similar quality logic to one domain: reading.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/focused-and-diffuse-learning-modes|Focused and Diffuse Learning Modes]], speed reading is more executional. Oakley's concept explains how concentration and integration alternate; speed reading explains how a reader can move through text with more purpose and less fixation drag.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/learning-pyramid|Learning Pyramid]], speed reading is earlier in the chain. It improves intake and engagement, but it does not by itself prove recall, explanation, or application.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/attention-and-focus-framework|Attention and Focus Framework]], speed reading is a narrower protocol for reading tasks. The focus synthesis covers general attentional state design; this concept applies that logic to text interaction.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- Speed gains can be real while still hiding shallow retention if the reader never checks recall or application afterward.
- The branch still needs stronger evidence on which genres benefit from higher pace and which demand deliberate slowing.
- The source treats finger-guiding techniques as low value, but the vault still lacks a stronger contrast source on when pacing tools help reduce regressions for struggling readers.
