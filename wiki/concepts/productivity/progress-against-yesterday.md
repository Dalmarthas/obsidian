# What It Is

Progress against yesterday is the practice of measuring self-improvement primarily against one's own recent prior state rather than against other people's visible outcomes. In the current vault, it functions as an anti-comparison rule that keeps motivation tied to controllable improvement instead of to unstable social rank.

# Why It Matters

- It gives the productivity branch a cleaner answer to the motivational damage caused by constant upward comparison.
- It translates `self-improvement` into a daily evaluative loop rather than leaving it as an abstract identity claim.
- It links honest self-measurement with small-course correction, making progress more operational and less performative.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-12-rules-for-life-by-jordan-peterson]] argues that comparison with stronger others often destroys confidence because the visible gap feels too large and too uncontrollable.
- The same source recommends treating yesterday's self as the relevant baseline and asking whether today's behavior is incrementally better on concrete dimensions such as health, engagement, contribution, kindness, and honesty.
- It also adds a simple rating-and-adjustment loop: score the prior day, find one concrete correction, and repeat.
- That same source makes progress against yesterday inseparable from truthfulness. Improvement becomes fake if the self-evaluation hides avoidance, misalignment, or repeated small lies.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/future-self-prioritization|Future-Self Prioritization]], this concept is shorter-horizon and more evaluative. Future-self prioritization asks what present choices serve the longer-term self; progress against yesterday asks whether today's self is actually improving relative to the immediately prior baseline.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/conscientiousness|Conscientiousness]], this page is less trait-like and more procedural. Conscientiousness describes a disposition toward order and follow-through; progress against yesterday gives that disposition a daily scoreboard.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/leadership/meaning-through-responsibility|Meaning Through Responsibility]], this concept is less existential. Responsibility explains why burden matters; progress against yesterday explains how to notice whether daily behavior is moving toward or away from a better self.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/assertiveness|Assertiveness]], this page is more internal. Assertiveness is the outward ability to say what is true; progress against yesterday is the inward loop that notices where truth has not yet been lived.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- A yesterday-based metric can still become neurotic if the person starts over-scoring, compulsively optimizing, or treating every day as a referendum on worth.
- The source gives a useful heuristic, but the vault still lacks stronger evidence on how often such self-rating improves behavior versus merely increasing self-monitoring.
- The idea protects against envy better than against aimlessness; a person can still improve against yesterday while serving the wrong long-term direction.
