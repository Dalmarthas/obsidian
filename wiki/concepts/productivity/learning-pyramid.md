# What It Is

The learning pyramid is a six-level model for turning shallow recognition into durable understanding. In the current vault, the levels are understand, recall, explain, apply, analyze, and create. The concept matters because it gives the learning branch a visible ladder for testing whether knowledge is merely familiar or genuinely usable.

# Why It Matters

- It exposes the illusion of competence that appears when learners stop at `I understand it when I see it`.
- It links memory, explanation, practice, and creation into one progression rather than treating them as unrelated study tricks.
- It gives the vault a cleaner way to distinguish early contact with material from real operational mastery.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-the-only-skill-that-matters-by-jonathan-levi]] argues that many learners stop at basic understanding and mistake that for mastery.
- The same source treats recall as the first real test because the idea must survive without prompts.
- It then adds explanation, application, and analysis as increasingly demanding checks on whether the learner can use and inspect the knowledge in the wild.
- The final `create` level makes codification itself part of learning: writing a guide, building a model, or teaching the idea reveals whether it has been internalized deeply enough to be reconstructed independently.

# Source Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/retrieval-practice|Retrieval Practice]], the learning pyramid is broader. Retrieval practice is one stage inside the climb; the pyramid maps the whole ascent from first contact to generative use.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/effective-practice|Effective Practice]], this concept is more stage-based and evaluative. Effective practice explains what good repetitions look like; the learning pyramid explains what level of command those repetitions should eventually produce.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/rapid-skill-acquisition|Rapid Skill Acquisition]], the learning pyramid is less about speed and barrier removal and more about depth-testing. Rapid skill acquisition gets the learner moving; the learning pyramid checks how far understanding has actually climbed.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/chunking|Chunking]], this page is less about memory structure and more about proof of usable command. Chunking explains how units of understanding form; the pyramid shows what learners can do once those units are stable enough to retrieve and manipulate.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The six levels are clarifying, but the exact sequence and boundaries may vary by domain.
- `Create` can sound grander than it needs to be; the useful test is often whether the learner can reconstruct and codify the model independently, not whether they produce something novel to the whole field.
- The branch still needs stronger evidence on which levels are most diagnostic in symbolic, physical, social, and creative domains respectively.
