# What It Is

Mass intimacy is the attempt to create messages for millions that still feel like they are speaking directly to one person. In the current source base, this means respecting the audience's intelligence and private context while operating at mass scale. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-07-advertising-goodby-silverstein-masterclass-summary|Goodby Silverstein Masterclass Summary]].

# Why It Matters

- It explains how broad campaigns can still feel personal rather than generic.
- It discourages condescending or overly corporate tone.
- It ties media scale to psychological specificity.
- It is a useful concept beyond advertising because it describes one of the core tensions of any public communication.

# Evidence In This Vault

- Messages should make people feel understood rather than targeted.
- Advertisers should speak to audiences as they would speak to real people they respect.
- The phone is presented as a model medium for one-to-one feeling at mass scale.
- Audience trust depends on sounding human, not managerial or manipulative.

# Tensions / Open Questions

- The more precisely a message targets someone, the easier it is for intimacy to feel invasive rather than welcome.
- Mass intimacy can become formulaic if it is reduced to tone tricks rather than rooted in real insight.
