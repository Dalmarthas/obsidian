# Question

What reusable performance model does the quiet-eye source add to the vault?

# Answer

The current source supports a five-part model:

1. Treat visual attention as part of execution.
   Seeing well in performance contexts is not just about eyesight but about how attention is stabilized at the decisive moment.

2. Stabilize the final fixation.
   Better performers often hold a longer, quieter final gaze before and during execution instead of scattering attention.

3. Train perception with movement, not separately.
   The most useful attentional skill is coupled to real task demands rather than trained as abstract concentration alone.

4. Practice under variable conditions.
   Realistic variation helps performers build attention that survives pressure rather than only repeating sterile routines.

5. Stay epistemically modest about mechanism.
   Quiet eye may support planning, correction, or simply mark deeper expertise; the training value is real even if the full causal chain is still debated.

The distinctive layer is that attention is treated as a trainable performance behavior rather than as a personality trait or generic advice to `focus harder`.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/effective-practice|Effective Practice]], this model is more specific about what focused attention looks like at the point of action instead of only saying that attention matters.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/leadership/high-performance-mindset|High-Performance Mindset]], this framework is less about courage, regulation, or motivation and more about the perceptual behavior that can make skilled action more reliable under pressure.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/chess/board-vision|Board Vision]], quiet eye is less field-wide and more point-of-execution oriented: not everything visible on the board, but the steadiness of attention at the critical moment.

# Gaps

- The branch currently rests on one secondary summary and needs direct primary research or higher-quality reviews.
- The transfer from motor performance to symbolic domains is still more suggestive than established in the current source base.
- The vault still needs a sharper comparison between gaze steadiness, broader situational scanning, and when each matters more.
