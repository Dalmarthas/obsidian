# Question

What healthier relationship model does the current anti-Hollywood-romance source propose?

# Answer

The source adds a five-part countermodel:

1. Prefer emotional quality over emotional spectacle.
   Stability, security, and mutual respect are better signs of relational health than dramatic highs and lows.

2. Respect boundaries and rejection literally.
   `No` is not an invitation to prove devotion through persistence, pressure, or theatrical pursuit.

3. Do not treat grand gestures as repair.
   Real reconciliation depends on accountability, changed behavior, and honest conversation, not cinematic displays.

4. Build a fuller self before expecting romance to complete you.
   Self-acceptance, self-improvement, friendship, and family connection create a stronger base for romantic life than rescue fantasies do.

5. Judge relationships by whether they support growth without requiring chaos.
   Mature love should make life steadier, clearer, and more expansive rather than more confusing, obsessive, or self-erasing.

# Comparisons

- Compared with mainstream movie romance, this model values calmness over volatility, repair over spectacle, and wholeness over rescue.
- Compared with purely cynical anti-romance takes, it is not dismissing love; it is arguing for less fantasy-loaded and more reality-compatible forms of love.
- Compared with the vault's self-acceptance language in [[wiki/concepts/creativity/creative-integrity|Creative Integrity]], the overlap is narrower and more personal: the point is not artistic freedom, but entering romance from less shame and less need to be saved.

# Gaps

- This synthesis is built from one secondary summary rather than a richer cross-source relationship branch.
- The vault still needs stronger material on attachment, conflict repair, and how healthy attraction differs from unhealthy instability.
- The branch also needs more sources that are not framed mainly as critiques of Hollywood.
