# Question

What reusable worldview framework does the current Sean Carroll source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a five-part naturalistic worldview model:

1. Replace purpose-loaded `why` questions with lawful patterns where appropriate.
   At the deepest level, reality is not best explained by assuming every event must have a human-style reason or intention behind it.

2. Treat everyday causality as an emergent description, not as fundamental furniture.
   Human-scale talk about causes can still be real and useful even if the deepest layer is better captured by equations, symmetries, and evolving states.

3. Let entropy explain direction, asymmetry, and the temporary rise of complexity.
   The arrow of time is what makes memory, irreversibility, development, and life itself intelligible inside a physical universe.

4. Defend higher-level vocabularies instead of collapsing everything into particle talk.
   Biology, psychology, consciousness, and ethics remain valid because explanation happens at multiple scales.

5. Ground meaning inside finite life rather than outside the universe.
   The fact that value is emergent does not make it fake. Human significance comes from conscious beings, limited time, and lived experience, not from cosmic favoritism.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/steve-jobs-stanford-framework|Steve Jobs Stanford Framework]], Carroll is less about vocational courage and more about metaphysical framing. Jobs answers how mortality can clarify a life decision; Carroll answers how mortality can still coexist with real meaning in a nonteleological universe.
- Compared with the current psychology-heavy `others` branch, Carroll contributes less interpersonal heuristics and more ontology: what kinds of explanations count, where causality belongs, and how human significance can survive scientific realism.

# Gaps

- The current branch is still built from one secondary summary rather than a denser Carroll cluster.
- The vault still needs stronger counterweights from philosophers of mind, cosmology, and religion to stress-test the naturalistic-meaning claim.
- The unresolved low-entropy beginning remains the biggest open hole in the explanatory chain.
