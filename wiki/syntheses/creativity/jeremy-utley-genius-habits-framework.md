# Question

What trainable creativity framework does Jeremy Utley's genius-habits source support?

# Answer

The source supports a four-habit creativity framework:

1. Build disciplined inspiration.
   Do not wait for random insight. Deliberately seek unexpected inputs, distant analogies, unrelated fields, lived observations, and reference material that can later recombine.

2. Capture ideas and frictions constantly.
   Use a notebook, notes app, voice memo system, or other trusted capture channel. Treat small annoyances as bug-list entries because daily friction often points toward real product, process, or policy opportunities.

3. Protect creative white space.
   Leave meaningful problems unresolved long enough for background processing. Walk, read, sleep, wander, free-write, and revisit open questions rather than forcing premature closure.

4. Generate for quantity before quality.
   Ask for many candidates, bring multiple options to meetings, include strange or weak ideas, and then select, combine, test, or discard. Rare strong ideas usually appear inside a larger distribution.

AI is a force multiplier across the framework, not the framework itself. It can supply analogies, receive raw thought dumps, cluster bug lists, produce many candidates, and combine strange options. But the user still owns taste, capture discipline, selection, testing, and the decision to leave space for problems to mature.

# Basis

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-habit-that-built-every-genius]] distinguishes creativity from raw IQ and identifies inspiration, capture, white space, and quantity as the core habits.
- The source uses MacKinnon's architect research to support the habit-over-IQ frame.
- It uses Jobs, Bezos, Beethoven, Anne Lamott, Tversky, Kahneman, Loewi, Linus Pauling, Bob Sutton, Astro Teller, Dyson, SNL, and Taco Bell examples to make the habits concrete.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-jeremy-utley-ai-ignites-human-creativity]] reinforces the quantity layer through idea flow, regeneration, bad-idea tolerance, and AI as a volume-and-variation engine.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/inspiration-discipline|Inspiration Discipline]], this framework is broader. Inspiration discipline is the input habit inside the larger system.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/creative-white-space|Creative White Space]], this framework includes white space but also capture, input design, and idea quantity.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/idea-flow|Idea Flow]], this framework includes the upstream conditions that improve what enters the idea-flow funnel.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-powered-creativity|AI-Powered Creativity]], this framework is less tool-specific. AI amplifies the habits, but the habits remain human operating practices.

# Gaps

- The framework is strong as an operating model, but the source gives limited guidance on how to fit white space into high-pressure workplaces.
- The evidence is mostly illustrative and secondary; numerical ratios such as 2,000 ideas per success should be treated as heuristics.
- The framework needs sharper limits around when to stop generating and start selecting, prototyping, or shipping.
- It also needs more examples of team-level capture and bug-list rituals that reliably convert observations into shipped improvements.
