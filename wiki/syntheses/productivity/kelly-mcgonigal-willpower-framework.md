# Question

What reusable willpower model does the current Kelly McGonigal source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a six-part model:

1. Treat willpower as a state, not just a virtue.
   Self-control gets stronger or weaker depending on sleep, stress load, physical condition, and attentional stability.

2. Support the regulating brain before demanding heroics.
   Sleep, exercise, mindfulness, and steadier routines matter because restraint depends partly on functioning executive systems.

3. Make the future self emotionally real.
   Better long-term choices become easier when present decisions feel connected to a vivid later self rather than to an abstract idea of `someday`.

4. Plan likely failure in advance.
   Anticipating where lapses will happen produces stronger behavior change than only visualizing success.

5. Recover from failure without self-attack.
   Shame multiplies lapses; self-compassion shortens them.

6. Surf urges instead of wrestling them.
   Observe cravings, breathe, and wait long enough for the urge to crest and weaken before acting.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/difficult-but-rewarding-behaviors-framework|Difficult but Rewarding Behaviors Framework]], McGonigal is less about reward-environment engineering and more about protecting self-control capacity from the inside.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/health/judson-brewer-habit-change-framework|Judson Brewer Habit Change Framework]], McGonigal still values mindfulness, but she keeps a stronger top-down self-control frame instead of making curiosity-based relearning the whole model.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/health/kelly-mcgonigal-stress-resilience-framework|Kelly McGonigal Stress Resilience Framework]], this branch is less about pressure appraisal and more about temptation, relapse, and long-horizon choice protection.

# Gaps

- The branch still needs stronger primary evidence on how robust `willpower training` claims remain across contexts.
- The vault does not yet have a dedicated concept page for lapse recovery or self-compassion, even though this source makes that mechanism important.
- More sources are needed to sort when mindful urge management beats environmental redesign and when the reverse is true.
