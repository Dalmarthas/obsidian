# Question

What reusable learning framework does the current Jonathan Levi source add to the vault?

# Answer

The current source adds a five-part superlearning framework:

1. Pair adjacent skills to generate transfer.
   Study two related skills or subskills in parallel so each reveals nuances, contrasts, and stuck points in the other.

2. Attack difficult concepts from multiple angles.
   Use several teachers, explanations, and formats until the idea clicks instead of assuming one confusing source is the final word.

3. Refuse recognition-level mastery.
   Treat `I understand it when I read it` as the floor, not the goal.

4. Force learning up the pyramid.
   Move from understanding to recall, explanation, application, analysis, and creation so each stage tests the reality of the last one.

5. Treat plateaus as design failures, not identity failures.
   When progress stalls, redesign the method by combining adjacent-skill transfer, multi-source explanation, and higher-level retrieval and creation tasks.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/barbara-oakley-learning-framework|Barbara Oakley Learning Framework]], Levi is less cognitive-state oriented and more staircase-oriented. Oakley is stronger on chunking, focused-versus-diffuse processing, and anti-procrastination entry; Levi is stronger on anti-plateau escalation from recall to creation.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/josh-kaufman-rapid-skill-acquisition-framework|Josh Kaufman Rapid Skill Acquisition Framework]], Levi is less focused on beginner-speed compression and more focused on forcing depth and perspective once practice has begun.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/robert-greene-mastery-framework|Robert Greene Mastery Framework]], Levi is less identity-and-apprenticeship heavy and more tactical. Greene is stronger on life direction and long-horizon originality; Levi is stronger on method redesign when understanding remains shallow.
- Compared with the existing learning branch overall, Levi contributes a strong anti-single-source warning: confusion may say more about the teaching angle than about the learner, so rotating sources and representations is often part of serious study rather than evidence of inconsistency.

# Gaps

- This branch currently rests on one compact secondary summary and a heuristic pyramid structure rather than strong primary or experimental support.
- The source is useful on method design but still underspecified on dosage: how much multi-learning is productive before fragmentation starts.
- The vault still needs stronger sources on which domains truly benefit from paired-skill learning and which demand stricter sequential depth.
