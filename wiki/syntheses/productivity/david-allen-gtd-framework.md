# Question

What reusable productivity framework does the current David Allen source add to the vault?

# Answer

The current source adds a seven-part GTD framework:

1. Stop using the mind as a storage device.
   Stress increases when commitments live in working memory instead of in a reliable external system.

2. Capture every open loop fast.
   Anything that triggers `I should remember this` or `I need to do something about this` belongs in an inbox, not in the head.

3. Clarify each item into an outcome and next action.
   Work becomes less sticky when each commitment is translated into the very next physical step and the larger result it serves.

4. Separate projects from actions.
   Multi-step outcomes need their own project list, while single next actions should live on executable lists.

5. Route commitments into the right containers.
   Calendar, next-action lists, follow-up lists, someday-maybe, and reference material reduce ambiguity because different kinds of work stop competing in one undifferentiated pile.

6. Rebuild trust through review cadence.
   Daily processing and weekly review keep the system current enough that the mind believes it can let go.

7. Connect immediate action back to longer horizons.
   Weekly review should move from longer-term life direction to active projects to current next actions so the system stays aligned rather than merely busy.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/daniel-pink-sustainable-productivity-framework|Daniel Pink Sustainable Productivity Framework]], Allen is less about rhythm, energy, and subtraction and more about externalizing commitments into a reliable control system.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/tim-ferriss-self-management-framework|Tim Ferriss Self-Management Framework]], Allen is less experimental and less fear-management oriented. Ferriss reduces noise through rules and constraints; Allen reduces noise by clarifying and storing open loops outside the head.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/jordan-peterson-self-management-framework|Jordan Peterson Self-Management Framework]], Allen is less values-and-meaning heavy and more operationally concrete about task capture, review, and project structure.
- Compared with the broader productivity branch, Allen contributes the strongest current anti-overwhelm mechanism: ambiguity and stress drop when commitments stop being mentally renegotiated and start living in a maintained external system.

# Gaps

- This branch currently rests on one compact secondary summary rather than Allen's primary text or higher-quality implementation sources.
- The source is much clearer on personal systems than on collaborative or interrupt-heavy environments.
- The vault still needs stronger counterweights on when GTD-style completeness becomes maintenance drag, rigidity, or avoidance theater.
