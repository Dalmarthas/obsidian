# Question

What reusable framework does the current source add for remembering what you read without drowning in detail?

# Answer

The source adds a seven-part PACER model:

1. Separate consumption from digestion.
   Reading is only intake. Retention depends on what you do after intake to transform material into something usable.

2. Stop trying to remember everything equally.
   The useful target is selective retention of what supports reasoning, application, and later problem-solving.

3. Classify what you read as you go.
   PACER turns the reading stream into five kinds of material rather than one undifferentiated pile.

4. Process each type with its own method.
   Procedural material should be practiced, analogous material critiqued, conceptual material mapped, evidence stored then rehearsed, and reference details offloaded to spaced recall.

5. Protect the `big three`.
   Procedural, Analogous, and Conceptual knowledge form the real understanding layer and should dominate the high-attention part of study.

6. Offload specifics instead of clogging the reading session.
   Evidence and Reference matter, but they should be stored quickly and reviewed later rather than monopolizing reading-time cognition.

7. Slow or stop when digestion fails.
   If you cannot do the right processing move for what you are reading, additional consumption is often fake progress.

Together these moves turn reading from passive exposure into selective knowledge construction.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/barbara-oakley-learning-framework|Barbara Oakley Learning Framework]], this synthesis is less about chunking and attentional modes, and more about classifying information during intake so the correct processing move happens immediately.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/retrieval-practice-under-pressure-framework|Retrieval Practice Under Pressure Framework]], this page is broader and earlier in the chain. Retrieval under pressure is one digestion tactic; PACER is a higher-level routing system for deciding what kind of digestion is needed.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/speed-reading-framework|Speed Reading Framework]], this model is less about moving through text efficiently and more about preventing efficient reading from degenerating into efficient forgetting.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/jonathan-levi-superlearning-framework|Jonathan Levi Superlearning Framework]], this page is less staircase-like and more classification-driven. Levi explains how to push learning upward after intake; PACER explains how to sort material so the right next step is chosen at all.

# Gaps

- The source gives a useful system, but the evidence layer for some of its quantitative claims remains thin.
- The vault still needs stronger guidance on how PACER should be adapted for domains where the line between concept, evidence, and reference is unstable.
- More material is needed on how to keep PACER from becoming over-engineered friction for exploratory or low-stakes reading.
