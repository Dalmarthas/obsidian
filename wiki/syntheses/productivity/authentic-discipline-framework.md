# Question

What reusable discipline model does the current source add beyond generic `use more self-control` advice?

# Answer

The source adds a six-part model:

1. Diagnose why discipline currently feels irrational.
   If the imagined future reward looks too similar to today's cheap leisure, the mind will often conclude that discipline is not worth the price.

2. Separate external `shoulds` from authentic wants.
   Goals imported from parents, advertising, peers, or status comparison rarely generate stable discipline once the outside pressure fades.

3. Use `Do I want to want this?` as a bridge question.
   Honest lack of current motivation is not the end of the process if there is still a real desire to become the kind of person who wants the change.

4. Build a `competing interest`.
   Lasting discipline emerges when something meaningful enough exists to rival the payoff of comfort, distraction, and fast reward.

5. Shrink the next action to a real but minimal unit.
   The point of a tiny action is not symbolic virtue. It is to create a repeatable proof of alignment with the chosen direction.

6. Treat all-or-nothing thoughts as sabotage signals.
   When the mind says the small step is pointless or that change must be total, the practical move is often to do the small action anyway.

Together these moves frame discipline less as moral force and more as a process of clarifying value, changing the reward comparison, and refusing perfectionist bargaining.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/kelly-mcgonigal-willpower-framework|Kelly McGonigal Willpower Framework]], this model is less physiological and less relapse-management oriented. It is stronger on motive selection, value conflict, and why discipline may fail before self-control is even tested.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/procrastination-defusing-framework|Procrastination Defusing Framework]], this page is less about reopening blocked options inside a stuck moment and more about making disciplined effort feel worthwhile in the first place.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/mind-training-procrastination-framework|Mind-Training Procrastination Framework]], this page is less defusion-heavy and more motivationally diagnostic. It distrusts all-or-nothing thoughts, but it still keeps a stronger emphasis on authentic desire and chosen direction.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/dopamine-regulation|Dopamine Regulation]], this framework is less about redesigning the environment around temptation and more about creating a future that beats the temptation on meaning.

# Gaps

- The current source is strong as a diagnostic and motivational heuristic, but it is still a secondary summary rather than a protocol-grade behavior-change source.
- The vault still needs sharper rules for distinguishing authentic values from temporary fascination, prestige fantasy, or self-image repair.
- More evidence is needed on when micro-actions are enough to build traction and when a larger restructuring of environment, schedule, or treatment is necessary.
