# Question

What durable persuasion framework can be extracted from the Daniel Pink masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a seven-part framework:

1. Start from service, not manipulation.
   The governing ethical test is whether the other person will actually be better off.

2. Attune before you push.
   Reduce ego, take perspective, and understand interests before offering movement.

3. Build buoyancy for rejection.
   Use interrogative self-talk, better explanatory style, and self-compassion so refusal does not stop the work.

4. Find the real problem.
   Curation and diagnosis matter more than spraying information or rushing to solutions.

5. Help people generate their own reasons.
   Agitate rather than irritate, ask readiness questions, and make action feel self-authored.

6. Remove friction.
   Social proof, specific off-ramps, and clear paths often matter more than more argument.

7. Design the moment.
   Timing, sequence, fresh starts, midpoints, and endings all change whether action happens.

- The vault still lacks primary transcript material, direct quotations, or external validation for which lessons were emphasized most strongly in the original class.

- Some research examples in the summary are illustrative rather than sufficient on their own for broad claims.
