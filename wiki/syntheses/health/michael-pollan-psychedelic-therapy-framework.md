# Question

What reusable therapeutic framework does the Michael Pollan psychedelics source add to the vault?

# Answer

The current source base supports a five-part model:

1. Treat the container as part of the treatment.
   Screening, preparation, environment, and guidance are not cosmetic. They help determine whether the experience becomes therapeutic or chaotic.

2. Use intention before intensity.
   The user should enter the experience with a clear reason for engaging it rather than treating the intervention as novelty or thrill-seeking.

3. Expect altered self-experience, not just symptom suppression.
   The source emphasizes ego-softening, reduced rumination, and changed self-perception as part of why some people find the experience meaningful.

4. Integrate after the event.
   A powerful session is not enough by itself. Reflection, discussion, and behavioral translation matter if the experience is going to affect ordinary life.

5. Stay open without becoming reckless.
   The source is optimistic about therapeutic potential, but it explicitly rejects unsupervised self-medication, weak-evidence hype, and casual microdosing certainty.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/health/kelly-mcgonigal-stress-resilience-framework|Kelly McGonigal Stress Resilience Framework]], Pollan is less about everyday reinterpretation of stress and more about intense, container-dependent altered states used therapeutically.
- Compared with the broader [[wiki/syntheses/cross-source/preventive-health-and-longevity-models|Preventive Health and Longevity Models]] synthesis, this framework is less fundamentals-first and more intervention-specific. It does not replace sleep, diet, movement, or stress hygiene; it addresses a narrower class of mental-health and existential problems.
- The branch also adds a different kind of caution than the current health sources: not mainly `does this work at all?`, but `under what conditions does a powerful intervention remain safe enough and structured enough to be therapeutic?`

# Gaps

- This framework currently rests on one secondary summary rather than primary clinical evidence or a broader Pollan branch.
- The vault still lacks clear evidence grading across indications such as depression, addiction, trauma, and end-of-life anxiety.
- The branch also needs more contrast from critics, adverse-event literature, and non-psychedelic approaches to similar therapeutic goals.
