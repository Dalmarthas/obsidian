# Question

What reusable stress-resilience model does the current Kelly McGonigal source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a five-part model:

1. Reinterpret arousal before calling it damage.
   A pounding heart and accelerated breathing can be read as the body preparing for challenge rather than failing under it.

2. Treat stress beliefs as part of the intervention.
   What people believe about stress may shape both physiology and long-term outcomes, not just their narrative about the experience.

3. Use connection as part of the stress response.
   Stress is not only about solitary defense. It can also move people toward support, empathy, and co-regulation.

4. Convert pressure into contribution.
   Helping other people under strain is framed here as protective for the helper as well as beneficial for the group.

5. Prefer meaning to comfort-only avoidance.
   A life organized around avoiding all stress may be less healthy and less fulfilling than one organized around worthwhile challenge.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/health/rhonda-patrick-sleep-protocol|Rhonda Patrick Sleep Protocol]], this source is less about environmental and circadian control and more about interpretation of pressure itself.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/leadership/high-performance-mindset|High-Performance Mindset]], McGonigal is less performance-driven and more health-driven: the goal is not just performing well under stress, but changing what stress does to the organism.
- Compared with the current longevity branch, this source contributes a nearer psychological mechanism rather than a broad prevention stack.

# Gaps

- The current branch rests on one secondary summary and needs stronger primary evidence coverage.
- The framework is most convincing for acute or meaningful stress, not obviously for burnout, trauma, or coercive chronic overload.
- The vault still needs more explicit health material on when stress reframing should give way to recovery, treatment, or environment change.
