# Question

What practical protocol for preserving cognition into old age can be extracted from the current brain-health source?

# Answer

The current branch supports a five-part protocol:

1. Do regular aerobic work.
   The source's clearest exercise prescription is about `30` minutes, `3` times weekly, around `70-75%` max heart rate.

2. Protect deep sleep.
   Good sleep matters not only for recovery but for glymphatic cleanup and lower long-run cognitive risk.

3. Consider a standard multivitamin as a low-friction support layer.
   This is the most tentative pillar, but the current source treats it as evidence-backed enough to deserve practical attention.

4. Reduce major cognitive-risk exposures.
   Avoid smoking, excess alcohol, obesity, traumatic brain injury, and chronically poor diet quality.

5. Think early, not late.
   The branch's underlying message is that brain preservation is a long-horizon prevention game, not something to start only after decline becomes obvious.

The clearest lesson in the source is that brain aging is modifiable enough to deserve routine prevention, not passive resignation.
