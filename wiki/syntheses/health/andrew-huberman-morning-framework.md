# Question

What reusable morning framework does the current Andrew Huberman source add to the vault?

# Answer

The current source supports a six-part morning model:

1. Anchor the clock first.
   Get outdoor light soon after waking so circadian timing, alertness, and mood receive a strong opening signal.

2. Hydrate before stimulating.
   Use water and possibly electrolytes as the first physiological reset rather than reaching immediately for caffeine.

3. Delay caffeine to smooth later energy.
   Wait roughly 90 to 120 minutes so wake chemistry can stabilize before adding stimulant support.

4. Protect one hard cognitive block early.
   Put the most demanding mental task before inboxes, feeds, and reactive digital fragmentation.

5. Add a repeatable physical challenge.
   Use resistance training or lighter cardio as a disciplined movement layer that supports energy and health rather than treating exercise as optional residue.

6. Use midday recovery deliberately.
   Naps or Yoga Nidra can function as a secondary reset rather than as evidence the whole routine failed.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/health/rhonda-patrick-sleep-protocol|Rhonda Patrick Sleep Protocol]], Huberman is less focused on nighttime diagnosis and more focused on using the morning to set the whole day's physiology and focus.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/daniel-pink-sustainable-productivity-framework|Daniel Pink Sustainable Productivity Framework]], Huberman is more biologically sequenced: Pink says protect prime hours; Huberman adds a routine for helping create them.
- Compared with broader self-management pages, this framework is more ordered and embodied: light, fluids, caffeine timing, hard focus, movement, then recovery.

# Gaps

- The source is secondary and compact, so its scientific confidence may be stronger in tone than in nuance.
- The framework still needs stronger contrast material for shift work, chronic illness, and lives with low schedule control.
- The vault needs more evidence on which pieces are foundational versus optional optimization layers.
