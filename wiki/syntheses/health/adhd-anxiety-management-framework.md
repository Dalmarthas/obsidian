# Question

What reusable management model does the current ADHD-anxiety source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a five-part model:

1. Separate symptom overlap from root cause.
   Do not assume racing thoughts, overwhelm, sleep disruption, or fidgeting all mean the same thing. Similar symptoms can come from executive-function weakness, threat-driven avoidance, or both.

2. Target executive function, not just motivation.
   When planning, task initiation, working memory, and inhibition are weak, the problem is not solved by more self-criticism. It is solved by designing supports around fragile steps.

3. Externalize regulation before overload escalates.
   Use calendars, checklists, written thought-dumping, visible cues, routines, and deliberate pause buffers so the brain does not have to hold everything internally at once.

4. Borrow structure socially.
   Use a `pit crew` approach: therapy, coaching, body doubling, or simply talking thoughts out loud can reduce chaos and restore sequence when solo regulation breaks down.

5. Slow decisions and shrink the target.
   Under overwhelm, reduce the scope of action to one concrete next step, delay impulsive decisions, and use movement or mindfulness to downshift enough for reflective action to come back online.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/health/kelly-mcgonigal-stress-resilience-framework|Kelly McGonigal Stress Resilience Framework]], this model is less about reframing stress physiology and more about compensating for impaired control and avoidance loops.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/stress-mindset|Stress Mindset]], this framework is more structural and disorder-adjacent. Stress mindset changes interpretation; this framework changes support architecture.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/health/sleep-quality|Sleep Quality]], this framework treats sleep problems mainly as one amplifier inside a larger regulation problem rather than the whole intervention target.

# Gaps

- The branch still relies on one secondary summary and needs stronger primary or clinically grounded coverage.
- The current vault does not yet contain a dedicated page on exposure work, medication distinctions, or formal diagnostic boundaries for ADHD and anxiety.
- The model is practical, but its pieces likely vary in usefulness depending on whether the main problem is executive dysfunction, anxiety, trauma, sleep loss, or some combination.
