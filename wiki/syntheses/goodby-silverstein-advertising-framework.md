# Question

What reusable advertising framework emerges from the Goodby Silverstein masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a five-part framework:

1. Start from human truth.
   The work should come from real observations, cultural listening, and respect for the audience.

2. Build intimacy at scale.
   A campaign may target millions, but it should still feel like it understands one actual person.

3. Let strategy carry boldness.
   Strong creative work becomes easier to defend when it is tied to a clear brand premise or insight.

4. Make ideas tangible fast.
   Use timing maps, rough storyboards, music, mood boards, and prototype videos to give shape to work before it is expensive.

5. Treat pitching and culture as part of the craft.
   Trust, empathy, room-reading, and creative safety are not side issues; they determine whether strong work gets made.

The distinct Goodby Silverstein layer is humane non-corporate tone: the work should feel alive, popular, culturally observant, and unafraid of beauty or humor.
