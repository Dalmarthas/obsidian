# Question

What practical piece-safety framework does this vault extract from John Bartholomew's lesson on undefended pieces?

# Answer

The source supports a five-part piece-safety framework:

1. Scan the board after every move for newly loose pieces on both sides.
2. Before committing to a move, check whether it removes a defender or leaves a piece hanging.
3. Use overprotection as a practical insurance policy when blunder frequency is still high.
4. Treat opponent loose pieces as tactical invitations, not just your own loose pieces as dangers.
5. If blunders are frequent, prioritize piece safety and danger sense over more advanced study topics until the habit becomes stable.

This framework is deliberately fundamental. Its value is not sophistication, but how reliably it cuts avoidable losses and creates easy tactical gains.

# Gaps
