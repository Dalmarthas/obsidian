# Question

What trade-discipline framework does the current vault extract from John Bartholomew's teaching?

# Answer

The current source supports a six-part trade-discipline model:

1. Never trade without being able to name the benefit or at least the neutrality of the result.
2. Judge trades by their effect on structure, activity, and long-term square control, not just by material count.
3. Prefer trades that happen on your terms and worsen the opponent's resulting position.
4. Recalculate exchange sequences for zwischenzug moves and tactical interruptions before assuming the trade tree is fixed.
5. Reevaluate bishop-versus-knight trades through the actual openness and piece activity of the position.
6. Review games specifically for bad trades, not only for outright blunders.

This model sharpens the vault's existing chess branch by making trade logic more concrete, more positional, and less automatic.

# Gaps

- It also needs more material comparing human strategic trade judgment against engine-first evaluation habits.
