# Question

What principle-based chess framework does this generic overview source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source contributes a seven-part cross-phase framework:

1. Build the opening around development, center influence, king safety, and restraint with premature queen moves or repeated moves.
2. Judge pieces by activity, not just nominal value: knights, bishops, rooks, and the king all improve or worsen depending on structure and phase.
3. Treat pawn structure as long-term strategy, because captures, pawn chains, and self-inflicted weaknesses shape the rest of the game.
4. Make exchanges according to the position's score: simplify differently when ahead than when behind.
5. Convert endgames through active kings, correct rook placement, and awareness of special structural patterns such as connected passers and opposite-colored bishops.
6. Reject "hope chess" by checking both sides and looking for a better move even after finding a decent one.
7. Use principles as guides, then break them consciously when the concrete position justifies the exception.

# Basis

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-essential-chess-principles]] is unusually broad rather than narrow: it compresses opening, middlegame, endgame, and mindset rules into one source.
- Its strongest durable value is that it links separate chess ideas together instead of teaching them as isolated tips.
- The source also adds a useful meta-rule for the branch: principled play is strongest when paired with context-sensitive exceptions rather than rigid rule worship.

# Gaps

- The vault still needs stronger source coverage for deep positional play, prophylaxis, and technical endgame theory.
- The branch now has a general principle map, but still lacks enough source diversity to compare multiple named coaches on when to break or bend standard rules.
