# Question

What pawn-play framework does the current vault extract from John Bartholomew's teaching?

# Answer

The current source supports a six-part pawn-play model:

1. Read the pawn skeleton before choosing a plan.
2. Treat isolated, doubled, and backward pawns as structural facts with both weaknesses and possible dynamic compensation.
3. Coordinate pieces and development before trying to transform the structure.
4. Use pawn breaks to open lines and challenge the opponent's setup when you are prepared for the consequences.
5. Attack hooks and structural targets rather than pushing pawns mechanically.
6. Judge pawn decisions by the position they create, not by dogma about "good" or "bad" structure.

This model strengthens the current chess branch by making the long-term structure of the position legible rather than leaving strategy as a vague backdrop behind tactics and calculation.

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-pawn-play-in-chess]] provides the strongest current source in the vault on pawn structures and pawn breaks as a standalone subject.

# Gaps
