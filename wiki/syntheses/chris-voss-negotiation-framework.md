# Question

What durable negotiation framework can be extracted from the Chris Voss masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current Voss branch supports a nine-part framework:

1. Regulate state before substance.
   Tone matters before arguments do. Use calm, low-friction delivery so the other person can actually hear and think.

2. Start with tactical empathy.
   Understand the other side's perspective without surrendering your own position or pretending agreement.

3. Demonstrate understanding in layers.
   Mirror, label, paraphrase, and summarize until the counterpart feels accurately understood, ideally strongly enough to give a `that's right`.

4. Let silence do work.
   After a mirror, label, summary, or calibrated question, stop talking and let the other person elaborate or react.

5. Use calibrated `what` and `how` questions.
   Make the other side think through implementation, tradeoffs, and reality instead of defending themselves against accusations.

6. Make `no` safe.
   No-oriented questions reduce suspicion, preserve autonomy, and often produce more honest movement than pressured `yes`.

7. Preempt the negative story.
   Use accusation audits before hard asks or bad news so the unspoken fears are named before they harden into resistance.

8. Treat strategic pressure points carefully.
   `Fair`, deadlines, and loss aversion are not minor details. They are emotional levers that should be handled with empathy, specificity, and skepticism about manipulation.

9. Hunt for black swans and practice progressively.
   Hidden information can change the whole deal, and the skill set should be drilled first in low-stakes workplace situations before high-stakes use.
