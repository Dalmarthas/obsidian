# Question

What durable negotiation framework can be extracted from the Chris Voss masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part framework:

1. Start with tactical empathy.
   Understand their perspective without surrendering your own.

2. Gather information before making your case.
   Influence improves when you know what the other side actually values, fears, or hides.

3. Use verbal tools that invite elaboration.
   Mirror, label, and then stop talking.

4. Ask calibrated `what` and `how` questions.
   Let the other side feel in control while still doing the work of problem-solving.

5. Make `no` safe.
   Honest resistance is often more useful than pressured agreement.

6. Hunt for black swans.
   Unknown unknowns often matter more than the visible bargaining surface.
