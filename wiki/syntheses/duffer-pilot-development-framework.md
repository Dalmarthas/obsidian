# Question

What reusable television-pilot framework emerges from the Duffer Brothers masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part operating model:

1. Find the right big idea.
   Choose a concept with real personal energy, simple clarity, and a durable central drive.

2. Stay zoomed out as long as possible.
   Move from idea to paragraph to blue sky to outline in gradual steps instead of prematurely drafting scenes.

3. Design the pilot around engine, not only event.
   The pilot must prove the recurring machine of the series, not just resolve one dramatic incident.

4. Build characters and world directionally.
   Characters need A-to-B movement, and the world should be vivid enough to sustain future episodes without overbuilding lore.

5. Outline in layers.
   Story outline, scaffolding outline, and final outline should each catch different categories of structural failure.

6. Back out when something breaks.
   Structural, character, or pacing problems should be fixed at the level where they originate rather than brute-forced inside script pages.

The distinctive Duffer layer is anti-chaos discipline: the faster route is often slower at first because it respects development stages.

# Gaps

- This framework rests on a secondary summary rather than primary class material.
- It is strongly derived from the Stranger Things development story and may need counterexamples from less outline-heavy TV creators.
- The vault still lacks primary pilot scripts as test cases for this model.
