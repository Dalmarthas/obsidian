# Question

What collaboration framework does the current vault extract from Jeremy Utley's AI creativity and productivity talk?

# Answer

The current sources support a twelve-part collaboration framework:

1. Treat the model like an eager intern that needs coaching, not like a perfect oracle.
2. Supply rich context so the task is actually understandable in your real situation.
3. Use examples to anchor tone, structure, or logic more precisely.
4. Ask the model to request missing information before answering when the brief is incomplete.
5. Use role assignment when you need a specific lens, standard, or domain frame.
6. Use roleplay simulation when you need rehearsal, not just advice.
7. Ask for critique, assumption checks, and alternatives so the model sharpens rather than replaces your judgment.
8. Iterate through artifacts: profiles, examples, drafts, transcripts, scorecards, and revised instructions.
9. Let the model interview you about workflows, responsibilities, KPIs, and objectives before deciding where AI belongs.
10. Push past first-pass adequacy by demanding volume, variation, wild alternatives, metaphors, and counterintuitive options before filtering.
11. Use voice to capture unsynthesized thinking, then let AI structure the material into drafts, plans, and follow-ups.
12. Regenerate, compare, and respond with explicit feedback so the model becomes a creative sampling partner rather than a one-shot answer source.

The central shift is from command-writing to collaboration design. Utley's stronger claim is that the best users are not merely prompt writers; they are coaches and creative directors who know how to get useful work out of another intelligence while staying responsible for judgment, taste, and selection.

# Basis

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-jeremy-utley-ai-creativity-and-productivity]] frames effective AI use as coaching and collaboration rather than technical wizardry.
- The source emphasizes context engineering, reverse prompting, few-shot examples, role assignment, and iterative critique as the highest-leverage techniques.
- It also makes the practical claim that human imagination and briefing quality often limit results more than model capability does.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-5-step-playbook-for-10x-your-ai-productivity]] deepens the same model with clearer treatment of AI's yes-man tendency, critical-thinking preservation, roleplay as a conversation flight simulator, and multi-stage handoffs between profiler, counterpart, and evaluator roles.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-how-to-master-ai-powered-creativity-in-just-13-minutes-jeremy-utley]] adds the teammate frame, AI expert consultation prompt, non-technical workflow prototyping, inspiration-as-input discipline, and anti-satisficing rule that creative users should ask for more than the first coherent answer.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-jeremy-utley-ai-ignites-human-creativity]] adds the idea-flow mechanism behind that anti-satisficing rule: quantity, variance, bad-idea tolerance, voice capture, regeneration, and feedback are how users turn AI from oracle into creative collaborator.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/ai-powered-creativity|AI-Powered Creativity]], this synthesis is broader and more workflow-oriented. The concept page names the creativity-specific pattern; this framework includes the surrounding collaboration behaviors that make the pattern repeatable.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/ai/reverse-prompting|Reverse Prompting]], the new source expands elicitation from "ask clarifying questions about this task" to "interview me about my work so we can discover where AI should be used at all."
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/creativity/idea-flow|Idea Flow]], this synthesis is the broader AI-collaboration operating model. Idea flow names one central creative mechanism inside it.

# Gaps

- The branch still needs more evidence on which collaboration techniques work best by task type and by model family.
- It also needs stronger comparisons between collaboration-first prompting and more automated workflow architectures.
- The newer source's chain-of-thought language should be translated cautiously for current assistants: users may need concise rationale, assumptions, checks, or decision criteria rather than private hidden reasoning.
- The newest source's quantitative productivity and labor-savings claims are useful as examples, but the underlying methods are not documented in the summary and should not be treated as independently verified.
- The idea-flow source is persuasive on creativity practice but thin on governance, privacy, and compliance; organizational rollout needs those layers separately.
