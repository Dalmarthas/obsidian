# Question

What reusable framework does the current Harari source add to the vault for thinking about AI, governance, and self-knowledge?

# Answer

The source adds a six-part framework:

1. Focus on decision environments, not just tool power.
   The central issue is not merely that machines get more capable, but that they may shape the conditions under which humans choose.

2. Treat AI plus biology as the real discontinuity.
   Data about bodies, emotions, and vulnerability makes technological power more intimate than earlier automation waves.

3. Assume old political stories will strain under new conditions.
   Liberal-democratic ideas of autonomy and informed choice become harder to defend when systems model people asymmetrically well.

4. Interrogate the fictions that organize society.
   Nations, money, corporations, and religions coordinate humans, but they must remain subordinate to the suffering of real beings.

5. Build self-knowledge as civic defense.
   Meditation, therapy, reflection, and emotional literacy are not just private wellness tools. They are protections against manipulation.

6. Match global problems with global coordination.
   Climate risk, nuclear danger, and technological disruption are structurally larger than nationalist problem-solving frames.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/ai/kasparov-human-machine-collaboration-framework|Kasparov Human-Machine Collaboration Framework]], Harari is less workflow-oriented and more civilizational. Kasparov asks how to work with AI well; Harari asks what kinds of societies AI may produce.
- Compared with the current AI foundations branch, Harari adds less technical detail and more governance, media, and moral risk.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/others/naturalistic-meaning|Naturalistic Meaning]], Harari is less interested in defending meaning inside a physical universe and more interested in defending autonomy and compassion inside manipulated information environments.

# Gaps

- The current branch still needs stronger sources on concrete policy, institutional design, and democratic countermeasures.
- The source is diagnostically powerful but lighter on implementation than on warning.
- The vault still lacks a dedicated concept page for attention economy or hacked attention, even though that mechanism matters here.
