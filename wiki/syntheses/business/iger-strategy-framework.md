# Question

What reusable business-strategy framework emerges from the Bob Iger masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part model:

1. Pick a few real priorities.
   Strategy should narrow attention to a small number of choices that actually govern capital and time.

2. Articulate strategy relentlessly.
   Clear repetition, face-to-face explanation, and constant reinforcement matter as much as the strategy itself.

3. Invest where future value is forming.
   Put resources into creativity, enabling technology, and global reach before disruption forces reactive decisions.

4. Negotiate with candor and reduced ego.
   Reveal genuine interests, aim for mutual gain, and build trust quickly enough to move big deals forward.

5. Steward brands instead of flattening them.
   Protect the essence of powerful brands while adapting them to changing markets and distribution structures.

6. Embrace thoughtful risk.
   Status quo is dangerous in dynamic markets, but risk should still be informed, scenario-aware, and responsibly chosen.

The distinctive Iger layer is strategic modernization: respect what made the institution valuable, but do not mistake reverence for leadership.
