# Question

What durable execution framework does the current 4DX source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source compiles into a five-part execution model:

1. Name the one priority that must not get buried.
   The `Whirlwind` is unavoidable, so the first move is not trying to eliminate urgent work but explicitly protecting one wildly important goal from it.

2. Define the goal in a way that makes completion unmistakable.
   `From X to Y by Z` matters because it forces a baseline, target, and deadline instead of vague aspiration.

3. Win through lead behaviors, not only outcome watching.
   Lag measures tell you whether the goal is happening; lead measures tell you what to do this week. Good execution depends on choosing the few controllable actions that actually drive the result.

4. Make the score legible enough to change daily behavior.
   A scoreboard works here not as reporting theater but as a motivational device. People engage more when they can tell quickly whether they are winning.

5. Install a weekly accountability cadence.
   Execution survives ordinary chaos when there is a recurring rhythm of commitment, report, and recommitment. The meeting is not the work, but it protects the work from disappearing.

The distinctive contribution of 4DX is not any single part in isolation. It is the way focus, metrics, visibility, and accountability are bound into one operating loop that can coexist with day-to-day turbulence.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/scalable-business-systems-framework|Scalable Business Systems Framework]], 4DX is narrower and more behavioral. The scalable-systems synthesis is stronger on mapping the machine; 4DX is stronger on protecting one critical priority inside the machine.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/seth-godin-strategy-framework|Seth Godin Strategy Framework]], 4DX is less philosophical and less about game selection. It assumes the direction has already been chosen and focuses on execution under daily pressure.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/simon-sinek-success-metrics-framework|Simon Sinek Success Metrics Framework]], 4DX is more operational and motivational. Sinek is stronger on incomplete scoreboards and values; 4DX is stronger on how a simple scoreboard can drive weekly behavior when the right metrics are chosen.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/john-doerr-okr-framework|John Doerr OKR Framework]], 4DX is stricter and simpler: one WIG, a few lead measures, and weekly accountability. Doerr is stronger on stretch calibration, mixed quality-and-quantity key results, and review of whether the target itself is ambitious enough.

# Gaps

- The current source is strongest on operational follow-through, not on choosing the right strategy in the first place.
- It assumes the lead measures are valid; the vault still needs stronger material on how to test whether they are genuinely causal.
- The framework may be too narrow for settings where multiple experiments must run in parallel or where one public scoreboard distorts necessary nuance.
