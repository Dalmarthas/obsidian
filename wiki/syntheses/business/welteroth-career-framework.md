# Question

What reusable career-design framework emerges from the Elaine Welteroth masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a five-part model:

1. Start with excavation.
   Understand the deeper patterns of what energizes you, what you value, and what you have always been pulled toward.

2. Use the zone of genius as a compass.
   Look for the overlap of passion, talent, values, and skills rather than chasing prestige alone.

3. Design the career as an enterprise.
   Treat your life as a multi-lane system with a coherent purpose instead of a single-track ladder.

4. Translate vision into blueprint and mind map.
   Create a practical architecture that connects why, industries, projects, relationships, and timing.

5. Build confidence before the leap.
   Use financial planning, side tests, community support, and selective yes/no filtering to reduce reckless improvisation.

The distinct Welteroth layer is permission: reinvention, difference, and self-authorship are not detours from success but ingredients of it.

# Gaps

- This framework rests on a secondary summary rather than primary class material.
- The approach is tuned to creative and media-adjacent careers, so other domains may need stronger institutional translation.
- The branch still needs complementary sources on execution under lower-optionality conditions and on how teams scale around a multi-hyphenate founder.
