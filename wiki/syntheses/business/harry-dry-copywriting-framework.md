# Question

What reusable copywriting framework can be extracted from the current Harry Dry source?

# Answer

The current branch supports a six-part framework:

1. Start from a real audience-state change.
   Know what the reader thinks now, what they should think next, and what friction sits between those two states.

2. Make the line visualizable.
   If the reader cannot picture it, they are less likely to remember or feel it.

3. Make the line falsifiable.
   Claims that can be checked against reality carry more trust and weight than soft abstractions.

4. Say something only you can say.
   Strong copy depends on real differentiation, not just polished phrasing.

5. Build from facts, then shape them.
   Facts, distinctions, and observable details are often the strongest raw material for structure, credibility, and emotion.

6. Revise in the real medium.
   Write where the copy will actually live, use layout and design context early, and improve through multiple versions and feedback.

The source's clearest lesson is that memorable writing is rarely born from ornamental cleverness. It comes from concrete truth arranged with enough taste and structure to stick.

# Gaps

- The current source is compact and secondary rather than a full masterclass or archive of examples.
- The branch still needs stronger treatment of long-form sales pages, email sequences, and pricing or offer mechanics.
- The vault also needs additional writing sources that either confirm or challenge the strength of the falsifiability rule outside business copy.
