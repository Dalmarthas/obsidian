# Question

What reusable validation framework does the Lean Startup source add to the vault?

# Answer

The current Eric Ries branch now supports an eight-step model:

1. Start with the vision, but isolate the riskiest assumption.
   Do not try to validate the whole dream at once. Identify the belief that would most damage the business if it proved false.

2. Define the startup by uncertainty, not by size.
   The method is for any institution creating something new under extreme uncertainty, including internal innovation teams inside larger companies.

3. Build the smallest credible test for that assumption.
   The MVP is not a ceremonial mini-product. It is the cheapest serious instrument that can expose whether the assumption survives contact with reality.

4. Prefer behavior over opinion.
   Purchases, pre-orders, sign-ups, or other commitments matter more than compliments, interviews, or social encouragement.

5. Use low-infrastructure testing modes when possible.
   Concierge MVPs test value manually. Smokescreen MVPs test demand before full buildout.

6. Reduce the time through the build-measure-learn loop.
   Speed matters because every cycle gives the team another chance to discover reality before resources are gone.

7. Use innovation accounting instead of vanity accounting.
   Learning still needs accountability. The team should define the milestone, measure actionable movement, and reject dashboards that mainly reward motion or presentation quality.

8. Pivot or persevere, then repeat.
   Learning is cyclical. Each round should either strengthen confidence in the direction or force a meaningful change in approach.

# Gaps

- The branch now has two secondary summaries, but it still rests on secondary material rather than direct Ries texts.
- The vault still needs stronger counterexamples from markets where lightweight MVP tests are less feasible.
- The current branch is stronger on startup experimentation than on how mature organizations should know when to stop experimenting and shift into normal operating discipline.
