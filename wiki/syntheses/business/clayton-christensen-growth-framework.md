# Question

What reusable growth framework does the Clayton Christensen source add to the vault?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part model:

1. Start with the job, not the segment.
   Real growth begins by understanding the progress customers are trying to make in a specific situation.

2. Separate innovation types instead of calling everything innovation.
   Sustaining, disruptive, efficiency, and still-potential innovations create very different outcomes and should not be judged by the same logic.

3. Treat disruption as a business-model move, not just a technology move.
   Growth often comes from making something accessible to non-consumers or overlooked users, not merely from making the existing premium offer better.

4. Watch what your metrics are teaching the company to ignore.
   Ratio-based finance can quietly push managers toward safer efficiency choices and away from the kinds of bets that create new demand.

5. Balance the portfolio across time horizons.
   Sustaining and efficiency innovations matter, but they cannot be the only categories that receive talent, money, and legitimacy.

6. Use theory consciously.
   Managers always act from causal models. Better strategy comes from making those models explicit enough to test and revise.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/iger-strategy-framework|Iger Strategy Framework]], Christensen is less focused on a small set of executive priorities and more focused on the causal machinery that either enables or blocks future growth.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/lean-startup-validation-framework|Lean Startup Validation Framework]], Christensen is less about rapid experiment sequencing and more about identifying what kind of innovation is being pursued and why existing incentives may reject it.
- Iger is stronger on repeated strategic articulation at scale.
- Ries is stronger on testing risky assumptions quickly.
- Christensen is stronger on why companies systematically misallocate capital away from market-creating innovation in the first place.

# Gaps

- This framework currently rests on one secondary summary rather than a deeper Christensen branch.
- The source strongly criticizes common finance metrics, but the vault still lacks a well-developed positive alternative for long-horizon innovation accounting.
- The jobs-to-be-done layer is strong conceptually, but the current branch still needs more concrete case material from outside the canonical milkshake example.
