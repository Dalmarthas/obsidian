# Question

What reusable sales framework does the current source add to the vault?

# Answer

The current source base supports a nine-part modern sales model:

1. Optimize speed to lead before polishing the pitch.
   A fresh lead is a perishable asset, so response-time design is one of the highest-leverage conversion levers.

2. Design the calendar as part of the close.
   Slot spacing, immediate-call paths, same-day pull-forwards, and weekend availability change show rates and utilization before the rep says anything.

3. Treat show rate as a core performance metric.
   Reminder sequences, prep artifacts, and better slot timing can lift total revenue by improving attendance, not only close rate.

4. Route opportunities asymmetrically.
   Better-qualified or hotter leads should not be distributed randomly when stronger reps can extract much more value from them.

5. Train the team through repeatable loops.
   Huddles, role-play, one-on-ones, script discipline, and game-tape review turn closing from heroics into a teachable system.

6. Run the call as diagnosis plus structured movement.
   The CLOSER sequence turns the sale into a repeatable path from motive and pain to offer fit, concerns, and reinforced commitment.

7. Handle objections by chunking up from details to real decision friction.
   Instead of blind answers, find the underlying concern and use reusable patterns such as AAA, scale questions, and best-case/worst-case prompts.

8. Join sales, marketing, and customer success into one machine.
   The sale starts before the call and continues after payment, so handoff quality, expectation alignment, and retention belong inside the selling system itself.

9. Control the live sales conversation without losing buyer fit.
   The Belfort source adds the call-level layer: establish authority quickly, keep the conversation on a purposeful line, build certainty through logic and emotion, and treat objections as uncertainty to diagnose. The ethical limiter is that not every `no` is a barrier to break; some are evidence of poor fit or legitimate refusal.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/scalable-business-systems-framework|Scalable Business Systems Framework]], this model is narrower and more revenue-engine specific. The older synthesis explains the machine in general; this one shows what that machine looks like inside a call-driven sales organization.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/freymvork-prodazhi-grebenyuka|Фреймворк продаж Гребенюка]], this source is more explicit about speed-to-lead, calendar architecture, show-rate economics, and sales-to-CS handoff. The Grebenyuk branch is stronger on Russian-language stage logic and on the conversational structure of diagnosis, qualification, pre-close, and objection handling.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/communication/chris-voss-negotiation-framework|Chris Voss Negotiation Framework]], this framework is less about empathy sequencing and hidden leverage discovery, and more about system design around the call plus standardized objection movement.

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/jordan-belfort-straight-line-sales-framework|Jordan Belfort Straight Line Sales Framework]], this synthesis is broader and more operational. Belfort supplies the live persuasion mechanics inside the call, while this page connects that call to lead response, calendar design, routing, training, and handoff quality.

# Gaps

- The source is still one secondary summary rather than a broader base of sales-operations research and multi-industry comparisons.
- Its strongest fit is high-ticket, appointment-based selling; the vault still needs better treatment of enterprise, channel, and product-led sales models.
- The ethics layer is still incomplete: the source is strong on performance mechanics but thinner on how to distinguish responsible structured selling from manipulative process control.
- The Belfort layer sharpens that ethics gap because conversation control and `no means not certain yet` can improve diagnosis, but can also normalize pressure when the buyer's refusal should be accepted.
