# Question

What reusable career-and-life framework does the current Steve Jobs Stanford source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a five-part framework:

1. Trust retrospective coherence.
   Not every meaningful step will look rational in advance. Some detours only reveal their value later, so uncertainty has to be endured before the pattern is visible.

2. Follow the work with real charge.
   Love of the craft matters because it keeps the search alive and gives setbacks something stronger to collide with than pure status ambition.

3. Use major setbacks as reset points.
   Loss, rejection, and forced exits can strip away dead identity layers and restore beginner energy if they are used for reinvention rather than for bitterness.

4. Let mortality clarify the filter.
   Awareness of death reduces the power of embarrassment, dogma, and external expectations, making it easier to act on what actually matters.

5. Stay hungry and stay foolish.
   Curiosity, unfinishedness, and willingness to look unpolished are treated as advantages, not as beginner flaws to eliminate too quickly.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/welteroth-career-framework|Welteroth Career Framework]], Jobs is less operational and less financially grounded. Welteroth is stronger on building an actual plan; Jobs is stronger on courage, trust in detours, and identity-level permission to keep searching.
- Compared with the current high-performance branch, Jobs is less about routines and repeatable excellence, and more about existential filtering, reinvention after loss, and preserving aliveness in the work.
- Compared with the vault's authenticity branch, Jobs turns authenticity into a harder decision rule: do not let others' expectations write the life for you.

# Gaps

- The current source is inspirational and compact, but much lighter on concrete systems for ordinary career constraints, money, or institutional responsibility.
- It still needs stronger counterweights on survivorship bias, privilege, and the risk of over-romanticizing intuition.
- The branch would benefit from future sources on vocation, calling, and late-stage reinvention that are less founder-centric.
