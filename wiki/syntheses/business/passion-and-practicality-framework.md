# Question

What reusable career-design framework does the current source add to the vault's passion-versus-practicality branch?

# Answer

The source adds a seven-part model:

1. Reject the binary between passion and practicality.
   Good career design often combines both rather than choosing one permanently.

2. Replace `find your passion` with `develop your passion`.
   Interest usually deepens through repeated exposure, competence, and real engagement rather than appearing as instant certainty.

3. Prefer harmonious passion over obsessive passion.
   Deep care is useful when it remains integrated with the rest of life; identity-fused passion raises burnout, fragility, and `never enough` pressure.

4. Build enough stability to think clearly.
   Chronic money stress narrows cognitive bandwidth, so a workable base of safety often improves rather than delays meaningful work.

5. Run cheap tests before identity-level leaps.
   Freelance projects, internships, volunteering, selling to peers, and deadline-based trials give better evidence than fantasy alone.

6. Respect market structure.
   Power-law careers require harsher realism about odds, aptitude, timing, and luck than more abundant-skill fields.

7. Use adjacent paths and explicit decision points.
   A nearby role, a fixed trial horizon, or a hybrid day-job-plus-passion structure can preserve both runway and regret minimization better than pure `all in` or pure surrender.

Together these moves reframe passion as something to cultivate under constraints rather than something to worship or suppress.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/cal-newport-career-capital-framework|Cal Newport Career Capital Framework]], this model is less anti-passion and more passion-shaping. Newport is stronger on earning leverage through rare skill; this source is stronger on how passion should evolve so that leverage-building does not become either fantasy or joyless misfit.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/welteroth-career-framework|Welteroth Career Framework]], this page is less permission-centered and more risk-adjusted. Welteroth is stronger on self-authorship and reinvention; this source is stronger on stability, power-law realism, and testing the professional version of the work.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/naval-ravikant-life-design-framework|Naval Ravikant Life Design Framework]], this model is more vocational and less philosophical. Naval is stronger on desire filtration and freedom; this page is stronger on adjacent career moves, aptitude realism, and the distinction between harmonious and obsessive passion.

# Gaps

- The source introduces harmonious versus obsessive passion as a strong distinction, but the vault still lacks a wider source cluster on passion psychology.
- The branch needs more material on how to estimate aptitude realistically without collapsing into discouragement or prestige metrics.
- Future sources should sharpen how family duty, debt, disability, or immigration constraints change the recommended level of career risk.
