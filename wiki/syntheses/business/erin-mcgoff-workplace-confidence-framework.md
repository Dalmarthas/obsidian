# Question

What reusable workplace-confidence framework does the current Erin McGoff source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a seven-part work-confidence model:

1. Build confidence from self-talk before performance.
   Confidence is treated less as hype and more as stable inner narration: speak to yourself like a supportive but honest friend.

2. Answer the issue behind the issue.
   Many workplace questions are proxies. The useful answer speaks to the hidden concern underneath the stated prompt.

3. Use self-awareness, not confession, in interviews.
   The `10/90 rule` turns weakness answers into proof of judgment and growth: briefly name the gap, then spend most of the answer on how you are improving it.

4. Treat passive aggression as a clarity problem first.
   Before assuming hostility, ask curious clarifying questions that force either ownership or clarification without immediate escalation.

5. Quit as a long-horizon professional.
   Even when leaving a bad environment, the default model is dignity, brevity, and reputation protection rather than cathartic destruction.

6. Ask for more pay like a business case, not a grievance.
   Bring market data, think from managerial constraints, and enroll your boss as an ally who can advocate upward.

7. Sound more confident by aligning wording with conviction.
   Cleaner phrases such as `I'm confident` and `and I also know` make competence and sustainability easier to hear than apologetic or internally split language.

Together these moves define workplace confidence as compassionate self-command plus strategically framed professional communication.
