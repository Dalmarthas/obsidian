# Question

What reusable practice framework does Seth Godin's Impact Theory source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source supports a twelve-part practice framework:

1. Treat the practice as the output.
   The controllable win is showing up, making, learning, and shipping. Outcomes matter, but they are too noisy to serve as the daily operating center.

2. Build streaks that bypass mood.
   A visible 30- to 100-day streak makes the work less dependent on inspiration, confidence, or immediate proof.

3. Define the intended change.
   Every serious project should answer: who is it for, what is it for, and what change does it seek to make?

4. Put the work on the hook.
   Shipping is generous exposure: the maker offers something that might help a specific group while accepting that it might not work.

5. Reject industrial compliance scripts.
   `Will this be on the test?`, platform likes, status games, and fake urgency all train attention away from the work that compounds.

6. Stay peculiar enough to matter.
   The maker should not become an interchangeable script-follower. Peculiarity becomes strategic when it is useful to a real audience.

7. Keep commerce real without becoming a hack.
   Income matters, but formulas, vanity metrics, and applause-chasing become dangerous when they make the maker serve the scoreboard instead of the audience.

8. Let behavior update identity.
   Act like the kind of person who writes, ships, paints, sells, teaches, or leads; the self-story follows repeated behavior more reliably than behavior follows a perfect self-story.

9. Choose passion as a narrative.
   Passion is less something found in advance and more a story people learn to tell about work they have committed to doing with care.

10. Develop taste through new mistakes.
   Rejections, failed proposals, and imperfect launches are useful when they teach what the field, gatekeepers, and audience actually value.

11. Filter feedback aggressively.
   Listen to intended audiences, trusted peers, aligned experts, and people who can help the work get better. Ignore mass noise that only reveals the reviewer's preferences.

12. Choose sustaining metrics.
   Contribution, learning, depth of engagement, trusted referrals, and work one is proud to repeat are safer measures than bestseller labels, billionaire rankings, follower counts, or infinite status holes.

The compact model: put focus into a generous, audience-specific practice that compounds identity, taste, trust, and usefulness; protect that practice from metrics and feedback loops that reward becoming a hack.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/seth-godin-remarkable-marketing-framework|Seth Godin Remarkable Marketing Framework]], this page is less about the market-facing system and more about the maker-facing practice that creates the work.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/seth-godin-strategy-framework|Seth Godin Strategy Framework]], this page is less about systems, time horizons, and games, and more about daily creative behavior under uncertainty.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/productivity/robert-greene-mastery-framework|Robert Greene Mastery Framework]], Godin is less vocational and apprenticeship-heavy and more focused on shipping, audience, feedback filtering, and generosity.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/productivity/deliberate-practice|Deliberate Practice]], Godin's practice is broader than weakness-targeted skill improvement. It includes identity, audience, taste, generosity, and commercial posture.

# Gaps

- The framework is based on a secondary video summary rather than a full transcript or primary book text.
- The source is strongest for creative, entrepreneurial, educational, and audience-facing work; transfer to safety-critical or highly standardized domains needs caution.
- The passion-as-choice claim is useful as an agency lever but can understate constraints, temperament, health, and real economic pressure.
- The framework still needs harder operating rules for when persistence should stop because the audience, economics, or strategy are not working.
