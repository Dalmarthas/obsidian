# Question

What reusable strategy-and-leadership framework does the current Steve Jobs focus source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a six-part framework:

1. Define focus as refusal.
   Strategy becomes real when leaders say `no` to many attractive directions rather than trying to preserve every promising initiative.

2. Judge the portfolio, not just the project.
   Good local work can still produce a bad company-level outcome if the overall mix is incoherent or scattered.

3. Distinguish micro logic from macro logic.
   A project may make sense to its own team yet fail against ecosystem trends, product architecture, or the wider company direction.

4. Protect integration over proliferation.
   The aim is not a large number of decent products, but a small set of coherent offerings where the whole exceeds the sum of the parts.

5. Accept the social cost of pruning.
   Leaders have to absorb anger, bad press, and one-sided narratives when they kill misaligned work, without scapegoating publicly or becoming defensive.

6. Treat management quality as directional discipline.
   Talent quality is not enough. Leadership earns its keep by converging talent behind a coherent path rather than letting good people fragment themselves.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/steve-jobs-stanford-framework|Steve Jobs Stanford Framework]], this source is less existential and more managerial. The Stanford branch is about life direction, reinvention, and mortality; this branch is about focus, pruning, integration, and executive willingness to endure blame.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/iger-strategy-framework|Iger Strategy Framework]], Jobs is less portfolio-capital-allocation oriented and more ruthless about subtraction. Iger clarifies repeated priorities; Jobs clarifies what leaders must stop doing to make priorities real.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/business/seth-godin-strategy-framework|Seth Godin Strategy Framework]], Jobs is less about systems, empathy, and time horizons at the worldview level, and more about the painful operational consequences of choosing one path over others.

# Gaps

- The current source is compact and example-driven, but it is not a full historical or technical account of Apple's 1997 choices.
- The branch still needs stronger material on when broad experimentation is wiser than aggressive concentration.
- It also needs more explicit guidance on humane project shutdowns so focus does not become a license for careless internal damage.
