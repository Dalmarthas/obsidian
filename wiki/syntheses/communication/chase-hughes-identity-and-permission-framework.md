# Question

What new identity-and-permission model does this Chase Hughes source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a six-part model:

1. Change behavior by changing what the situation means.
   PCP argues that perception and context shift before permission does.

2. Use identity, not only incentives.
   Precommitments and `I am` language create stronger pressure than ordinary goals because they threaten self-image when violated.

3. Move people indirectly when direct pressure would trigger defense.
   Negative dissociation and adjacent language tactics work by letting the listener adopt the identity themselves.

4. Read adult behavior as replayed childhood contracts.
   Safety, belonging, and reward scripts are treated as durable background programs rather than as solved childhood history.

5. Pair influence with human recognition.
   The source repeatedly argues that people most need to feel seen and heard, and that real connection becomes more valuable as synthetic connection spreads.

6. Treat the same mechanics as dual-use.
   The line between manipulation and healing is not the mechanism itself but the ethics, context, and transparency of its use.
