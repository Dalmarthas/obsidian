# Question

What durable conversation framework does the new composite conversation source add to the vault?

# Answer

The current source base supports a five-part model:

1. Start with curiosity.
   Enter the interaction wanting to discover the person, not impress them.

2. Make attention feel real.
   Presence, warmth, and calm self-possession change the emotional quality of the exchange before any clever line does.

3. Avoid dead-script conversation.
   Specific humor, candid observations, and slightly non-default answers make the interaction more vivid and memorable.

4. Ask for emotional texture, not only facts.
   Better questions pull out values, excitement, highlights, and meaning rather than stopping at surface biography.

5. Make people feel accurately heard.
   Reflect details, react specifically, paraphrase cleanly, and prove that their story actually landed.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/communication/george-stephanopoulos-communication-framework|George Stephanopoulos Communication Framework]], this source is less about public accountability and more about interpersonal warmth, engagement, and memorable connection.
- Stephanopoulos is stronger on clarity, concision, and pressure-tested questioning.
- The new source is stronger on first-contact social energy: curiosity, warmth, playful specificity, and emotionally resonant prompts.
- Together they make the communication branch broader: one handles disciplined clarity under pressure, the other handles connective conversation in everyday and professional social settings.

# Gaps

- This framework is built from a composite secondary summary rather than from primary-source transcripts or one coherent author.
- The branch still needs more evidence on how these techniques translate into harder conversations where trust is low or stakes are high.
- The charisma layer is useful but especially vulnerable to cargo-cult imitation.
