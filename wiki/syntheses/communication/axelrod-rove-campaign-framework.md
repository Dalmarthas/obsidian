# Question

What durable campaign framework can be extracted from the Axelrod and Rove political campaign masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a seven-part framework:

1. Start with purpose.
   The campaign has to answer why the candidate is running in terms of action and mission, not status.

2. Define the choice.
   Winning depends on framing what the election is about before the opponent defines it for you.

3. Research both sides hard.
   Know your candidate and your opponent deeply enough that surprise is minimized.

4. Target ruthlessly but not blindly.
   Focus on persuadable and movable voters while avoiding universes so narrow that you miss the race.

5. Build an integrated operating plan.
   Message, budget, staffing, field, media, and timeline must reinforce one another.

6. Stay on message under pressure.
   Debates, gaffes, attacks, and crises should be answered through the campaign's core identity rather than through panicked improvisation.

7. Close with disciplined execution.
   Ground game, early vote strategy, turnout monitoring, and final message freshness determine whether support actually converts into votes.

# Gaps

- The source is still a secondary summary with missing lesson content in a few areas such as polling and parts of fundraising.
- The framework is strongest for modern U.S. electoral politics and may transfer unevenly outside that context.
- The branch still needs stronger explicit treatment of democratic ethics, misinformation limits, and when effective tactics damage public trust.
