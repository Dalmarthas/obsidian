# Question

What durable communication framework can be extracted from the George Stephanopoulos masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part framework:

1. Prepare until you can be present.
   Preparation is not there to make you rigid. It is there to free you to respond in the moment.

2. Use the Four Cs.
   Clarity, concision, curiosity, and candor are the governing communication standards.

3. Listen with your eyes as well as your ears.
   Tone, hesitation, body language, and silence reveal what literal words may conceal.

4. Ask the shortest question that does the job.
   Direct questions, yes-or-no questions, and even single-word follow-ups can cut through evasion.

5. Hold people accountable without theatrical aggression.
   Focus on actions and consequences, not on imagined motives.

6. Let silence work.
   Extra beats often produce fuller, more honest answers than immediate follow-up pressure.
