# Question

What influence model does the current Chase Hughes 90-second source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a six-part interaction model:

1. Win the first 90 seconds before argument begins.
   Authority, attention, identity flexibility, and perceived importance are treated as early variables that shape everything later in the conversation.

2. Treat interaction design as pre-evaluative, not just verbal.
   Stillness, pace, pattern breaks, and timing are presented as stronger than content-only persuasion.

3. Prefer installation over persuasion.
   The source argues that language lands more strongly when it feels like observation or self-recognition rather than direct instruction.

4. Chain influence moves in nervous-system order.
   Weapon classes are stacked so identity, emotion, and hidden directives reinforce one another rather than compete.

5. Move beneath the polite mask in calibrated steps.
   The deviance ladder is used to move from surface compliance toward private and then taboo truth.

6. Use a permission bridge before deeper honesty.
   Slowing cadence, signaling deliberation, and visibly changing conversational register are treated as necessary before deeper or stranger material will land without recoil.

The framework's strongest caveat is ethical: its techniques are explicitly optimized to lower reflective resistance, so this branch sits closer to manipulation-risk than most communication models in the vault.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/communication/chase-hughes-anti-bullying-framework|Chase Hughes Anti-Bullying Framework]], this branch is less about conflict interruption and more about proactively shaping attention, meaning, and identity framing.
- Compared with Pink's persuasion model, this source is much less autonomy-centered and much more willing to work beneath explicit agreement.
- Compared with Voss-style tactical empathy, it is less about disclosure and joint problem-solving and more about narrator-level influence sequencing.
- Compared with the wider [[wiki/syntheses/cross-source/communication-and-influence-models|Communication and Influence Models]] page, this source is the vault's clearest example of influence-through-lowered-evaluation rather than influence-through-clarity.

# Gaps

- The source is still a secondary summary rather than a primary transcript.
- The vault does not yet have strong evidence for the exact timing and mechanism claims behind the `90-second` model.
- The branch needs better comparison with counseling ethics, coercion research, and evidence-based influence literature.
