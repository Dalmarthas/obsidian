# Question

What reusable writing and filmmaking framework emerges from the *Dark Knight* source?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part model:

1. Build conflict around worldview, not just objective.
   A villain becomes more potent when they embody a coherent philosophy that pressures everyone else.

2. Give the hero a real moral paradox.
   Heroism becomes dramatically alive when the protagonist's methods are powerful, necessary, and dangerous at the same time.

3. Let internal flaw generate the tragedy.
   The deepest collapses should feel seeded inside the character rather than dropped in from outside.

4. Design twists to feel inevitable and surprising.
   Strong reversals are prepared early enough to feel earned but staged sharply enough to still hit.

5. Preserve some menace through strategic withholding.
   Not every antagonist needs a fully resolved origin if mystery is part of the force they exert.

6. Use visual inversion deliberately.
   Daylight menace, spatial exposure, and other expectation-reversing image choices can heighten unease and support theme.

The distinctive Nolan layer is moral and structural pressure fused together: character psychology, philosophical conflict, twist design, and image strategy all serve the same destabilizing argument.
