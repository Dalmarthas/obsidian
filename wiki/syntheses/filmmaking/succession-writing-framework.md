# Question

What reusable writing framework emerges from the Succession writers' room source?

# Answer

The current source base supports a seven-part model:

1. Keep the room open before you close it down.
   Strong story work begins with broad exploration and only later narrows toward the truest version.

2. Use egoless pitching.
   Ideas are valuable not only when they survive intact, but when they provoke better ideas or sharper reactions.

3. Build humor from character truth.
   Comedy should emerge from psychology, status, and emotional reality rather than from detachable punchlines.

4. Write people, not archetypes.
   Most characters should feel psychologically specific, internally contradictory, and socially situated.

5. Let subtext carry the scene.
   Dialogue should reveal self-deception, status struggle, and hidden motives rather than flattening everything into explicit explanation.

6. Research just enough to protect plausibility.
   Technical and elite-world detail should support credibility while emotional stakes remain primary.

7. Finish bad drafts anyway.
   Deadlines, readings, collaborators, and accountability structures help writers get past self-doubt and into revision.

The distinctive `Succession` layer is truth under pressure: ensemble scenes, jokes, business stakes, and family cruelty all work because they are rooted in believable psychology rather than in genre tricks or standalone cleverness.
