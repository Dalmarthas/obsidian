# Question

What should be in place before drafting a screenplay?

# Answer

Based on the current source base, the minimum pre-draft checklist is:

1. Read several produced screenplays to calibrate your sense of what a finished script looks like.
2. Outline the story before drafting, at least enough to know the beginning, middle, and end.
3. Map the story against a structural model, usually three acts plus major turning points.
4. Learn screenplay formatting before you draft so you are not rewriting the same material into compliance later.
5. Use inexpensive or free tooling at the start; do not confuse buying software with making progress.

Operational interpretation:

- If the dramatic question is unclear, the outline is not ready.
- If the key turns are missing, the structure is not ready.
- If the format is unfamiliar, drafting speed will be wasted on avoidable cleanup.
