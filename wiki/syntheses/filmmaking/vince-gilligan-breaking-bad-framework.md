# Question

What reusable writing framework emerges from Vince Gilligan's *Breaking Bad* process?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part model:

1. Build the series around real transformation.
   Let the protagonist truly change instead of preserving television stasis.

2. Root the plot in psychology, not convenience.
   Ask what the character would genuinely do next from pride, fear, hunger, denial, or ego.

3. Make the pilot hook immediate.
   Start with a strong image, flash-forward, or destabilizing event so the show does not begin blandly.

4. Keep antagonists intelligent.
   Opposition stays credible when enemies fail through believable emotion or circumstance rather than through sudden stupidity.

5. Use the room as an authenticity filter.
   Boards, cards, and repeated challenges help the team catch fake turns before they harden into canon.

6. Let theme emerge from consequence.
   Empire, pride, self-delusion, and moral corrosion become clearer when the story earns them instead of announcing them in advance.

The distinctive Gilligan layer is this: plot is strongest when it feels like the inevitable expression of who the character has become.
