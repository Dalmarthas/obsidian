# Question

What reusable framework emerges from the current source on believable character writing?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part model:

1. Start from emotional truth, not trait labels.
   A character feels deep when the writer finds a real human entry point rather than a descriptive type.

2. Build character through want, obstacle, and tactic.
   The audience learns who the person is by watching what they want and what they do under resistance.

3. Give every major character their own point of view.
   The character should feel as if they could be the lead of their own story, not merely support furniture for someone else's arc.

4. Use contradiction instead of sanding it away.
   Hypocrisy, self-justification, and conflicting desires are part of what makes a character feel real.

5. Interview the character.
   Ask uncomfortable questions until hidden fear, shame, logic, and voice begin to emerge.

6. Research reality where needed.
   Real-world analogues, firsthand detail, and observed behavior make scenes less generic and less `writerly`.

The distinctive layer in this source is engineered believability: characters do not become vivid through backstory bulk alone, but through empathy, contradiction, and practical tools that make their behavior feel emotionally inevitable.
