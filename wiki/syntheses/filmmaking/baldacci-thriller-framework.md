# Question

What reusable thriller-writing framework emerges from the David Baldacci masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part model:

1. Live like a writer.
   Keep a writer's prism on the world so ordinary life keeps generating possible stories.

2. Open with a big pop.
   Build a first chapter strong enough to act as a touchstone for the whole novel.

3. Research immersively, then distill ruthlessly.
   Go beyond desk facts, but leave most of the research out of the finished book.

4. Stay within plausibility.
   The story can be extreme, but consequences, logistics, and bodies should still feel believable.

5. Make every chapter earn its place.
   Use chapter-level questions, shorter units, and variation in scene function to sustain momentum.

6. Edit collaboratively and aggressively.
   Work with editors who can find missed possibilities and pacing failures, then revise with focus rather than ego.

The distinctive Baldacci layer is professional stamina: treat thriller writing as a long craft career built from disciplined observation, research, and revision.
