# Question

What durable acting framework can be extracted from the Kevin Spacey acting masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a seven-part model:

1. React truthfully instead of demonstrating.
   Performance becomes alive when the actor is responding to a person, a want, a disappointment, or a discovery rather than displaying a preplanned mood.

2. Serve the writing.
   Step up into the language, understand why the words are there, and stop treating the text as a problem to simplify around your habits.

3. Raise stakes and vary the journey.
   A piece cannot survive on one emotional note; shifts, contradictions, and pressure create the life inside it.

4. Trust the audience to do part of the work.
   Hold some emotion in, avoid overillustrating, and let implication pull viewers forward.

5. Prepare concretely.
   Read the full play, map the scene, mark the shifts, choose important words, and test opposite approaches, physical tasks, masks, and mirrors.

6. Distinguish stage and screen demands.
   Theater teaches long arc through repetition, while screen acting requires explicit tracking of what the character knows and how the arc will cut together out of order.

7. Train like a professional.
   Keep monologues rotating, practice outside audition windows, arrive with multiple choices, take direction, and build a career strategically instead of opportunistically.

# Gaps

- The source is a secondary summary rather than a primary transcript.
- It is strongest on dramatic, text-based acting rather than on comedy, improvisation-heavy work, or devised ensemble processes.
- The branch still needs counterweights from directors, acting teachers, or performers with substantially different methods.
