# Question

What reusable diagnostic framework emerges from the `29 common screenwriting mistakes` source?

# Answer

The source compiles into a five-pass script diagnostic:

1. Concept pass.
   Is the idea genuinely yours, emotionally alive, and genre-appropriate, or is it soft, trend-chasing, or unowned?

2. Protagonist pass.
   Does the main character have a visible external goal, identifiable vulnerability, and real growth pressure, or are they vague, passive, or emotionally sealed off?

3. Setup-and-genre pass.
   Does the setup do enough work to orient the audience, establish the promise, and justify the genre, or did the script rush into motion without earning clarity?

4. Propulsion pass.
   Do stakes, conflict, surprise, and good-news/bad-news turns keep rising, or is the script merely moving without intensifying?

5. Professionalism pass.
   Has the work actually been rewritten, pressure-tested, and cleaned, or is the writer trying to outsource unfinished thinking to early readers?

# Basis

- The source repeatedly returns to external-goal clarity as a prerequisite for audience investment.
- It treats soft concepts, weak genre choices, and rushed setup as upstream story failures rather than surface flaws.
- It stresses flaws, vulnerability, and emotional honesty as essential correctives to flat character design.
- It frames revision as targeted diagnosis, not just polishing.
- It treats note-taking, multiple drafts, and not circulating immature work as part of professional craft.

# Comparisons

- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/premise-testing|Premise Testing]] is the strongest overlap for the first pass:
  concept failure is often visible before drafting.
- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/story-propulsion|Story Propulsion]] is the strongest overlap for the fourth pass:
  scripts often die not because nothing happens, but because movement lacks escalation and consequence.
- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/character-driven-storytelling|Character-Driven Storytelling]] is the strongest overlap for the second pass:
  passive or underbuilt characters poison the whole engine.
- [[wiki/concepts/filmmaking/writing-process-and-revision|Writing Process and Revision]] is the strongest overlap for the fifth pass:
  craft improves when diagnostics become iterative process rather than shame.

# Gaps

- The source is broad rather than tightly attributed, so the exact hierarchy among its 29 mistakes is compressed here.
- The vault still lacks a fully explicit staged checklist separating pre-draft, post-outline, post-draft, and pre-submission diagnostics.
- The framework would improve with more case-study sources showing the same failures in actual produced or unproduced scripts.
