# Question

What reusable screenwriting framework emerges from the Aaron Sorkin masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part operating model:

1. Start with intention and obstacle.
   Every story and every scene needs a strong want and a credible resistance.

2. Build character from tactics, not biography.
   What matters most is how people pursue what they want under pressure.

3. Treat structure as a practical guide.
   Setup, inciting action, escalation, payoff, and prepared resolution are not formulas so much as load-bearing supports.

4. Move every scene.
   A scene should travel from one state to another and launch the next beat instead of merely resting in place.

5. Write dialogue for performance.
   Rhythm, interruption, cadence, and speakability matter as much as literal realism.

6. Rewrite diagnostically.
   Finish the draft, identify the real failures, cut what the story can live without, and respond to notes by solving the underlying problem.

The distinctive Sorkin layer on top of this is audience trust: the writer should guide the audience confidently while still making them do part of the interpretive work.
