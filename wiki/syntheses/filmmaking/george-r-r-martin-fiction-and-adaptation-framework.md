# Question

What reusable writing and adaptation framework emerges from George R.R. Martin's source?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part model:

1. Keep the original work and adaptation mentally separate.
   Related mediums can inform each other, but they should not be forced into one continuity.

2. Protect long-horizon narrative intent from fan contamination.
   Curiosity and fandom matter, but core story decisions should not be rewritten to chase or dodge theories.

3. Accept butterfly effects in adaptation.
   Small changes in survival, casting, budget, or legal conditions can create major later divergence.

4. Choose POV characters for agency, not for camera utility.
   A point-of-view slot should belong to someone with real dramatic weight and a meaningful arc.

5. Build worlds from transformed history.
   Religions, cultures, and political systems feel richer when grounded in real historical patterns and then imaginatively recomposed.

6. Treat rulership as hard, not mythically simple.
   Power, virtue, gender, and governance should remain structurally difficult rather than collapsing into fairy-tale certainty.

The distinctive Martin layer is disciplined narrative integrity: protect the deeper logic of the story even while adaptation, fandom, and production pressures keep trying to bend it.
