# Question

What short-form storytelling framework does the current vault extract from *How To Become A Master Storyteller*?

# Answer

The source supports a six-part framework:

1. Dance between context and conflict.
   Use context to orient and conflict to reopen curiosity. Beat transitions should feel like `therefore` or `but`, not only `and then`.

2. Write for rhythm.
   Vary sentence length, syllabic feel, and delivery cadence so the script sounds alive when spoken.

3. Use conversational tone.
   Speak to one imagined person rather than performing at an anonymous crowd.

4. Design from the ending.
   Decide the final line, feeling, or insight first, then build the middle so the story naturally arrives there.

5. Choose a story lens.
   Make familiar material distinct by selecting a less obvious but credible angle.

6. Open with a specific visual hook.
   The first line should reveal the plot clearly, and the first image should give immediate visual proof of what the viewer is entering.

The framework's operating principle is that short-form storytelling is not just compression. It is a retention-sensitive form where clarity, curiosity, rhythm, tone, and visual proof have to arrive faster than in long-form work.

# Gaps

- The source is strongest for short-form video and may overfit to platform retention mechanics.
- It does not deeply address long-form character development, moral complexity, research rigor, or thematic layering.
- The `visual hooks are 10x more effective` claim should be treated as directional creator judgment, not as a measured universal ratio.
