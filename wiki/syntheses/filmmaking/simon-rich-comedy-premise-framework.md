# Question

What reusable creative framework emerges from the Simon Rich source on comedy premise generation?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part model:

1. Generate first, judge later.
   Premise abundance is a strength, not a sign of lack of taste.

2. Use frameworks instead of waiting for inspiration.
   `What if?`, trope inversion, literalized fear, and satirical reframing all create entry points into new premises.

3. Mine ordinary life aggressively.
   Headlines, awkward conversations, and small absurdities can become story seeds when exaggerated or recombined.

4. Treat amusement as a signal.
   If the idea naturally entertains the writer, it has a stronger chance of holding energy through later development.

5. Filter after abundance.
   Most ideas should die; only a few deserve deeper refinement.

6. Build ideation stamina like a muscle.
   The point of volume is not merely more ideas, but a stronger long-term ability to keep finding premises under pressure.

The distinctive Simon Rich layer is premise abundance with low-stakes play: strong comedy ideas come less from waiting for brilliance and more from maintaining a system that produces enough raw material for brilliance to appear inside it.

# Gaps

- This framework rests on a secondary summary rather than a primary transcript.
- The source is stronger on idea generation than on full-story architecture after a premise is chosen.
- The branch still needs richer primary-source evidence on how Rich distinguishes a funny premise from a premise that can also sustain emotional depth.
