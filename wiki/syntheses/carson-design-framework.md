# Question

What reusable design framework emerges from the David Carson masterclass summary?

# Answer

The current source base supports a six-part model:

1. Make design communicate before reading.
   The page should intrigue and orient the viewer before the copy is consciously processed.

2. Trust intuition.
   Use felt judgment as an active design tool instead of defaulting entirely to inherited systems.

3. Treat typography as expressive form.
   Type arrangement, spacing, voice, and materiality are meaning-bearing image choices.

4. Break rules purposefully.
   Risk, surprise, and irregularity matter when they intensify the message rather than merely decorate it.

5. Keep the human visible.
   Handwork, collage, texture, and personal choice make the work feel inhabited rather than machine-sanded.

6. Avoid the dead middle.
   Simplicity or full complexity can work; safe in-between design often lacks force.

The distinctive Carson layer is rebellious permission: do not wait for the field to tell you your eye is allowed to matter.
