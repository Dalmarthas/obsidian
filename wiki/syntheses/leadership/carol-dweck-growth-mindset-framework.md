# Question

What reusable learning and culture model does the current Carol Dweck source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a six-part model:

1. Diagnose the interpretation of difficulty.
   The first question is not whether a task is hard, but whether the person reads that hardness as exposure of limitation or as a chance to develop.

2. Change the praise language.
   Reward strategy, persistence, adaptation, and learning process more than identity labels like "smart" or "talented."

3. Replace verdict language with developmental language.
   `Not yet` is a compact way to keep current inability from turning into fixed identity.

4. Build challenge tolerance deliberately.
   Growth mindset is not a declaration. It is repeated willingness to enter stretch, accept correction, and revise strategy.

5. Design cultures that reward learning instead of image protection.
   Teams and organizations become more adaptive when experimentation, collaboration, and honest error correction are safer than status defense.

6. Watch for false growth mindset.
   The model fails when leaders use growth language without giving people coaching, feedback, or room to learn from mistakes.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/leadership/high-performance-mindset|High-Performance Mindset]], Dweck is less focused on elite execution and more focused on how people interpret difficulty and feedback before performance habits harden.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/leadership/organizational-culture|Organizational Culture]], this framework is more specific about one cultural axis: whether the environment rewards development or protects status.
- Compared with the Goleman branch, Dweck is less about emotional regulation and more about the belief structure that determines whether challenge is embraced or avoided.

# Gaps

- The branch currently rests on one secondary summary and needs stronger primary-source depth.
- The current source is strong on mindset framing and praise language, but thinner on how to measure durable culture change over time.
- The vault still needs stronger counterexamples where growth language is used manipulatively or where development limits are more binding than mindset framing.
