# Question

What reusable leadership framework do the current Jordan Peterson leadership sources add to the vault?

# Answer

The Peterson leadership branch now adds a seven-part framework:

1. Build leadership on character, not just position.
   The leader has to be trustworthy, conscientious, and reliable enough that other people can orient themselves around what is said.

2. Define an ethical destination.
   Leadership starts by knowing where the group is going and why that destination deserves effort.

3. Tell the organizing story.
   The leader must explain the enterprise as a coherent narrative that makes the goal, the ethic, and the shared benefit intelligible.

4. Stop trying to manufacture motivation artificially.
   Instead of relying on hype, pressure, or cliches, identify work genuinely worth doing and frame it clearly enough that people can recognize its value.

   The newer anti-`motivation` source sharpens this further: leaders should make goal, role, and mutual benefit explicit enough that progress itself starts doing motivational work.

5. Treat consistency as more important than style.
   Warmth, severity, and charisma are secondary to whether the leader's words and actions stay aligned over time.

6. Build a game people can voluntarily play.
   Healthy organizations work better when roles, rules, and aims are arranged so participants can willingly accept their part rather than merely endure enforcement.

7. Align organizational roles with personal life direction.
   Engagement rises when people can place their role inside a broader life plan; misalignment produces hidden contradiction and brittle performance.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/leadership/seth-godin-leadership-framework|Seth Godin Leadership Framework]], Peterson is less about leading change under uncertainty and more about building a meaningful cooperative structure that people can willingly inhabit.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/leadership/simon-sinek-infinite-game-framework|Simon Sinek Infinite Game Framework]], Peterson is less trust-and-stewardship centered and more focused on narrative alignment, meaning, and the distinction between voluntary cooperation and coercion.
- The newer anti-`motivation` Peterson source also makes his branch more behaviorally specific than before: it links meaningful story to felt progress, and felt progress to sustained motivation.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/leadership/simon-sinek-trust-centered-leadership-framework|Simon Sinek Trust-Centered Leadership Framework]], Peterson is more personality-and-character centered. Sinek asks what kind of social environment leaders build; Peterson adds a prior question about whether the leader is reliable enough to deserve trust in the first place.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/leadership/clinton-leadership-framework|Clinton Leadership Framework]], Peterson is less process-and-mediation oriented and more foundational: before negotiation or execution quality, the leader must articulate what game is being played and why anyone should play it.
- Compared with [[wiki/syntheses/leadership/jordan-peterson-responsibility-framework|Jordan Peterson Responsibility Framework]], this branch moves from personal burden to organizational design. The responsibility framework asks what an individual should carry; this one asks how a leader builds a structure people can join without inner contradiction.

# Gaps

- The guide-to-leadership source is more philosophically sharp than empirically detailed, especially around Piagetian extrapolation and the reported productivity gains from life-plan writing.
- The anti-`motivation` source improves the practical framing, but it still leans on asserted productivity and persistence effects without enough methodological detail.
- The character source is stronger on trait and moral language than on operational assessment: the vault still needs better tests for how organizations should evaluate trustworthiness without reducing it to image.
- The branch still needs stronger sources on compensation, bargaining power, and how much `voluntary` cooperation is possible inside constrained labor systems.
- The vault also needs clearer tests for when mission and life-plan alignment language is genuine versus manipulative.
