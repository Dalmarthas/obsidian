# Question

What reusable leadership model does the current Daniel Goleman source add to the vault?

# Answer

The source adds a six-part model:

1. Treat IQ as entry ticket, not final differentiator.
   In demanding environments, raw intelligence often gets people into the room, but emotional and social skill increasingly explain who becomes reliably effective.

2. Start with self-awareness.
   People cannot regulate what they do not notice. Leadership quality improves when a person can detect emotional state before it governs behavior automatically.

3. Build self-regulation before high-stakes performance.
   Distress, impulsive anger, and emotional hijack consume cognitive bandwidth. Emotional self-mastery protects judgment, ethics, and working memory.

4. Use social intelligence to shape climate, not just read it.
   Rapport, attention, empathy, and positive affect are not soft decorations. They alter how teams coordinate, trust, and think together.

5. Hire and develop for demonstrated competencies.
   Instead of over-trusting credentials, identify what distinguishes top performers in context and train toward those traits.

6. Treat emotional intelligence as trainable infrastructure.
   Practices that improve attention and regulation can strengthen the system over time, which means EQ is not a fixed personality gift.

# Comparisons

- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/communication/attunement|Attunement]], Goleman offers a broader model that includes internal regulation, not only perspective-taking.
- Compared with [[wiki/concepts/leadership/high-performance-mindset|High-Performance Mindset]], this source is less about grit, standards, and repeated excellence, and more about the emotional architecture that protects cognition and relationships under pressure.
- Compared with the vault's stress branch, Goleman is less about reinterpreting stress and more about preventing emotional hijack while improving interpersonal functioning.

# Gaps

- The branch currently rests on one secondary summary and needs stronger primary-source depth.
- The neuroscience framing is useful, but the vault still needs more careful treatment of where popularized brain-language may oversimplify the evidence.
- The current source is strongest on leadership effectiveness and performance; it says less about institutional misuse of EQ language in evaluation or corporate politics.
