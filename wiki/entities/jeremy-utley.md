# Overview

Jeremy Utley appears in this vault as an AI creativity and productivity teacher whose main contribution is a collaboration model for using LLMs more like guided creative partners than passive answer engines.

# Role In This Vault

His role is to deepen the AI and creativity branches from static prompt structure toward live creative practice: context-rich setup, clarifying-question loops, role assignment, roleplaying, critique, iterative refinement, inspiration discipline, idea-flow volume, white space, and using AI to preserve rather than replace critical thinking.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-jeremy-utley-ai-creativity-and-productivity]] frames AI use as coaching an eager intern rather than issuing machine-style commands.
- The same source introduces context engineering and reverse prompting as practical upgrades over shallow prompt phrasing.
- It also positions role assignment, few-shot prompting, and critique loops as repeatable ways to get more originality and usefulness from AI systems.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-5-step-playbook-for-10x-your-ai-productivity]] adds sharper treatment of AI's agreeable failure mode, critical-thinking scaffolding, and conversation roleplay as a rehearsal simulator.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-how-to-master-ai-powered-creativity-in-just-13-minutes-jeremy-utley]] adds the tool-versus-teammate distinction, AI expert consultation as workflow discovery, and the warning that AI makes it dangerously easy to stop at a first "good enough" idea.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-jeremy-utley-ai-ignites-human-creativity]] adds the idea-flow argument: AI is most creative when used for quantity, variation, regeneration, voice-based thought capture, and feedback loops rather than one-shot answers.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-habit-that-built-every-genius]] adds a broader genius-as-habits frame: disciplined inspiration, constant idea capture, protected white space, and idea quantity are trainable practices that AI can amplify but not replace.
