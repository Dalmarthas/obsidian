# Overview

Clayton Christensen enters this vault as a business thinker focused on how real growth is created, why incumbents miss it, and how flawed internal theories and finance metrics distort strategic choices. In the current source base, his role is not generic innovation inspiration but a more diagnostic challenge to growth stories that rely too heavily on incremental improvement, efficiency gains, or competitor-centric thinking.

# Role In This Vault

- He deepens the business branch with a more causal theory of innovation and growth.
- He adds a reusable customer-understanding lens through `jobs to be done`.
- He sharpens the vault's strategy layer by showing how capital-allocation rules can quietly block the very growth companies claim to want.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source argues that growth follows innovations that solve real jobs rather than simply improving products in abstract feature terms.
- It separates sustaining, disruptive, and efficiency innovation instead of treating all innovation as one undifferentiated good.
- It also warns that ratio-based financial metrics can systematically bias companies away from disruptive investment.
