# Overview
Daniel Levitin enters this vault as a neuroscience-oriented source focused here on stress-impaired judgment, prospective planning, and practical decision hygiene. In the current source base, his role is not broad brain science. It is a more concrete account of how to protect thinking before pressure arrives.
# Role In This Vault
- He sharpens the new stress branch by adding a more executive-function-centered account of what stress disrupts.
- He makes premortem planning concrete enough to deserve its own canonical page rather than remaining a passing timing tactic.
- He adds a health-adjacent decision layer around medical tradeoffs, advance family discussion, and risk literacy under pressure.
# Evidence In This Vault
- The current source argues that stress chemistry narrows rational judgment and increases preventable mistakes.
- It recommends premortems as a practical way to improve decisions before crisis degrades cognition.
- It also emphasizes environmental systems and advance conversations as safeguards against stress-driven chaos.
