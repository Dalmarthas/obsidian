# Overview

Paul Thomas Anderson is a filmmaker whose branch adds a practical model of screenwriting built around personal rhythm, economy on the page, character truth, productive imperfection, and collaborative flexibility. In the current vault, he sharpens the idea that strong screenwriting is not maximal explanation but disciplined restraint that still leaves the work alive for actors and the set.

# Role In This Vault

- He deepens the writing branch with a clearer screenplay-versus-prose distinction.
- He adds a stronger anti-preciousness rule around on-set collaboration and line mutability.
- He sharpens the process branch around personal rhythm and the productive use of mistakes.
- He reinforces creative integrity in a less mystical and more practical register than some other sources.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source treats daily practice as necessary but individualized.
- It frames the screenplay as a blueprint that should stay economical and playable.
- It treats intuition and roughness as meaningful parts of the process rather than signs of fraud.
- It uses character resistance as a diagnostic tool for catching false story movement.
- It treats collaboration with actors as a gain in realism rather than a betrayal of authorship.
