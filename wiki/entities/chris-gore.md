# Overview

Chris Gore enters the vault as a critic and indie-film advocate who sharpens the ecosystem layer around filmmaking rather than the writing or directing layer alone. His branch is most useful where the vault needs language for studio risk aversion, franchise decay, audience disrespect, and the continuing creative importance of independent cinema.

# Role In This Vault

- He adds the strongest current critique of corporate studio logic as a force that shapes what kinds of films get made.
- He gives the vault a language for the difference between creator-led filmmaking and franchise-factory filmmaking.
- He strengthens the audience-respect layer by arguing that viewers are more perceptive than executives often assume.
- He also gives the vault an explicit pro-indie counterweight to blockbuster monoculture.
- The second Gore source adds sharper language for `studio malpractice`, franchise stewardship failure, marketing-product mismatch, and the way adaptation dependence exposes systemic risk aversion.
