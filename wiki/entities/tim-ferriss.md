# Overview

Tim Ferriss enters this vault as a systems-minded self-management teacher whose current contribution is to combine stoic fear-reduction, journaling, meditation, and boundary-setting into a practical operating model for handling distraction, anxiety, and restless ambition.

# Role In This Vault

- He adds a stronger stoic-discomfort layer to the productivity branch.
- He deepens the vault's systems-over-willpower logic with more explicit use of rules, defaults, batching, and automation.
- He gives the branch a more experimental stance toward habits, routines, and downside planning.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source presents Ferriss as using stoic exercises to reduce fear of embarrassment, poverty, and external opinion.
- It also treats journaling and meditation as recurring tools for externalizing loops and stabilizing attention.
- The same source emphasizes systems, short experiments, and worst-case-scenario planning over vague discipline narratives.
