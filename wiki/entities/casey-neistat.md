# Overview

Casey Neistat enters this vault not mainly as a filmmaker biography but as a teacher of creator independence in the platform era. In the current source base, his role is to make a sharper claim than generic hustle rhetoric: many creative careers now start by publishing with available tools, bypassing permission systems, and using the internet as both distribution channel and apprenticeship environment.

# Role In This Vault

- He deepens the business branch around creator-native career design rather than conventional ladder-climbing.
- He adds a more explicit permissionless-publication layer to creative entrepreneurship.
- He strengthens the vault's career material by arguing that access can increasingly be built through output and audience connection rather than waiting for legacy approval.
- He also adds a creator-economy version of framework-driven action: start with current tools, publish, collaborate, and learn in public.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source argues that old `you can't` rules often reflect outdated gatekeeping assumptions more than real constraints.
- It presents accessible technology and internet distribution as the main environmental shift that makes independent creation more viable.
- It also frames collaboration and scene-building as part of the method rather than as optional extras.
- The branch is strongest where it distinguishes resourcefulness from permission-seeking, not where it merely celebrates confidence.
