# Overview

Max Landis is presented in this vault as a blunt screenwriting commentator focused on page-level effectiveness. The current source frames him less as a theory-heavy structural teacher and more as a practical advocate for sparseness, clarity, momentum, readable dialogue, and the idea that a screenplay must work as a pleasurable read before it ever becomes a produced film. Main source: [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-max-landis-on-good-screenplays|Max Landis on Good Screenplays]].

# Role In This Vault

Landis functions here as a readability-and-momentum pressure point inside the broader filmmaking branch. His current contribution sharpens the difference between scripts that are merely technically formatted and scripts that actually compel a reader to keep turning pages.
