# Overview

Don Yaeger enters this vault through a conversation with [[wiki/entities/simon-sinek|Simon Sinek]] about leadership, sports, mentorship, appreciation, and long-term people development. In this source, his role is to bring coaching stories, especially about [[wiki/entities/john-wooden|John Wooden]], into a broader leadership model.

# Role In This Vault

- He supplies the Wooden case material that sharpens the standards-of-being branch.
- He adds a practical appreciation ritual through his long-running weekly letter practice.
- He adds a structural family-presence example through a calendar rule limiting nights away from home.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-leadership-advice-nobody-follows-but-everyone-should]] presents Yaeger as Sinek's conversation partner on leadership lessons that are widely known but rarely practiced.
- His weekly letter practice is used to show that appreciation can become a trained attentional habit.
- His work on mentorship with Wooden is used to distinguish real mentoring from status access or paid guru dynamics.
