# Overview

John Truby is a screenwriting teacher and story-structure thinker whose current branch in this vault centers on genre mastery, premise rigor, anti-formula structure thinking, long-horizon craft mastery, and the idea that plot should emerge from character weakness rather than from externally imposed mechanics.

# Role In This Vault

- He adds the strongest explicit genre-first model in the filmmaking branch.
- He sharpens the vault's live tension between useful structural scaffolds and shallow formula worship.
- He strengthens the pre-draft side of the process layer through premise testing, loglines, and scene-sequence planning.
- He gives the vault a clearer statement that character weakness is not a decorative trait but a structural generator.
- He now also adds a professionalization layer: most failure is framed as undertraining plus psychological attrition rather than bad luck alone.
