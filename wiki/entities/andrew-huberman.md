# Overview

Andrew Huberman appears in this vault as a translator of neurobiology into practical routines for attention, energy, and performance. In the current source base, his role is no longer just morning sequencing. He now also anchors a more explicit focus branch on attentional narrowing, interval-like work, environmental design, and treating mental fatigue as a physiological constraint rather than as simple weakness.

# Role In This Vault

Huberman currently anchors a health-and-productivity crossover branch focused on:

- using light as a timing signal
- delaying caffeine as an energy-stability tactic
- protecting early deep-work blocks
- linking physiology, focus, and training into one day-start sequence
- designing attention through sensory constraint rather than relying on pure willpower
- using intervals, context shifts, and recovery to protect deeper focus
