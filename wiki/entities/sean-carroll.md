# Overview

Sean Carroll enters this vault as a bridge figure between physics, philosophy, and existential interpretation. In the current source base, his role is not simply to explain cosmology. It is to argue that a scientific picture of the universe can absorb questions about causality, consciousness, and meaning without collapsing into mysticism or nihilism.

# Role In This Vault

- He gives the vault a clearer language for distinguishing fundamental description from higher-level explanation.
- He sharpens the vault's handling of emergence by making room for causation, purpose, and conscious life as real but nonfundamental features.
- He adds a stronger naturalistic argument that finitude and physicalism do not erase human meaning.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source argues that modern physics replaces naive purpose-driven causality with lawful patterns and structural explanation.
- It treats entropy increase and the arrow of time as central to how complexity, memory, and life become possible.
- It claims that consciousness and value can be emergent features of the physical world rather than evidence for dualism or supernatural purpose.
