# Overview

Po-Shen Loh appears in this vault as a mathematician, Olympiad coach, and education entrepreneur whose main contribution is a creative-thinking model for the AI era.

# Role In This Vault

His role is to connect creativity, AI judgment, math education, thoughtfulness, and social entrepreneurship. Loh sharpens the vault's existing AI-collaboration branch by arguing that humans increasingly need to evaluate and adapt machine-generated answers. He also sharpens the learning branch by treating unfamiliar problems, writing, communication, and world simulation as training grounds for independent thought rather than as tasks to outsource.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-how-to-be-a-creative-thinker-carnegie-mellon-university-po-shen-loh]] frames AI-era creativity as learning to "grade the homework": evaluate, correct, and adapt generated answers rather than passively accepting them.
- The same source argues that creative thinking is trained through unfamiliar problems, especially in math contexts where students cannot rely on rehearsed tricks.
- It also presents Loh's education model as a win-win-win ecosystem linking middle school learners, high-performing high school math students, and drama-trained communication coaches.
- The source positions real-world interaction as part of creative work: talking with parents, students, and schools surfaces pain points that desk-bound ideation would miss.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-only-trait-for-success-in-the-ai-era-how-to-build-it-po-shen-loh]] defines the AI-era human edge as thoughtfulness: mental fitness, empathy, world simulation, bias-aware perspective gathering, and authentic value creation for others.
- The same source adds a social-resilience layer to Loh's education model: critical thinking, charisma, pair teaching, and trusted networks of kind, capable people are treated as preparation for technological and societal uncertainty.
