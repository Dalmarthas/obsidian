# Overview

John Bartholomew appears in this vault as a chess educator whose teaching emphasizes disciplined strategic judgment rather than automatic play.

# Role In This Vault

His current role in the vault is to deepen the chess branch on trade logic, pawn structures, coordination, development tempo, piece safety, recurring beginner mistake patterns, and the strategic consequences hidden inside seemingly simple decisions.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-trades-in-chess]] presents Bartholomew as strongest on purposeful trading, structure-aware exchanges, bishop-versus-knight judgment, and awareness of zwischenzug resources during trade sequences.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-pawn-play-in-chess]] adds a broader structural layer around isolated, doubled, and backward pawns, pawn breaks, hooks, and synchronization between pawn play and piece activity.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-beginner-chess-mistakes]] adds a diagnostic improvement layer around common beginner errors, loose pieces, developmental sequencing, key squares, and pattern-based review.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-piece-and-pawn-coordination]] adds the clearest current Bartholomew layer on coordinated development, pawn restraint, undefended pieces, and how development lag should shape exchanges and recovery plans.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-recurring-beginner-chess-mistakes]] strengthens the same beginner-improvement branch with a case-study view of ignored threats, zwischenzug blindness, mistimed pawn breaks, weak-square creation, and the planning cost of locking positions while ahead in activity.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-john-bartholomew-undefended-pieces]] adds the clearest single-focus Bartholomew lesson in the vault on loose-piece awareness, overprotection, danger sense, and why many blunders are still just failures of piece safety.
