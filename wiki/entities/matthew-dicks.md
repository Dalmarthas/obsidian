# Overview

Matthew Dicks enters this vault as a personal-storytelling teacher. The current source presents his method through one core idea: a story becomes memorable when it is built around a brief moment of internal change and arranged so the audience can feel the before/after contrast.

# Role In This Vault

- He adds a personal-narrative version of transformation in storytelling.
- He gives the vault a compact craft rule for finding the real story inside an anecdote: locate the five-second realization.
- He connects structure to vulnerability, showing that emotional connection depends on both truthful exposure and careful suspense design.
- He gives practical delivery-adjacent tactics: start in action, misdirect expectations, slow down before the reveal, and name the realization plainly.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-storyworthy-by-matthew-dicks-core-message|STORYWORTHY by Matthew Dicks Core Message]] defines the `five-second moment` as the center of a powerful personal story.
- The same source treats contrast as the story's spine: the listener should understand who the speaker was before the realization and what changed afterward.
- It also frames misdirection and delayed reveal as ways to make the audience participate in anticipation instead of passively receiving a lesson.
