# Overview

Judson Brewer enters this vault as a physician-researcher and mindfulness teacher focused here on habit formation, craving, and behavior change. His contribution is not generic meditation advice. It is a learning-based account of how curiosity can update the brain's reward expectations and loosen harmful habits without relying mainly on willpower.

# Role In This Vault

- He opens the vault's first explicit health branch on habit loops and craving-driven behavior.
- He adds a mechanistic bridge between mindfulness practice and practical behavior change.
- He also gives the vault a more precise alternative to discipline-heavy self-regulation language.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source presents habit change as reward-based learning rather than pure cognitive control.
- It frames mindful curiosity as the main intervention move and `disenchantment` as the moment the loop begins to dissolve.
- The source's strongest practical contribution is a repeated experiment cycle: notice the urge, investigate it closely, observe the real payoff, and learn from the result.
