# Overview

Carol Dweck enters this vault as the main figure behind the growth-mindset branch, especially where learning behavior, feedback language, and culture design intersect. In the current source base, her role is not generic positivity or motivational language. It is a structured distinction between developmental and defensive interpretations of difficulty.

# Role In This Vault

- She gives the leadership branch a cleaner vocabulary for fixed versus growth-oriented interpretations of challenge.
- She links praise, feedback, and learning behavior in a way that is usable for education, management, and self-development.
- She adds a culture-design layer by showing that mindset can become institutional rather than remaining only personal.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source argues that praise for innate talent can make people more brittle and more afraid of challenge.
- It argues that process-oriented feedback strengthens persistence, experimentation, and strategy revision.
- It also frames growth cultures as more innovative, collaborative, and ethically healthy than fixed cultures that center status protection.
