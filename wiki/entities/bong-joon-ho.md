# Overview

Bong Joon-Ho is a filmmaker whose branch adds a tightly integrated model of story construction built from character interaction, moral ambiguity, family pressure, architectural suspense, and specificity that scales outward into universality. In the current vault, he is less a general screenwriting teacher than a high-value case of how theme, space, and social structure can become one dramatic machine.

# Role In This Vault

- He deepens the vault's filmmaking branch with a clearer architecture-and-space layer.
- He sharpens character design by showing how behavior in relationship can reveal personality faster than biography.
- He adds a stronger anti-binary moral layer that complements existing character and story pages.
- He gives the vault an explicit example of universality-through-specificity rather than generic `make it relatable` advice.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source shows Bong building character through action and group interaction rather than static profiles.
- It shows him rejecting simple villain frameworks in favor of understandable but compromised people.
- It places family systems at the center of dramatic escalation.
- It treats architecture as a suspense and meaning engine rather than background décor.
- It connects deeply Korean social detail to worldwide narrative resonance instead of treating local detail as an obstacle to reach.
