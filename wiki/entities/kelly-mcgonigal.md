# Overview

Kelly McGonigal enters this vault as a health psychologist working at the boundary between physiology, mindset, and behavior change. In the current source base, her contribution is not general wellness advice. It is a paired account of how interpretation changes the effects of stress and how willpower depends on trainable conditions such as sleep, mindfulness, self-compassion, and stronger connection to the future self.

# Role In This Vault

- She opens the vault's first explicit stress-focused health branch.
- She also opens a self-regulation branch that links health inputs to everyday discipline, temptation resistance, and lapse recovery.
- She creates a useful crossover point between the health domain and the vault's productivity material on future-self thinking, follow-through, and behavioral design.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The stress source argues that stress beliefs alter health outcomes rather than merely changing subjective experience.
- It also presents oxytocin and prosocial action as part of the stress response, not as separate comfort advice layered on top.
- The willpower source argues that self-control depends partly on physiological conditions, future-self vividness, self-compassion after lapses, and mindful urge management.
- Across both sources, her strongest recurring move is to replace moralized inner struggle with a more mechanistic account of how interpretation and practice change behavior.
