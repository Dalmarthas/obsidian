# Overview

Kelly McGonigal enters this vault as a health psychologist focused here on how stress beliefs change physiology, behavior, and resilience. In the current source base, her contribution is not general wellness advice. It is a specific reframing of stress from pure damage signal to a response that can support courage, social connection, and meaningful effort when interpreted well.

# Role In This Vault

- She opens the vault's first explicit stress-focused health branch.
- She adds a more direct bridge between psychology and preventive health than the current sleep, longevity, and exercise pages provide on their own.
- She also creates a useful crossover point between the health domain and the existing performance-minded material on challenge and recovery.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source argues that stress beliefs alter health outcomes rather than merely changing subjective experience.
- It also presents oxytocin and prosocial action as part of the stress response, not as separate comfort advice layered on top.
- The source's strongest practical contribution is a three-part response: reinterpret arousal, seek connection, and choose meaning over comfort-only avoidance.
