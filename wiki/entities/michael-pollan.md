# Overview

Michael Pollan enters this vault here as a journalist and interpreter of psychedelic research rather than primarily as a food writer. In the current source base, his role is to make the therapeutic-psychedelics branch legible by connecting research history, clinical structure, altered-state phenomenology, and cultural caution.

# Role In This Vault

- He opens a new health branch around psychedelic-assisted therapy and altered-state intervention.
- He sharpens the vault's distinction between therapeutic structure and careless experimentation.
- He adds a more explicit account of why context, ritual, and integration are part of the intervention rather than optional extras.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source argues that the strongest therapeutic use cases rely on screening, guided support, and integration rather than unsupervised use.
- It frames `set and setting` as one of the main causal factors in outcome quality.
- It also keeps a skeptical line on microdosing and on overclaiming neuroscience certainty.
