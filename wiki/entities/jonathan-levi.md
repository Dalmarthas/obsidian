# Overview

Jonathan Levi enters this vault as a practical-learning source focused on escaping plateaus through parallel skill pairing, multi-source explanation hunting, and forcing understanding upward from recognition to creation. In the current learning branch, his distinctive role is not basic motivation, but methodology design for when ordinary single-source study stops producing real progress.

# Role In This Vault

- He gives the learning branch a clearer anti-plateau model.
- He adds a stronger case for using multiple teachers and formats as a rational response to unclear explanations rather than as indecision.
- He sharpens the difference between recognition and genuine mastery by formalizing a climb from understanding to creation.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source argues that loosely related skills can speed one another through comparison and transfer.
- It also argues that explanation quality varies enough that learners should expect to rotate sources until something clicks.
- It adds a six-level progression that turns `I kind of get it` into a visibly insufficient stopping point.
- It treats recall, explanation, application, analysis, and creation as active tests that reveal whether learning has become usable.
