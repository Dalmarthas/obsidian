# Overview

Garry Kasparov enters this vault not only as a historic chess champion but as a technology thinker whose strongest current contribution is a model for productive human-machine partnership under conditions of rapid change.

# Role In This Vault

- He gives the AI branch a sharper process-first account of collaboration between humans and machines.
- He gives the chess branch a more mature engine-use philosophy centered on skepticism, interpretation, and not outsourcing understanding.
- He adds a broader pro-adaptation stance toward technological change that treats new tools as invitations to redesign work rather than proof of human obsolescence.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source uses Kasparov's Deep Blue experience to argue that process and interface can outperform raw power in mixed human-machine systems.
- It presents his `advanced chess` idea as a practical demonstration that collaboration design matters more than simple human-versus-machine framing.
- It also presents his later view that technology should push people toward new games and new forms of mastery rather than fear-driven defensiveness.
