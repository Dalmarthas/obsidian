# Overview

John Wooden enters this vault as a coaching and leadership exemplar used by Don Yaeger and Simon Sinek to show how long-term excellence can be built through character, relationships, consistent standards, and team-first behavior rather than direct obsession with winning.

# Role In This Vault

- He gives the leadership branch a concrete model of standards of being.
- He links high performance with humility, appreciation, and consistent treatment of stars.
- He strengthens the mentorship branch through the idea that a leader can keep learning from living people and deeply studied historical examples.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-the-leadership-advice-nobody-follows-but-everyone-should]] presents Wooden's Pyramid of Success as a framework that aims at greatness and character rather than trophies alone.
- The Bill Walton hair-length story is used to show that team standards are only real when they apply to elite performers.
- The "thank the passer" habit is used to show how recognition can be built into routine behavior.
- Wooden's love letters are used as an example of appreciation as a repeated practice, not merely a private feeling.
