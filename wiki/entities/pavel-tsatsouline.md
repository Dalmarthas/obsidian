# Overview

Pavel Tsatsouline enters this vault as a strength-and-conditioning teacher whose model emphasizes skillful practice, repeatable compound movements, submaximal loading, and fatigue discipline. In the current branch, his role is not bodybuilding variety or maximal hype, but training design that keeps strength, recovery, and long-horizon function aligned.

# Role In This Vault

- He anchors the vault's first explicit strength-training branch inside the health domain.
- His source gives the health branch a concrete exercise model instead of treating movement as a vague recommendation.
- The branch is especially useful because it connects strength, adaptation, recovery, and aging without requiring maximalism.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The source frames strength as a foundational quality that supports broader performance and daily function.
- It treats frequent, submaximal, technically clean practice as a better default than constant exhaustion.
- It adds a specific recovery idea through heterochronicity: different tissues and systems adapt and recover on different clocks.
- It also links strength training back to broader health through grip strength, resilience, and sustainable movement across life stages.
