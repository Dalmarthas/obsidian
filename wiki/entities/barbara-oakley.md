# Overview

Barbara Oakley enters this vault as a learning-strategy teacher whose branch makes study more mechanistic and less mystical. In the current source base, her contribution is not just `study harder`. It is a practical account of how concentration, breaks, memory structure, retrieval, and anti-procrastination tactics interact to produce real learning.

# Role In This Vault

- She deepens the productivity branch's learning cluster with a more explicit memory-and-attention model.
- She adds a bridge between practice quality and study design by showing how chunking, recall, and diffuse-mode breaks support retention.
- She also adds a humane anti-procrastination frame that treats avoidance as a response to pain prediction rather than as proof of bad character.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source distinguishes focused from diffuse learning modes and treats their alternation as necessary for insight.
- It also presents chunking and active recall as better indicators of real understanding than passive review.
- The same source argues that short timed focus sessions can shrink procrastination by lowering the emotional cost of starting.
