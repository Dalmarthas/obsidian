# Overview

Dale Carnegie appears in this vault as a foundational communication thinker centered on relationship-building through sincere interest, appreciation, and other-focused social skill. In the current branch, he matters less as a historical figure in isolation and more as a durable source of humane influence principles that still recur in modern communication advice.

# Role In This Vault

- He gives the vault a classic baseline for `be interested, not interesting`.
- He adds a stronger case for appreciation as a motivational and relational force.
- He serves as a useful counterweight to colder or more tactical influence models by insisting that goodwill and recognition are not optional extras.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-13-how-to-win-friends-and-influence-people-by-dale-carnegie|How to Win Friends and Influence People by Dale Carnegie]] distills the branch into two central behaviors:
  - genuine interest in other people
  - frequent, specific appreciation
- That source also shows how Carnegie-style curiosity can compound into access, help, and long-range cooperation.
- It further strengthens the vault's anti-ridicule position by treating appreciation as a better behavior lever than shame in many everyday contexts.
