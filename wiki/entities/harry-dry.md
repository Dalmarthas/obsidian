# Overview

Harry Dry enters this vault as a copywriting teacher associated here with specificity, visualizability, falsifiability, and brand-distinctive claims. In the current branch, his role is not broad advertising theory but a sharper written-messaging craft layer for business communication that has to be remembered, trusted, and acted on.

# Role In This Vault

- He anchors the vault's first explicit copywriting branch.
- His source adds a written persuasion layer that was relatively thin in the communication branch before this ingest.
- The branch is especially useful because it connects audience psychology, factuality, design awareness, and revision discipline in one compact system.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The source gives the branch a three-rule screen: visualizable, falsifiable, and unique.
- It argues that abstract language usually underperforms compared with concrete, picturable wording.
- It treats facts as a source of memorability, credibility, and structure rather than as dry support.
- It also contributes a craft process: write in the real medium, use design context early, and revise through feedback rather than through solitary polishing alone.
