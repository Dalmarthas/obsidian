# Overview

David Sinclair enters this vault as the figure most strongly associated here with aging biology, epigenetic information loss, and the claim that aging should become a direct target of medicine. Because the current source is composite, his branch is clearest at the level of aging theory and long-horizon prevention rather than as a clean source for every practical tip contained in the raw file.

# Role In This Vault

- He anchors the vault's first explicit longevity-and-aging branch.
- His current contribution is strongest where it reframes aging from an inevitable background process into something mechanistic, measurable, and potentially modifiable.
- The branch is especially useful because it sits between practical prevention and future-facing intervention, while still forcing the vault to separate fundamentals from hype.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The source ties Sinclair most clearly to the ideas of aging as disease, information loss in the epigenome, epigenetic clocks, and partial reprogramming.
- The same branch also connects him to practical longevity themes such as fasting, exercise, insulin sensitivity, and healthspan.
- At the same time, the source contains mixed-author-feeling sections on sleep, superfoods, fish oil, and supplement skepticism, so the vault preserves a caveat around attribution.
