# Overview

GothamChess appears in this vault as a practical chess educator who teaches through annotated games, fast pattern recognition, and clear explanations of why moves succeed or fail.

# Role In This Vault

This is the first named teacher in the vault's chess branch. The current sources anchor a practical-improvement model that is less about pure theory and more about usable decisions in real games, especially around planning from openings, spotting imbalances, punishing mistakes systematically, and using training environments pragmatically.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-gothamchess-how-to-win-at-chess]] presents GothamChess as strongest on practical opening plans, tactical vigilance, purposeful exchanges, and endgame conversion.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-gothamchess-beginner-chess-guide]] deepens that picture by emphasizing beginner error reduction, board vision, time management, and anti-blunder checklists.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-gothamchess-essential-chess-tips]] adds a broader planning layer around imbalances, attack conditions, unfamiliar moves, and repertoire depth versus breadth.
- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-gothamchess-beating-beginner-chess-bots]] adds the most explicit training-context layer so far: steady play against bots, simplification when ahead, basic mating technique, and the reminder that bots are for support work rather than as a full substitute for human competition.
- The branch currently frames his teaching style as club-player-oriented, example-driven, and organized around recurring decision mistakes.
