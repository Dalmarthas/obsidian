# Overview

Jordan Belfort enters this vault as a controversial sales teacher whose current contribution is the Straight Line model: a structured approach to moving prospects from uncertainty toward a buying decision through certainty transfer, conversational control, first-impression authority, tonality, preparation, and state management.

# Role In This Vault

- He gives the business branch a call-level sales model that complements the existing operations-heavy [[wiki/syntheses/business/modern-sales-systems-framework|Modern Sales Systems Framework]].
- He gives the communication branch a sharper example of persuasion through authority, emotional state, tonality, and scripted movement rather than through empathy alone.
- He also strengthens the vault's manipulation boundary problem: the same tools that make selling more teachable can become coercive when `no` is treated only as friction to overcome.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-21-jordan-belfort-straight-line-strategy|Jordan Belfort Straight Line Strategy]] argues that sales is the transfer of certainty.
- The source frames a sales conversation as a controlled line from opening to close rather than a free-form exchange.
- It emphasizes the first few seconds of perceived sharpness, enthusiasm, and expertise.
- It treats many objections as unresolved uncertainty, while also raising ethical concerns around pressure and buyer autonomy.
