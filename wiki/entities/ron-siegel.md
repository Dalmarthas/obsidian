# Overview

Ron Siegel enters this vault as a mindfulness teacher and clinician whose current contribution is to ground mindfulness in evidence, mechanism, and practical psychological use rather than in vague wellness language.

# Role In This Vault

- He gives the health branch a broader canonical mindfulness definition than the current Brewer branch alone provides.
- He sharpens the mechanism side through negativity bias, experiential avoidance, and acceptance-based contact with discomfort.
- He adds a more explicit clinical humility layer by treating mindfulness as useful but not universal.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source defines mindfulness as present-moment awareness with acceptance, kindness, and nonjudgment.
- It frames experiential avoidance as a central process that maintains anxiety and depression.
- It also presents mindfulness as a trainable skill with evidence for reduced reactivity, better emotional regulation, and relapse prevention in some populations.
