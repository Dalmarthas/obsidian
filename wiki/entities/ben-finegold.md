# Overview

Ben Finegold appears in this vault as a chess teacher whose current contribution is strongly psychological and practical: keep playing, keep checking for resources, and do not let emotions turn a difficult position into a lost one before the board actually proves it.

# Role In This Vault

His role is to deepen the chess branch on blunder psychology, resignation discipline, complacency in winning positions, and the practical truth that many results are still reversible until the final mistake is made.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-ben-finegold-blunders-in-chess]] argues that blunders overwhelm abstract game quality more often than players admit.
- The same source treats premature resignation as a recurring practical error and pushes players to keep searching for hidden defensive resources.
- It also adds a useful conversion warning: winning positions are not self-converting, because relaxation itself is often the last major blunder.
