# Overview

Josh Kaufman appears in this vault as a practical translator of learning into an operational system. In the current source, he is not framed as a mastery theorist. He is framed as an advocate for crossing the early competence barrier quickly through practice design, selective study, and feedback-aware repetition.

# Role In This Vault

Kaufman currently anchors the vault's first dedicated rapid-learning branch inside productivity. His role is to make skill-building feel more executable:

- separate usable proficiency from mastery mythology
- break skills into learnable components
- shorten the preparation phase until it supports self-correction
- normalize frustration, repetition, and sleep as part of the learning loop
- add pre-commitment and barrier removal so early practice survives the emotional cost of beginnerhood
