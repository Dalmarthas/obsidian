# Overview

Eric Edson is a screenwriting teacher whose current vault branch centers on psychologically grounded story structure, active protagonists with visible goals, dramatic reversals at act transitions, and the `Hero Goal Sequence` as the working unit of screenplay movement.

# Role In This Vault

- He strengthens the classical-structure side of the filmmaking branch without reducing structure to page-count dogma alone.
- He adds one of the vault's clearest micro-structural tools for diagnosing drift, repetition, and passive plotting.
- He sharpens the link between character, visible objective, and adversarial pressure in visual storytelling.
- He adds a stronger planning claim that knowing the ending early is not merely helpful but structurally stabilizing.
- He gives the vault a useful counterweight to looser craft talk by showing how emotional engagement can be engineered through recurring change units.
