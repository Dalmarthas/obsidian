# Overview

Rory Sutherland enters this vault as a marketing thinker focused on the irrational, emotional, perception-shaped, and constraint-shaped side of value. In the current source base, his role is not campaign craft alone but a broader challenge to optimization-heavy business logic: unusual ideas, framing shifts, psychologically meaningful design, and better diagnosis of the real limiting factor can all create more value than narrow efficiency improvements.

# Role In This Vault

- He deepens the advertising branch with a more behaviorally informed account of how brands become valuable.
- He adds a strategy-side warning that measurement-heavy systems can become blind to rare breakthrough ideas.
- He gives the business branch two reusable ideas that were previously underdeveloped here: `reverse benchmarking` and `perception-driven value`.
- He now also adds a systems-diagnosis layer through `bottleneck theory`, pushing the vault toward constraint-aware prioritization rather than diffuse optimization.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source treats breakthrough marketing as `fat-tailed`, with rare ideas producing outsized results.
- It argues that firms often win not by copying category best practice but by investing where the category is neglectful.
- It also insists that perceived value can be strategically redesigned through framing, context, aesthetics, and emotional meaning.
- The newer source adds that growth is often blocked by one real constraint at a time, and that the hard task is identifying whether the true limiter is awareness, availability, logistics, or another overlooked narrow point.
