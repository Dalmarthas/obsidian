# Overview

Laszlo Polgar appears in this vault as a training philosopher rather than as a direct instructional voice. The current branch treats him as the anchor for a problem-first, long-horizon model of chess improvement.

# Role In This Vault

His role here is to supply the most explicit current answer to the question of how a player should train over years rather than only how to play the next game: repeated positions, minimal spoon-feeding, slow high-quality work, and delayed emphasis on opening theory.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-09-polgar-long-term-chess-improvement-plan]] frames Polgar's method around thousands of structured positions and very short solutions so the learner builds independent chess logic.
- The same source treats tactics, miniature games, middlegames, and endgames as a long-term curriculum rather than isolated exercises.
- It also attributes to this branch a strong de-emphasis on opening memorization below master level and a preference for physical-board work over shallow speed-solving.
