# Overview

Alok Kanojia enters this vault as a translator between psychology, motivation, and introspective practice. In the current source base, his role is to explain why effort often collapses not because people lack desire in the abstract, but because identity beliefs distort both emotion and arousal around the task.

# Role In This Vault

Kanojia currently anchors a motivation-and-productivity branch focused on:

- identity as a hidden lever behind boredom and overwhelm
- the distinction between thoughts about self and direct present-moment experience
- loosening rigid identity beliefs to improve effort access
- blending practical psychology with meditative self-observation
- debunking productivity myths that reward passive consumption, divided attention, exhaustion theater, and self-first extraction
- reframing conscientiousness as partially trainable through repeated costly prosocial action
- treating procrastination as identification with the mind's bargaining loop rather than as simple lack of discipline
