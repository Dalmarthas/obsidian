# Overview

Erin McGoff enters this vault as a practical career communicator focused on workplace confidence, hiring, quitting, raises, and emotionally loaded professional conversations. In the current source base, her contribution is less about abstract motivation and more about translating common workplace anxiety into usable framing rules, scripts, and decision criteria.

# Role In This Vault

- She strengthens the career branch where the vault already had interview storytelling and broader career-design material, but less concentrated guidance on the emotional pressure of everyday work conversations.
- She adds a more explicit `issue behind the issue` lens for workplace questions, which helps translate surface prompts into hidden evaluation criteria.
- She also adds a confidence model centered on internal voice, not just external polish:
  - supportive but honest self-talk
  - self-awareness over image management
  - practical framing for weakness questions, raises, quitting, and passive aggression
  - manager-side empathy when making requests inside constrained organizations

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source adds the `10/90 rule` for weakness questions, emphasizing that interviewers care more about self-awareness and improvement process than about polished flawlessness.
- It also adds a useful work-confidence substrate: real confidence is built partly by challenging unearned self-criticism and becoming less brutal in private self-conversation.
- The same source contributes a two-level career-conversation model:
  - answer the explicit question
  - but design the answer for the hidden concern underneath it
- It also expands the vault's workplace-interaction branch through pragmatic scripts for passive aggression, raise requests, and graceful quitting.
