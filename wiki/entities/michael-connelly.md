# Overview

Michael Connelly enters this vault as a crime-fiction craft teacher focused on vivid specificity, character-first storytelling, Los Angeles as a living story engine, daily rewriting, compressed dialogue, and the moral cost of confronting darkness.

# Role In This Vault

- He adds a crime-fiction branch that complements Dan Brown's suspense engineering and David Baldacci's professional thriller process.
- He gives the vault a stronger concept of telling details: one precise detail that reveals character or place without slowing the page.
- He deepens the fiction-writing branch by connecting setting, research, conflict, and procedural structure back to character.
- He sharpens the adaptation branch by contrasting novelistic interiority with television's need to externalize character.

# Evidence In This Vault

- [[wiki/sources/2026-04-23-how-to-write-vividly-well-michael-connelly]] presents Connelly's core craft rule as vividness through selective detail rather than descriptive accumulation.
- The source describes his morning rewrite loop and full-manuscript paper pass as the process by which weak details, excess dialogue, and under-seeded payoffs are improved.
- It frames Harry Bosch, Mickey Haller, and other characters as different lenses on Los Angeles, power, bureaucracy, age, outsiderhood, and moral pressure.
- It treats murder investigation as a way to pass through social layers and ask what darkness does to people who repeatedly confront it.
