# Overview

Daniel Goleman enters this vault as the main figure behind the emotional-intelligence branch, especially where self-mastery, empathy, rapport, and leadership effectiveness intersect. In the current source base, his role is not generic pop psychology. It is a structured argument that emotional and social skill often outperform raw cognitive advantage once basic intelligence is already high.

# Role In This Vault

- He gives the leadership branch a clearer vocabulary for self-awareness, self-regulation, and social intelligence.
- He links interpersonal effectiveness to neuroscience and trainability rather than treating it as innate charm.
- He adds a competence-modeling lens for selection and development that complements the vault's existing performance and leadership branches.

# Evidence In This Vault

- The current source argues that EQ often matters more than IQ for star performance and leadership effectiveness.
- It frames emotional hijack as a real performance problem rather than as a moral flaw alone.
- It treats rapport, empathy, and positive emotional contagion as operational advantages in teams and organizations.
- It also argues that these capacities can be strengthened over time through deliberate practice rather than treated as fixed traits.
